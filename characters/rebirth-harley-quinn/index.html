Outfit[]

“
She's callin' the shots now, puddin'.
”
— In-Game Description

Rebirth Harley Quinn is a DC Series Outfit in Fortnite that can be purchased in the Item Shop for 1,200 V-Bucks. Rebirth Harley Quinn was first released in Chapter 2: Season 7 and is part of the ...And Red All Over Set.

Rebirth Harley Quinn can also be obtained by redeeming a code found in a copy of Batman/Fortnite: Zero Point Issue 1 for $4.99.

Why are there shops labelled as 'parts'?
For varying reasons, Epic Games may decide to add or remove items from the Item Shop before the usual rotation occurs. The Fortnite Wiki treats each change to the Item Shop within a single day as a separate 'part' that makes up the day's Item Shop History.
Why are there two different numbers for Rebirth Harley Quinn's appearances?
Rebirth Harley Quinn has, on at least one occasion, appeared in more than one shop in a single day. This has resulted in its count for appearances in the Item Shop being higher than the amount of days it has appeared in it. Both counts are listed by the Fortnite Wiki in order to be as accurate as possible, unlike other sources.

Harley Quinn is a character from Gotham City. The former girlfriend and sidekick to The Joker; a criminal psychologist turned flamboyant criminal menace. While her intentions are typically good, her methods often portray her insanity; Quinn has acted as both ally and enemy to Batman.

Batman/Fortnite: Zero Point Issue 1[]

She fights Batman before getting into the island, after saying "They" already went through. She then jumps into the Crack in the sky and enters The Loop.

Batman/Fortnite: Zero Point Issue 2[]

Batman and Catwoman then finds her in Steamy Stacks eliminating groups of combatants; they then strike at her and after a bit of fight, she headbutts Batman into The Storm resulting his temporary death.

Batman/Fortnite: Zero Point Issue 6[]

Batman and Catwoman return to The Loop to find Harley Quinn, to use her to hold the panels of The Bridge. They find her fighting but she doesn't want to return to Gotham, and would rather stay in the island instead. Batman knocks her out and carry her back to The Bridge. They open the panels and Harley wakes up, running back to the Island.
