Why are there shops labelled as 'parts'?
For varying reasons, Epic Games may decide to add or remove items from the Item Shop before the usual rotation occurs. The Fortnite Wiki treats each change to the Item Shop within a single day as a separate 'part' that makes up the day's Item Shop History.
Why are there two different numbers for Snoop Dogg's appearances?
Snoop Dogg has, on at least one occasion, appeared in more than one shop in a single day. This has resulted in its count for appearances in the Item Shop being higher than the amount of days it has appeared in it. Both counts are listed by the Fortnite Wiki in order to be as accurate as possible, unlike other sources.

Stink Bomb
Behavior

Behavior[]

Snoop Dogg will attack the player if he notices them undisguised. Though if disguised, he can be talked to for additional dialogue.

Shortly after getting knocked down, he will Self-Revive and join the team of the player who defeated him. Snoop Dogg will replenish all of his health and act as a Supply Specialist Character, providing Double G Bombs and Ammunition to allies.

Besides this, he will grant the team he is following access to his GHOST Chests and neutrality from his henchmen, Sentry Turrets and Sentry Cameras.

Snoop Dogg, when recruited, may also request a High Five or Hug from the player he is following. If ignored, he will cancel the offer and turn away.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Snoop Dogg's Boss music is Who Am I (What's My Name). Though its the Official Clean Version that heavily revises the lyrics to be much less profanic.
    • At 4 minutes long, Snoop Dogg's Boss music has the second longest length of playtime of all the Bosses in Battle Royale. It is only beaten by Eminem.
  • Snoop Dogg's revive stinger is a portion of The Next Episode by Dr. Dre and Snoop Dogg.
  • Whenever the player performs an Emote as a follower, Snoop Dogg will choose between performing The Dogg and Snoop's Walk, rather than just performing Dance Moves like other Bosses.
    • Though Snoop Dogg uses the Y2K Snoop Dogg Style, performing The Dogg reverts him to his default.
  • Snoop Dogg's use of Stink Bombs is likely a reference to his open usage of marijuana.
  • Snoop Dogg, along with Dynamo TNTina and Meowdas are the first Bosses that can be recruited by the player.
    • The feature seems to be recycled from the Latverian Guards due to the Emote requests, now fully fleshed out.
    • Because of this, it is impossible to gain the Who's the Boss? Elimination Accolade when defeating them as they will immediately join the player's team after getting knocked.
  • If the player dismisses Snoop Dogg, he will be eliminated instantly, as opposed to returning to his starting location like other Hireable Characters.
  • Along with other Remix bosses, Snoop Dogg will not drop his items if eliminated by The Storm.
  • If the player that knocked Snoop Dogg is eliminated before he Self-Revives, he will remain knocked and can be Revived manually by another player. However the popup that indicates the option will have no text.
