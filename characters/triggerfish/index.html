Outfit[]

“
Stick it to 'em.
”
— In-Game Description

Triggerfish is a Rare Outfit in Fortnite that can be purchased in the Item Shop for 1,200 V-Bucks, with Kebcio's Locker Bundle for 1,700 V-Bucks, with Giova's Locker Bundle for 2,200 V-Bucks or with the Triggerfish Bundle for 1,500 V-Bucks. Triggerfish was first released in Chapter 2: Season 1 and is part of the Fish Food Set.

Why are there shops labelled as 'parts'?
For varying reasons, Epic Games may decide to add or remove items from the Item Shop before the usual rotation occurs. The Fortnite Wiki treats each change to the Item Shop within a single day as a separate 'part' that makes up the day's Item Shop History.
Why are there two different numbers for Triggerfish's appearances?
Triggerfish has, on at least one occasion, appeared in more than one shop in a single day. This has resulted in its count for appearances in the Item Shop being higher than the amount of days it has appeared in it. Both counts are listed by the Fortnite Wiki in order to be as accurate as possible, unlike other sources.

Triggerfish is an Atlantean fish, O.X.R. recruit, member of the Explorers and a former member of War Effort. He is a war trained, combat ready fish.

Timeline

Timeline[]

Chapter 2: Season 5[]

Triggerfish appeared as a Character on the island with a post-traumatic stress disorder caused by a sushi chef.

Chapter 2: Season 6[]

In the cinematic trailer, Triggerfish is seen having fun alongside Master Chief in The Loop.

Chapter 2: Season 8[]

In the cinematic trailer, him and members of War Effort can be seen exploring the ruins of The Mothership before encountering a Corruption Cube. After witnessing the death of Headhunter, Triggerfish is caught in Sideways Anomaly and is forced to fight horde of Cube Monsters.

Chapter 3: Season 2[]

In Fortnite X Marvel: Zero War, Triggerfish appeared in front of The Scientist with a crucial intel on the Imagined Order's army forces. Triggerfish gave the intel to the Scientist before fainting in his arms.

Chapter 4: Season 3[]

In the cinematic trailer, him and members of The Explorers can be seen exploring the ruins of Rumble Ruins. Triggerfish pickup the Cybertron Cannon and unknowingly summons Optimus Prime. His confusion made him accidentally fire the Cybertron Cannon, causing Rumbles Ruins to collapse.

Chapter 5: Season 4[]

During the Absolute Doom Event, Triggerfish got stomped on by titan sized Doom, eliminating him from the fight.

Chapter 6: Season 4[]

As a regular Fishstick, when he is inspired by Lt. Ripp Slade's propaganda, Triggerfish enlisted in the O.X.R. to purge Oninoshima from the Swarm, an army of Onimorphs born from the Dark Presence's severed tentacle after the Super Showdown. As the Swarm overwhelmed the island, Triggerfish was encased in a goo pod until the Looper break him free.
