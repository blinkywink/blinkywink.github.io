Dino Megazord is an Epic Outfit in Fortnite that could have been obtained as a reward from the Megazord Quests. Dino Megazord was released in Chapter 6: Season 4 and is part of the Mighty Morphin Set.

Dino Megazord's Black & Gold style could also be obtained early by completing the Bonus Rewards ahead of his September 18th release.

Activation

Activation[]

During a match, after the first Storm circle has formed, Rita Repulsa's laugh will be heard before the five Zords combine in the sky to form the Megazord above a predetermined spot on the island. The Megazord's location will be marked on the map before it lands. Once spawned, a hologram of Zordon will appear in front of the Megazord, allowing the player to interact with him to pilot it.

While the Megazord is active, a large glowing icon will mark its location on the map.

Piloting the Megazord[]

Once a player interacts with Zordon, they and their squad will be warped into the cockpit of the Megazord. Any missing members (or the other four seats if the player is solo) will be replaced by one of five Power Rangers (Red, Blue, Pink, Yellow, or Black) and will control the co-pilot abilities on their own, targetting enemies nearby.

The Megazord pilots and Power Rangers are then tasked by Zordon with defeating Rita's Monster, a gigantic Queen that cannot be damaged by players not piloting the Megazord.

While piloting the Megazord, the controlling team's loadouts prior to entering will be replaced by four new main abilities (two for the other players in the team), which they could use to defeat Rita's Monster and fend off other players:

Captain's Abilities[]

  • Shoulder Cannons
    • Charges two beams and fires them after a moment. Dealing 45 damage per beam in an AoE to players, 200 damage per beam in an AoE to The Swarm, and 800 damage per beam to Rita's Monster.
  • Power Slash
    • Swings the Megazord's Power Sword in close range. Dealing 80 damage to players, 2,000 damage to The Swarm, and 6,000 damage to Rita's Monster. This can be done twice per cycle and will stagger Rita's Monster if it hits.
  • Megazord Stomp
    • Stomps the ground with the Megazord's right foot. Dealing 70 damage to players, 1,000 damage to The Swarm, and 3,000 damage to Rita's Monster if caught nearby.
  • Area Scan
    • Scans a wide area around the Megazord, pinging players caught in its radius for 7 seconds. This ability has a cooldown of 13 seconds.

Co-Pilot Abilities[]

  • Rocket
    • Fires a single rocket wherever it is aimed. Dealing 35 damage in an AoE to players, 200 damage in an AoE to The Swarm, and 1,000 damage to Rita's Monster. This ability has fire rate of 1.1, a max magazine size of 4 and refills a rocket every 2.22 seconds.
  • Seeking Rocket
    • Fires a single rocket that can be controlled, forcing the co-pilot to its POV while guiding its movement until it detonates. Dealing 75 damage in an AoE to players, 400 damage in an AoE to The Swarm, and 2,000 damage to Rita's Monster. This ability has a cooldown of 15 seconds.
  • The Co-Pilots may also leave the Megazord if they wish, being replaced by a support Power Ranger if they exit and being rifted above with their previous loadout.

The Megazord will also damage anything that get caught in its path. Dealing 50 damage to players, and 150 damage to The Swarm if trampled on.

During the piloting team's time controlling the Megazord, their Health would slowly deplete until fully drained, with the amount of depletion increasing after a significant period of time has passed. This was a measure to ensure that the piloting players don't remain in the Megazord for too long.

However once Rita's Monster's health is depleted to 200 Health, it will become stunned and vulnurable to one final Finishing Strike only initiated by the captain. Charging up the Megazord's Power Sword for a powerful vertical slash that deals 99,999 critical damage to fully eliminate Rita's Monster while also regenerating the Megazord's health. Prolonging the amount of time it can remain active.

Once The Megazord is defeated, it will point its Power Sword into the air while beaming away, transporting it out of the Island. The players who had piloted it would be rifted back into the sky with full Health, Shields, and all the items they had gathered before being transported into the cockpit.

Battling the Megazord[]

Players in the vicinity are ordered by Rita Repulsa to destroy the Megazord alongside her Monster with the promise of rewards, sending down Supply Comets to grant high-tier loot to players nearby. As it gets attacked by Rita's Monster, parts of the Megazord's exterior will be corroded and glow purple, which will grant critical damage should players attack those spots, once a glowing weakspot takes enough damage it will disappear until it is hit again by Rita's Monster.

Dealing 2,000 damage to the Megazord will grant a player Rita Repulsa's Boon, and the player that dealt the most damage until the Megazord is defeated will reward them with the Megazord's Medallion.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Megazord's codename is ToyFoxGameplay.
    • Megazord's Weapons use the WID_ToyFoxDefaultWeapon codename.
  • The Megazord's combat music is Go Go Power Rangers, the Mighty Morphin Power Rangers' theme song.
  • Similar to Doctor Doom and Godzilla, the Megazord is colossal in comparison to all other entities in Battle Royale.
    • Like Godzilla, the Megazord uses a unique model that is not an Outfit under the four player model types.
    • The Megazord's model features a modified version of the Tank Cannons Back Bling showcasing its deployed design. It is also properly attached to the Megazord's model.
  • The Megazord's Finishing Strike damage is completely scripted. It has no actual damage values towards Rita's Monster in the game's code.
    • The Finishing Strike also has damage values for entities besides Rita's Monster. Dealing 150 damage to players and 1,000 damage to The Swarm.
  • Peter Griffin's eyes will increase in size while in the entering cutscene.

The Dino Megazord is a titan from the Power Rangers' reality.

It is a minor protagonist in Chapter 6: Season 4 and one of the main protagonists in Zero Hour.

History

History[]

Chapter 6: Season 4[]

Following the invasion of the Swarm on Oninoshima, Zordon sends the five Mighty Morphin' Power Rangers to help. Each of the rangers traversed the island and did what they could against the Onimorphs, all to buy time for the Zords to come together so they could board it and confront Rita Repulsa's own giant Queen.

The Megazord successfully defeats Rita's monster before taking care of the other Queens on the island while O.X.R drove the rest of the swarm away themselves, allowing Oninoshima to rebuild itself.

Chapter 6: Mini Season 2[]

Zero Hour[]

The Dino Megazord as well as Tommy and numerous others were rifted back onto Oninoshima to fight the Dark Presence. As soon as Jones gives the call, the titans all charge at the Dark Presence, the Megazord equipping it's power sword and slashing at the demon and it's swarmers.

Later on in the fight, the Megazord perches on a hill near the Dark Presence where it attempts to attack it with it's shoulder cannons, only to be discarded by the demon end pinned down for the remainder of the fight, only merely watching.
