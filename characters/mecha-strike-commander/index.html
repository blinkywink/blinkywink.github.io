Mecha Strike Commander is an Crew Series Outfit in Fortnite that could have been obtained by becoming a member of the Fortnite Crew during June 2022, as part of the June 2022 Fortnite Crew Pack. Mecha Strike Commander was released in Chapter 3: Season 2 and is part of the Collision Set.

Mecha Strike Commander, formerly known as Mecha Team Leader is a giant mech piloted by The Paradigm and the Mecha Weapons Team against the Imagined Order forces. It was recommissioned by The Seven to stop The Collider in Chapter 3: Season 2.

Overview

Overview[]

Mecha Strike Commander was created when The Seven decided to recover Paradigm's Mech on the Ice Moon. The Foundation, The Scientist, Iron Man, and The Paradigm repaired and improved it while another team managed to recover the Zero Shard, in order to power it. This version is far more powerful than its original counterpart, having many more weapons such as lasers and miniguns as well as a regeneration system. Additionally, unlike its original counterpart, the weapons seem to have infinite uses. In return, many more pilots are needed to manage the weapons, the different systems of the Mecha as well as its integrity, therefore a total of nine pilots are needed. In the Zero Hour finale event it returned to fight the Dark Presence.

History

History[]

Chapter 3: Season 2[]

  • May 28th 2022: Mecha Team Leader has received a new paint job and its right arm is replaced with a new one.
  • May 31st, 2022: Repairs on the Mecha are finished.

Collision[]

A team of pilots, including The Looper are called upon by The Seven to operate the Mecha, with The Paradigm being the pilot. The five of them control the Mecha in the ultimate battle against Doctor Slone and the Imagined Order to prevent the use of The Collider, and to save Reality Zero from decimation. The Mecha leaves from the Ice Moon outside of the Island and, after flying through space past asteroids and a destroyed Abductor, touches down at Synapse Station. From there, the Mecha begins a steady path towards The Collider, destroying Titan Tanks, shields surrounding IO Outposts, and IO Drills. The Fortress charges up a laser strike and attacks the Mecha, but is able to tank the attack. The Paradigm orders a power diversion to the "Heartbreaker" attack, and unleashes it upon The Fortress, destroying it. The Mecha continues its path towards The Collider, now destroying incoming missiles in addition to the IO paraphernalia before, until an entourage of missiles launched from a fleet of IO Airships nearly takes down the Mecha.

After recovering its armor due to the efforts of Peely, it begins to destroy the airships with its Light Sword and incoming B.R.U.T.E.s with the gunners' weaponry, all while steadily nearing The Collider. Finally, after eliminating all of the Imagined Order ground forces and reaching The Collider, the Mecha lifts the sword, about to strike, when a sudden seismic shift in the ground around it sends it crashing into a newly opened chasm below, where Doctor Slone lays waiting. As the Mecha lay in rubble, The Paradigm begins to try and get it charged up again, while her other operators proceed on foot to eliminate IO forces. Gradually, enough power becomes restored where The Paradigm can operate it by herself. The Mecha raises its left fist above an unsuspecting Doctor Slone, and while she sits in her tank, the Mecha crushes Slone and her tank, plummeting them into the debris and rubble below. The Mecha then gathers its other operators onto said hand to raise them enough to destroy the crystals meant to detonate The Zero Point. Once they are destroyed, the Mecha cups the other operators in both hands, shielding them from The Collider exploding apart, and the event ends.

Chapter 6: Mini Season 2[]

Zero Hour[]

Jones summons an alternate reality version of the Mecha Strike Commander (referred to as Mecha Team Leader for the duration of the fight) with an unknown pilot/pilots to fight against the Dark Presence alongside many other kaijus—including his nemesis, the Devourer. After Mecha Team Leader is violently blasted by the Dark Presence in an intense part later in the fight, Jones gets the risky idea to summon a Havoc Cube from a separate reality for help. After a brief altercation between the two, the Dark Presence uses Kong as a body shield before launching him at the cube, causing it to go haywire and nearly explode. An injured Mecha Team Leader returns and punts it into the Dark Presence, killing him.
