Joni the Red is a Crew Series Outfit in Fortnite that could have been obtained by becoming a member of the Fortnite Crew during December 2022, as part of the December 2022 Fortnite Crew Pack. Joni the Red was released in Chapter 3: Season 4 and is part of the Let's Hunt Vampires Set.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Joni the Red is named after Jonathan Harker - character from Bram Stoker's novel Dracula.
  • In Update v23.10, Joni the Red received a new style, Blue-Blood.
  • Joni's pronouns in English are She/Her/Hers but vary depending on the language spoken, with some languages avoiding using pronouns altogether.
  • Joni the Red is the first Fortnite Crew Outfit to be remixed.
  • Joni the Red has a male counterpart named Minato Hakaru.
  • Joni the Red's rarity was updated from Epic to Crew Series in Update v34.00.

Joni is a young vampire hunter who leads a high school club: The High Stakes Club. On various monster hunts, she has eliminated lowly vampires, snapshots of them, and a high profile vampire. She does so with her peers, and sometimes solo.

She is a minor protagonist in Fortnite, serving as a minor protagonist in Chapter 4: Season 1, Chapter 4: Season 4, and Chapter 6: Season 1.

Personality

Personality[]

Joni is an intense leader who shows harshness in her hunting. She is driven by her unyielding loyalty to her friends. If provoked, Joni's vengeance towards her enemies makes her think with her heart instead of her head.

Timeline

Timeline[]

Unknown Period of Time[]

Joni and her then best friend Minato Hakaru were attacked by a monster.[2] Following the attack, Joni and Minato both became harsher in their hunting, taking it as a lesson learned.[3] As Minato's hunting style became increasingly merciless, Joni lost trust in her best friend,[4] eventually leaving him behind for Helsie and shortly after Lucien West.[5] Joni and her new best friends become chosen family, bonding over their shared interests in Starfang and monster hunting.

Chapter 4: Season 1[]

Joni and her club are pulled from Reality: 783 to Asteria due to The Paradigm initiating Zero Fushion. After arriving, Joni leaves for a hunt and eventually tracks down three Driftwalker snapshots at Faulty Splits. She eliminates them with ease before residing there.[6]

Joni and Helsie are concerned with Lucien's disappearance after he left for a hunt and hadn't returned.[7] Joni then says they have to find him.[8] After his return, Joni tells Helsie to give him space.[9]

Following their reunion,[10] Joni attends a Starfang concert with her friends.[11] Unbeknownst to Joni, Countess Daraku writes High Stakes Club about Joni and her club, telling how she will kill them underneath a red sky.

Chapter 4: Season 4[]

Joni and her club get ready for another Starfang concert, renaming herself as Joni the Scarlet. At some point near the eclipse, Countess Daraku reveals herself as a vampire to the club, completely destroying Joni's love for her.[12] After the reveal, Joni decides to get her revenge and avenge the countless fans Daraku has fed on.[13] She does so by becoming Victoria Saint's mentee[14] and building the Wood Stake Shotgun alongside the High Stakes' Shotgun.

Shimmerdusk warns against keeping friends in monster hunting,[15] however Victoria, Joni, and her club decide to go against her beliefs. The High Stakes Club - now all mentees of Victoria[16] - help train The Looper to assist in hunting The Countess. Together, Joni and her allies kill her. Following their victory, she thanks The Looper for their efforts.[17]

Chapter 6: Season 1[]

Joni and her club arrive back to their home: Reality: 783. Later, Joni and her club reunite with Minato Hakaru, the Club's last member and solo hunter. For some reason, Helsie noted doesn't fully trust him anymore.[4] She also tells The Looper that Joni once told her he is way harsher than Joni was, noting Joni herself was already harsh herself.[5]
