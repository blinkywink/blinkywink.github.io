John "Jonesy" Jones, also known as Vengeance Jones, The Legend or simply Jones is a former high ranking member of the Imagined Order who worked under the command of Doctor Slone. He betrayed IO during the Zero Crisis in order to save Reality Zero. After having been rescued from prison by The Foundation during The End event, he joined The Seven. He is the original man behind all of Jonesy snapshots.

He is the main protagonist of Fortnite: Battle Royale, serving as the overarching protagonist of Chapter 1, the secondary protagonist of Chapter 2, one of the two main protagonists (alongside The Foundation) of Chapter 3 and Chapter 7, the main protagonist of Fortnite OG, and one of the three main protagonists of Chapter 5 and Chapter 6 (alongside Hope and The Looper).

Personality

Personality[]

Jones was originally an adventurous man who could easily adapt himself into all kinds of unknown places. He learned to speak 342,000 languages from all the realities he explored. He would try his best to fit in and befriend everyone he comes across.

Jones comes from an "Unstable World" reality where he needs to fight to live. He married an unnamed woman and had children with her. The IO offers him a job to join IO Island Management Team and Jones' family can get protection from the hostile world as long as he can work for them. After becoming an IO agent and gaining ageless immortality, he thought he had all the time to fix any mistake. But after his wife and children died of old age, he began to see his immortality as a curse and to be consummed from within by doubt and sadness, which he hides behind a mask of an unserious and joking person, except when the situation was really serious.[5]

However, despite everything, Jones maintained a heroic phase for a time and managed to remain himself, but the disappearance of The Seven along with the destruction of Reality Zero followed by Peely's kidnapping drastically changed him over time, creating a much more stern, serious, and colder self-image and adopting a philosophy of vengeance.[6] He also became arrogant after The Society's and The Pantheon's defeats although it didn't last long.[7] While this whole personality phase was reversed during the War against Doom,[8] he returned to his vengeance philosophy soon after, feeling his old self didn't fit anymore.[9] However, Jones still let Peely and The Looper see his old personality, sometimes.

Over time spent with Hope, Jones began to develop a protective father figure[10] personality towards her,[11] with Hope and Kendo's growing relationship making him very uncomfortable, going hard on Kendo as a result.[12]

Appearance

Appearance[]

Jones is a young looking man with blonde hair and blue eyes. As an IO agent, he wears a dark blue suit with a blue/red tie. As Jones Unchained, he has a white tank top and prison overalls. He later grows a beard as Vengeance Jones and sports a beige coat, a black sweater, a red scarf and sunglasses. Exiting his "Vengeance" way, Jones intended to return to being a true hero, and became Captain Jones, with a suit similar to Captain America's, but adapted in his style, and the shield, donated by Captain America himself. However, this change didn't last and he returned to his vengeance self, feeling better with it although he clarified that "vengeance" was not literal, but a state of mind.

Timeline

Timeline[]

Before Chapter 1[]

Jones is recruited by Doctor Slone into the Imagined Order, and becomes a member of the Island Management Team. He is tasked with jumping into The Zero Point. It creates one of the very first Snapshots of him, Bunker Jonesy, along with numerous others throughout his various trips into the Loop. He is sent through the Zero Point to many realities in The Omniverse to bring order to them, kidnapping people and putting them in The Loop so the IO can study them. He was also very knowledgeable about The Seven. As years past, Jones has children and a family that lived in Jones' very dangerous home Reality, under protection by the IO. As they get older, Jones wants to retire so he can spend time with his grandchildren he barely knows. Doctor Slone reminds that he became indispensable in the IO and if he leaves, his family won't be under protection anymore, forcing Jones to work many more years for descendants he never knew.

Season 8[]

After Agent Jones, sent for a mission in The Loop, first encountered Peely at Lucky Landing's Bao Bros, the two became friend and started hanging out together. After some time, they found themselve trapped in the bunker to escape the Volcano eruption. However, they found themselves without food, and Jones had no choice but to devour Peely to survive.

Season 9[]

After being trapped for decades, if not centuries, Jones, who turned into Bunker Jonesy and Peely, who became a smoothie were freed by Sentinel, Vega and Rox and discovered Neo Tilted. However, during their imprisonment in the Bunker, the act of devouring Peely allowed Bunker Jonesy to see visions of both the past and the future. In one of these visions, he saw a fight between a Kaiju and a Mecha that would happen in the near future and tried to warn the inhabitants of The Island. At some point, Jones would later escape The Loop, leaving behind the Bunker Jonesy snapshot behind.

Chapter 2: Season 2[]

Jones walks through The Bridge during The Device, and order his co-workers to change The Storm. While talking, The Looper gets teleported into his office, and listens to him talk. Jones opens the door and sees The Looper, asking them if they can hear him.

Chapter 2: Season 4[]

Jones is briefly seen at the end of the Devourer of Worlds Event. At first lying on the floor in an unconscious state, he then quickly wakes up as the building crumbles around him.

After the battle against Galactus finishes, the Imagined Order is in a state of panic. John Jones is lying on the ground as the phone on his table rings. He answers the phone, and Doctor Slone, orders him that he needs to reseal the bridge and that the last thing they need is someone escaping The Loop. Just as he walks out of his office, grabbing his Field Pack, Slone warns him about The Seven, but he leaves the office without hearing her. He enters The Bridge and locates the best Hunters across all realties. Through his Reality Logs, he brings several Hunters to The Island so they can create chaos while IO tries to fix The Zero Point. While doing this, he creates Pure Portals which overloads The Zero Point more.

Chapter 2: Season 6[]

With the Zero Point becoming more and more unstable, he turns to The Seven and tries to get access to "Restricted File 8752," all files related to the Seven. After multiple failed attempts, he goes onto the island and through a series of events, throws the Portal Device into the Zero Point, gets the help of The Foundation and they reseal the Zero Point. However, he is looped as a result and The Foundation seals himself into The Spire.

Jones was then given a gun to help hold off the IO Guards. After The Foundation rewires part of The Bridge, Jones then proceeds to activate the flipping of the Island, and at one point drops the gun in The Zero Point.

Jones officially joins The Seven as an ally, but is still disliked by The Scientist and the other members. He stays in the Seven Outposts with Seven Sentry while working on Geno, meanwhile his Snapshots started their own village society as The Joneses.

He instructs The Looper on giving intel from the IO Airships and instructing Peely how to drive. He is still working on Geno.

Hope, Jones, and their allies brace themselves for Doom's imminent arrival, setting up rift beacons near the central lake with the plan to send him back to his home reality. Peelverine is left in charge of activating the beacons, but he quickly delegates the task to The Looper. As The Looper activates the beacons one by one, they all begin to aim towards Doom's Arcane Forge.

As the final beacon fires at the forge, Hope active the rift but Doom's glowing green head emerges from the Arcane Forge, that was shielded, unleashing a powerful energy burst that engulfs the surrounding area with a wall of debris. Emerging as a giant, Doom swiftly annihilates almost everyone who opposes him in the area. After Doom flickered War Machine aside, Jones hurls his shield at Doom, but it has no effect and Doom knock him to the ground. With Doom poised to finish him off, Hope leaps into action, she grabs Jones's shield and shields them both as the King of Latveria launches energy blasts at them. In a bold move, Doom grabs Hope, bringing her face-to-face with him. She stabs him in the eye with the shield still charged with Pandora's magic, leaving Doom with a scar over his eye which enabled a weak point suppressing his physical immunity. However Hope injured her own arm in the process. Peelverine, Gwenpool, War Machine, Iron Man and Iron Spider—supported by an army of Loopers—charge in, engaging Doom in an epic battle at the central lake. Ultimately, the heroes and Loopers prevail, managing to weaken Doom enough to activate the final rift beacon, stripping him of his Pandora's Box powers and sending him back to his home reality. During the fight against Doom, three rift beacons were launched aside, landing at Grand Glacier, Brawler's Battleground and Restored Reels, and throughout the next few days, pumpkins and vines would begin to appear as Ultima Carver's Halloween Reality begins to merge with the Island. During this chaos, Hope spray paints the Apollo Island on the screen of Restored Reels, while Stark's Rift Beacon residing there began to spark opening a rift and sending everyone onto the Apollo Island.

Chapter 5: Season 5[]

Hope would disband The Underground and leave the Helios Island after realizing Pandora's Box was just the beginning, with Jones on her side. She tried to contact Valeria and convince her to meet up by bringing up some old memories, just to end up being ignored, and put on voice mail over and over. Hope would set up a meeting between The Underground and Valeria hoping her sister would show up. Hope would try calling her sister one last time, to tell Valeria about her strange power to see the future through her art.

Chapter 6: Season 1[]

A shard of The Zero Point arrives onto Oninoshima, alongside strangers from the island of Helios. As a result of the shard's landing, magic has re-emerged, and the abandoned portals are reopened for the demons of old to return across Oninoshima[27] - including Shogun X, the Demon Warriors' leader. Together, they threaten the Island dwellers, but in the process, gain resistance from Hope and Jones, whom have teamed up with the High Stakes Club,[28] alongside the reluctantly reunited forces of the River Guard and the Fox Clan.

Splinters of Possibility[]

Godzilla, a Titan and King of the Monsters, emerges from a Rift Gate in the ocean and destroys the nearby bridge. This worries Hope, The Looper and Nyanja. Nyanja and The Looper work together to discover Monarch's secrets and plant Kaiju detection beacons to monitor the Titan. Meanwhile, Hope gets to work on planting Godzilla Evacuation Shelter signs, and asks The Looper to take down Godzilla once he emerges.[29] Doughberman and Jones relocate to the now-shattered bridge, fighting demons together.

The Spirit Realm[]

With the shattered bridge slowly being repaired, Jones relocates to Hopeful Heights. Later, during the investigation of the Spirit Realm, Hope reveals to The Looper that the comet is a Shard of The Zero Point, a powerful object she has never seen herself but has heard of from Jones.

The Confrontation Event[]

During the Confrontation Event, Jones engages in discussions with Daigo, who questions him about his motivations and whether he is truly capable of resisting the seductive power of the Shard. [30] Later, discussing with Jones again, Daigo challenges him, asking whether he will lock away the power of the Shard or use it for his own ends. But in the end, Daigo choose to not trust Jones and prevent him from getting his hands on the Shard. As the situation escalates, Hope and Jones prepare for the dark times ahead now that the demons have obtained the Shard. After Daigo killed the Shogun X and saved Jade, Jones chooses—for now—to trust The Night Rose.

Chapter 6: Season 2[]

Hope reveals to The Looper that since Jones took her under his wing, she hopes to do the same if she ever meets someone like herself. Meanwhile, The Night Rose officially joins the Demon Hunters and works alongside Jones, searching for information in ancient texts. However, he suspects The Night Rose don't tell them everything. Jones later shares his belief that multiple Zero Point Shards may exist to The Looper. However, for now, the group's focus remains on reclaiming the one currently in the hands of the Demon Warriors.

Chapter 6: Mini Season 1[]

Following the arrival of the Death Star, Luke Skywalker and the rest of the Resistance arrived on Oninoshima to help the residents survive the galactic threat. Most of The Resistance set up their Resistance Base while the Clone Troopers set up their own Outpost Enclave and began working as a unified effort in spite of the Empire's new allies. Luke, determined to enlist new allies, tells Leia that people such as Hope, Jones and The Looper will be good to recruit.

Death Star Sabotage[]

After a successful assault on Star Destroyers around the Death Star, Hope, Jones, The Looper and their resistance allies successfully infiltrate the Death Star. Some disguise themselves as Stormtroopers, while others pose as prisoners, allowing them to slip past Imperial forces undetected. Once inside, they sabotage the tractor beam systems and other critical technologies, significantly reducing the Death Star's threat level—provided their mission succeeds.

Meanwhile, Fishstick attempts to experiment with a Rift-To-Go in space—an untested maneuver. The device caus him to vanish from the group and makes it appear in space, outside. This sabotage effort serves as the core of the resistance's inside operation, while the Blue Squadron maintains communication with Jones and Hope to coordinate their bombing run.

After disabling the Death Star's laser cannons, to allow Blue Leader's Tie Fighter Squadron to do their move, the team is soon confronted by a pair of Stormtroopers. Jones tries to use Jedi mind tricks, but the attempt fails, forcing the group to neutralize the enemies with conventional weapons. This is followed by a trip to the Tie Fighter ships room, with a fight against the Imperial forces. Just as escape seems within reach, a TIE Fighter crashes into the bridge, separating Hope from the rest of the team.

Trapped in what appears to be a dead end, the group faces Emperor Palpatine himself. He effortlessly deflects their attacks, disarms them using the Force, and overwhelms them with Force Lightning. With the team incapacitated and no options remaining, Jones prepares for the worst—until Hope returns just in time. She uses a Rift-To-Go to shield Jones from Palpatine's final strike. Though unsure if it would work, the gambit pays off: Jones, Hope and The Looper escapes moments before the Death Star explodes along with Fishstick and the few surviving members, falling toward the Island. However, the debris rains down on the Island, striking several places as well as the portal at Predator Peak.

Colliding with the Spirit Realm portal, the fragment triggered a powerful energy wave that hits the reality barrier surrounding Oninoshima and sends a reality wave across the Island, hitting Jones, Hope and The Looper.

Chapter 6: Season 3[]

Following the sabotage of the Death Star, a wave of Zero Point energy resulting from the collision of a fragment of the Death Star with the Predator Peak Portal affected a group of people in the Battle Bus, granting them superpowers. Around this time, Superman arrived in Reality: 783 and saved the Battle Bus and its passengers from the imminent crash. In light of Daigo reigniting the threat of the Demon Warriors alongside his new lieutenant, Kor, Hope decided to found a superhero academy, leadings to the creation of the Supernovas.

With the help of Midas, Jones and Hope managed to fund the idea of their academy, and became its first teachers. They invited the Night Rose who is also considered as one of the founding member and later Robin and Superman to be part of the Teaching Staff. For their first students, they invited the Battle Bus group: Sun Tan Specialist (who became Ziggy), Haylee Skye, Synthwave, Lightrider, and Morgan Myst, as well as the cyborg Killswitch — who's also been empowered by the Zero Point energy wave — to form the first hero group, known as The Supernovas. Additional students such as Siena Nyte and Sonia are also studying in the academy, though they possess no apparent powers and don't seem to belong to The Supernovas. For the Third and Final group, The Looper, Kendo, and Jade act as their field agents, investigating, fighting and relaying information to Hope and the group.

The Academy has its upsides and its downside moments with its students. Despite the great teachers they possess, Ziggy barely goes to class, and when he does, he brings snacks, which annoys Jones, but pleases the Night Rose. Haylee Skye skips the line with the excuse that "the beast is hungry", while Jones is trying to figure out if her and the beast get different grades or not. Killswitch usually has over-preparations and his so determined to his work that he asks Jones and other teachers for more homework.

Jones, alongside Hope and The Night Rose are also working on a secret project, The Vault alongside Midas. The goal being to contain The Zero Shard in the Spirit Realm, as well as the other potential shards. However, Jones and the other proved to not trust Midas, and redacted some of their informations to prevent misuses, as proved when The Looper acting secretly for Midas asked them some informations about the Spires.

Chapter 6: Season 4[]

Throughout the Infestation, Jones operated primarily alongside Hope at Supernova Academy, focusing on analysis, strategy, and countermeasures against the growing threat of the Spirit Realm. When The Looper visited the Academy to discuss the severed tentacle recovered from Superman, Jones contributed key intel gathered by the Supernovas. Together with Hope, he revealed that their findings suggested the Tentacle—and by extension The Swarm—had accessed the Island directly from the Spirit Realm without using standard Spirit Realm portals. While their data was incomplete, Jones emphasized the abnormal nature of this incursion and the necessity of locating the access point responsible.

After The Looper later identified and analyzed the Swarm's portal anomaly near Foxy Floodgate, Jones observed the anomaly's transformation into a dark red state. Though he remarked that it was unsettling, he acknowledged that the data it provided was nonetheless useful.

Later, following the apparent defeat and disappearance of the Swarm, Jones remained unconvinced that the threat had truly ended. Alongside Hope, he began preparing for an inevitable confrontation with the Dark Presence. With Onyx Winter placing O.X.R. assets under their command, Jones took on a central role in coordinating defenses and planning a counteroffensive. He supported the general's assessment that the Swarm could never be permanently defeated while their master still existed, echoing warnings from Ripp Slade and reinforcing Doctor Flyesbee's theory that an even greater presence loomed beyond the Swarm.

As plans advanced toward a direct assault on the Spirit Realm, Jones participated in strategic discussions aimed at defining a decisive response to the Dark Presence, culminating in a summit between the Island's leaders and allies. These preparations ultimately led to the development of a broader campaign that would place the Mighty Morphin Power Rangers and the Dino Megazord at the center of the coming conflict.

During the emergence of the mysterious anomaly in northwestern Oninoshima, Jones worked closely with Hope to devise a permanent solution. While Hope dispatched The Looper to investigate the anomaly itself, Jones focused on engineering a countermeasure capable of sealing Spirit Realm breaches. To that end, he enhanced his Portal Device, adapting it specifically to close unstable portals and reinforce the veil between realms.

When the anomaly reached a critical state, Jones and Hope rifted to a nearby control center to deploy their solution. Although they briefly succeeded in sealing the breach, the portal reopened into a far larger rift, allowing the Dark Presence to begin crossing into the Island. As chaos erupted and The Looper was struck by one of the entity's tentacles, Jones alongside Hope started to fight the creature.

When Kang and Kodos abducted Hope and Jones in an attempt to gain the Dark Presence's favor, Jones and Hope overpowered them and seized control of their saucer. During the ensuing confrontation, Jones noticed the a Zero Point Shard embedded within the Dark Presence's chest. Acting on this observation, he alerted Hope, enabling her to use the saucer's tractor beam to extract a fragment of the shard before the vessel was hurled into an inter-reality wormhole.

Chapter 6: Mini Season 2[]

Apocalypse D'Oh[]

After arriving in that unknown world completely twisted by Homer's reckless use of the Shard Remote, Hope, Jones and The Looper began to "clean up the mess", drawing the ire of the residents who are on the side of the changes made by Homer. After trying to talk to Homer for him to give up the Shard, he refused but offered to reconsider if they deliver a new flavor of Squishee from Kwik-E-Mart to him and that the mayor granted him an audience. After the delivery, The Looper went to the Town Hall to "raise concerns", fulfilling their tasks. However, Homer betrayed his word and decided to keep the Shard to continue his mission of "achieving nirvana in Springfield". Hope and Jones decided to keep trying, as they needed to Shard.

Sugar High[]

Back at the Simpson residence, Professor Frink informs Homer about the shard embedded in TV remote, stating it possesses Zero Point energy, which is the most dangerous force in the Omniverse. Frink can study it for fame, science, and, especially, Nobel Prizes. However, Homer tells him to wait as he must make Springfield into a paradise, when Frink asks what he can do more after Homer transformed his robo-caller into a lady-bot, Homer, inspired by a ring-shaped cloud, uses the shard to create raining giant food, consisting of donuts, pizza and pie slices, and fried chicken drumsticks, to feed the whole community of Springfield.

Impressed, Frink told Homer he could keep the Shard, but was disappointed he had nothing to present for the Nobels. So Homer transformed him into a Peelfessor Frink. After some time, however, instead of improving Springfield into a paradise, he is causing chaos here, creating Donut Storms that rain down supersized donuts upon many residents and buildings. Witnessing this, Jones, Hope The Looper began investigating, starting with asking Professor Frink and Ned Flanders about this, both understanding the situation was currently "not good". Right after, Jones noticed dangerous objects inside the Donut Storms.

Frink tells the group to introduce Springfieldieans to more of Homer's handiwork, with The Looper, Jones and Hope showing how dangerous those Donut Storms are to the neighborhood. Discussing with Frink and Flanders, they believe Homer would stop if the town was against him. Hope notes they might do it, as Homer's laziness works in their favor. As they manage to get the all of the resisdents of Springfield against Homer, The Looper collect their angry notes and gives them to Homer after insisting after his initial refusal.

Multiplidiocy[]

Facing with the hatred of the town, Homer decided to get rid of the Zero Shard and blame Lenny, throwing the Shard in the Nuclear Core. However, he found some candy left in a candy bar wrapper, tried to grab it, but fell into the reactor. The reaction caused a nuclear accident, releasing a nuclear gas that created countless Homer clones that began to invade Springfield. Bart and Lisa, discovering the situation started to eliminate clones along with others. This started to forl a giant Homer shaped cloud, as the clones were eliminated.

Trying to get back their father, they used the help of The Looper to get his attention, hoping that the cloud will reform Homer. They used everything, music, art, healthy food without it working. Running out of ideas, they kept calling his name, reforming Homer, but in a giant version. Hope arrived alongside Marge, and noticed the Shard was inside him, which is why he was bigger. Marge notes they have to get it out of him, but Lisa says he might be a fore of good. However, just after that Homer kicks a car, destroys a building and flees.

The Incredible Bulk[]

Following Homer, Hope and Jones, accompanied by Peely, Lisa and Marge, determined that the Shard was in his stomach. When Marge asked if there was a solution, Hope suggested vaporizing her motor cortex to immobilize her, then sending in a strike team to extract the Shard from her lower bowel. Marge then replied that Hope likely doesn't have many friends, but Jones said she does, before making a discreet "no" with his head. Hope says they absolutely needs the Shard to return to Oninoshima and defeat the Dark Presence. Marge tried to convince Hope she could convince Homer, but she ignored her and tried to execute her plan, which failed. Homer let out a huge belch, which removed all of Peely's skin.

Jones decides to try Marge's way, The Looper first helped Marge with her shopping list, before going to see professor Frink following her suggestion. Frink remembered the Rift Device, and told The Looper to recover his "simple" inventions and somehow make them better, to repair the Rift Device. Marge led The Looper to Ned's house to find the Sarcasm Detector, then to Comic Book Guy for the Hamburger Earmuffs. Returning to Marge for help in order to get through to Homer, The Looper is told to gather photos and decorate billboards, before emoting to get Homer's attention. Earning it, Homer finally burps out the Shard.

The Looper then returns to Hope, giving the Shard to Jones while giving hope all the objects they recovered from Frink to which Hope reacts sarcastically that this is exactly what she needed to save Reality, before noting that in any case they need Jones' rift device to work. Frink then intervened, trying to use his deigulator to makes Homer small again, however, the Zero Point energy within him prevent it from working. Hope gets the idea of getting Homer with them to face the Dark Presence, Homer having enough to be giant, accepts.

Zero Hour[]

After repairing the Rift Device, Jones and Hope began to prepare their return, configuring the Device as the embiggened Homer Simpson sleeps in the central square, while The Looper and the others were waiting in the lab. Hope and Jones began contacting various individuals throughout the Omniverse for help, with their primary contacts being Kong, Godzilla, Superman, the Power Rangers, the Mecha Team Leader, and the Resistance. Once the Rift Beacon etablished, Jones uses them to broadcast a distress message in those realities and rally a large number of heroes to Oninoshima to help fight the Dark Presence.

With the plans now set into motion, Jones uses the Rift Device to rift them out of Springfield, but due to improper calibration, they find themselves in a handful of other realities instead. These include a Hatsune Miku concert, an encounter with Playboi Carti on a basketball court, a visit into a reality where inanimate objects had become sentient, and back to Springfield. They eventually find themselves back onto Oninoshima, which had become heavily corrupted by the Dark Presence's influence. A large number of rifts appear behind them, and the innumerable bands of heroes rendezvous with Oninoshima's residents before following Jones into battle, leading the forces against the demon.

When the situation was desperate, Jones came up with a crazy idea, asking Hope to trust him and opened a new Rift, duel Revin the Cube emerged. Hope noted that it wasn't even crazy but insane, to which Jones agreed. As Jones had predicted, the Dark Presence and the Cube fought each other, Revin hitting the Dark Presence hard enough to stun him allowing all forces to attack. However this didn't last long, as Dark Presence used Kong as a shield before throwing it on the Cube, severely damaging it, to the point that it would explode.

However, Mecha Team Leader returned, Jones calling his name in joy, and threw the exploding Cube on Dark Presence, which eliminated the demon once and for all, freeing the Zero Point Shard and rifting back all allies to their worlds. After the battle, Jones, Hope, The Looper and Midas left Oninoshima and Reality: 783 with the Shard, going to a new world, Reality: 266 on the Golden Coast. Handing the shard to InnoLoop.

Chapter Seven: Season 1[]

The morning after a very boozy day and evening with Peely, Meowscles, and a new friend, Human Bill, whom they freed from Classified Canyon Jones woke up with one less tooth, and after recalling last day's events, he witnessed the Dark Voyager's Spaceship arriving on the Golden Coast and destroying the Battle Bus. Later, he sent a distress signal, which The Order, who investigated Reality: 783, was able to pick up the signal.

Lore Trivia[]

  • Jones' current personality might have been taken from The Foundation, who was Jones' partner for a time.
  • Beside Peely, The Looper is the only remaining friend of Jones from Reality Zero and the only one he can fully trust.
    • Jones seems to have hidden informations to Hope about his common past with The Looper in Reality Zero, with Hope herself unaware that The Looper already knew about The Zero Point.
    • All of Jones's other friends and companions are either dead or missing. Most of The Seven are Chromed, The Imagined was discorporated, The Paradigm sacrificed herself, and AMIE is missing after attempting to rescue The Scientist from his fate.
  • In Fortnite X Marvel: Zero War, Jones and Shuri from Reality: 616 shared a romance, however it never came to fruition due to Jones' loyalty to his late wife and family despite them being deceased since centuries, ghosting Shuri and her disguised invitation.
    • After Spider-Man explained her the situation, Shuri understood and stood by Jones's decision, thinking there is nothing more important than honoring the memory of loved ones who've passed
    • Fortnite X Marvel: Zero War Issue 5 left an opening for both characters about a possible future relationship.
  • Jones and The Looper share many similarities.
    • Both were technically introduced during The Device Event.
    • Both share a similar time as main protagonists/overarching protagonists, being the highest of all Fortnite.
    • Both were Loopers in Reality Zero, as well as members of the Imagined Order at some point, before joining The Seven.
    • Both lived several centuries.
    • Both took a mentee under their wing, being respectively Hope and Morgan Myst.
  • During Fortnite X Marvel: Zero War Issue 1, Jones wrongly believes Gunnar to be Geno.
    • This might be a reference to the fact that during Collision event, in the falling scene of The Imagined and Geno, the latter uses Gunnar's model.
