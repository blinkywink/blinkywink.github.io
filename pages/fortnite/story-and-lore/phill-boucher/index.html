Phill Boucher

Overview

This article is about a topic in the real world.
The media represented in this page is not in the Fortnite universe.

Phill Boucher is an American composer and sound designer. He is best known for his work scoring Epic Games’ Fortnite. Live Events and trailers music. He has also made several Player Anthems for Rocket League.

About

About[]

Composer Phill Boucher has collaborated with several major game creators including Epic Games, Robot Entertainment, Firaxis Games and Daybreak Games; in addition to contributing music to several Disney projects, including the Pirates of the Caribbean franchise.

Phill is best known for his work scoring Epic Games’ Fortnite and Firaxis’ Civilization VI. He has also written scores for Robot Entertainment’s Orcs Must Die! 3 and ReadySet Heroes, Firaxis’ XCOM: Chimera Squad and Daybreak Games’ Planetside: Arena. Previously, Phill worked at Hans Zimmer’s Remote Control Productions where he was mentored by composer Geoff Zanelli. During that time, he worked on a range of projects including Rango, Pirates of the Caribbean: On Stranger Tides, Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Men Tell No Tales, The Odd Life of Timothy Green, and The Lone Ranger.

Phill’s other credits include writing additional music for DuckTales, Wrecked, No Stranger Than Love, The Astronaut Wives Club, and Invisible Sister.

Originally hailing from Massachusetts, Phill always had a love of music – beginning with the trumpet in grade school to playing guitar in a metal band in high school. He is an alumni of Berklee College of Music and currently works out of his studio in Los Angeles.
