The Imagined Order (often referred to as IO or The Order) was an organization in Fortnite: Battle Royale first introduced in Chapter 2: Season 2, but they have been active for thousands of years.[18] It was an inter-multiversal organization tasked with maintaining "order" within The Omniverse through the use of The Zero Point.[19] Their archnemesis was The Seven, who ultimately defeated them.

They were major antagonists of Fortnite: Battle Royale, serving as the main antagonists alongside The Army of the Last Reality until Chapter 3: Season 2.

Overview

Overview[]

The Imagined Order, often referred to as just IO, was an ancient organization that had been in charge of The Loop for thousands of years.[18] They operated in the shadows until Chapter 2: Season 2, when The Looper was rifted into The Bridge during The Device event. The IO used the Zero Point to traverse and manipulate realities, often drastically upsetting their natural evolution.[20] Their goal was to create a "perfect" civilization aligned with Geno's vision.[21] To achieve this, they created The Loop as a method to "research" and sometime recruit fighters to add to their ranks while collecting information about everything. This information was stored in an archive where nothing was ever deleted.[22] According to The Foundation, the IO's actions threatened to upset the balance of The Omniverse. However, for the IO, this was an acceptable price in their relentless pursuit of knowledge and power.[23]

The IO took their mission very seriously, requiring recruits to take an oath dedicating their lives to The Order.[24] The penalty for treason was termination.[25] Island Management Team members were offered immortality and protection for their families as long as they remained on Reality Zero.[26] Despite their structured appearance, the IO was built on "mutual distrust and paranoia."[27] The organization's leadership was secretive and neglectful. Even longtime members like Jones were unaware of who Geno truly was.[28] According to Doctor Slone, Geno had "no time to waste on the likes of him".[29] Based on the information Jones retrieved from the IO database, the organization operated at least 4,862 sites[30] and had five "levels" of ranks,[31] excluding the Inner Circle.

History

History[]

Before Chapter 1[]

Geno discovers The Zero Point and enslaves it in order to use it to access all Realities.[20] In an unknown Reality, Geno forms the Oathbound Empire and becomes their Champion, but later abandons them, leaving a Snapshot named The Ageless to rule them[127] and later founded the Imagined Order in order to protect and perfect all realities, creating the ideal civilization in Geno's vision.[21] In Reality Zero, the Imagined Order build The Bridge, The Vault and other bases around the Zero Point at the epicenter of the Island. They start various experiments with the Zero Point, one being to send Agent Jones into the Zero Point to see what happens.[128] Agent Jones discovers The Loop and his first Snapshot, Jonesy The First[129], is created. After they learn how to use the Zero Point as a portal to other dimensions, they travel to different realities and bring order to everything, as they like to call it: changing and altering realities to their liking, kidnapping people and bringing them to The Island and into The Loop, and other despicable actions. Their actions caused The Seven to form in retaliation.

The IO form The Loop using The Zero Point and kidnap people from different Realities, forcing them to fight eachother forever as "research"[130] and to collect the best fighters to add to their ranks. Geno had two daughters, The Imagined and The Order and genetically modified them to be perfect.[131] He sends them into the Loop after making a bet to IO agents that they will be first to escape.[132] They weren't, making Geno replace them with their stronger Snapshots.[36] The Sisters leave Geno behind, feeling betrayed by him, and join The Seven, one of the IO's main enemies. To keep their full control of the Island, they build the rooms in The Bridge to control The Storm, Loot and the Chests of the Athena island to distract the Loopers and keep them away from trying to escape the Loop.[133]

They capture The Cube King, who left The Last Reality after finding out that the Cubes are evil. They abuse him for his knowledge, but he eventually gets rescued by The Seven and goes by the name of The Origin and becomes their Battle Strategist. [134]

At some point, the IO traveled to Reality: 337 to mine and study cubic darkmatter.

Season 5[]

As the Sideways Rift starts to close, The IO send a group of Enforcers, who aim to restore balance and order, arrive through Rifts. They return objects that previously vanished through interdimensional travel back to the island.[135]

Season 7[]

When the Snow Biome reaches Athena, the IO takeover a small island made of ice and turn it into the Bunker Island.

Season 9[]

After The Devourer breaks out of Polar Peak, the IO were happy to let it destroy everything in The Loop, although were worried that he would harm The Zero Point.[136] The Paradigm, who was also captured at some point, instead volunteers to pilot a Mech to defeat the Devourer, but the IO threatens her that if she doesn't do it their way, they would ceate a Doomsday Device to destroy The Loop.[137] Singularity and her troops start construction of the Mecha Team Leader at Pressure Plant.[38] The Seven thinks that The Paradigm managed to escape by herself. The Origin, not believing she could escape by herself, (as he needed to be rescued) thinks she got inside help to escape.[138] She would later be called a traitor for helping the IO.[139]

The Final Showdown[]

The Devourer rises from the ocean and start heading towards The Vault. The Mecha Team Leader leaves Pressure Plant and attacks the Devourer, getting the two in a fight. The Devourer rips out Mecha's arm and heavily damages it, but Mecha damages The Vault and pulls The Zero Point out. It goes for Singularity's statue and pulls it out, revealing a giant sword. Devourer attacks back but the Mecha strike the sword in his head, killing The Devourer with no sweat. It flies off into The Ice Moon, leaving the Island for a long while.

Season X[]

The Zero Point breaks out of The Vault after The Final Showdown. The IO manipulates The Seven into restabilizing the Zero Point without destroying The Bridge on the way.[140] The Seven launch seven Rockets that create a Rift in front of The Meteor, making it crash into The Zero Point, creating a Black Hole that reorganized the Island of Athena into Apollo and Artemis.

Chapter 2: Season 1[]

IO's best [redacted: D4] Team, which consist of 4 E.G.O. agents, gets sent into The Loop through a Plane[14] to explore the new form The Island has taken. The team joins forces with Remedy and Cameo and fight against A.L.T.E.R. agents led by Chaos Agent.

Chapter 2: Season 2[]

At the start of the season, a group of Sledges chase Lynx near Crash Site when she is called by Midas.[141] During The Device Event, the IO try to put everything back to normal as Midas pushes back The Storm and starts slowly destroying The Loop as we know it. Midas ends up teleporting The Looper into Agent Jones' Office in The Bridge where The Looper makes their first contact with the IO, but are sent back after Jones asks, bewildered, if The Looper could hear him.

Chapter 2: Season 4[]

The Bridge is on alarm after Galactus pulls out The Zero Point, causing chaos on both The Bridge and the island. IO Guards run away aimlessly while Jones wakes up in his office, with Doctor Slone calling him.

Chapter 2: Season 5[]

Agent Jones picks up his phone and is ordered by Doctor Slone to prevent The Loopers from escaping The Loop, then formulates the "Project Hunter" plan.[142] Jones uses The Zero Point and his Portal Device to bring the best Hunters across all Realities in order to cause chaos and keep Loopers away from The Zero Point.[143] He brings all the hunters, but ends up damaging The Zero Point more as he creates Portals that push The Zero Point's capabilities and energy up to its limits. Jones realizes that the IO is doing nothing about The Zero Point destabilizing, so he tries to fix it himself[144] but, in order to do that, he needs access to 'Restricted File 8752', a file about The Seven. He tries to acess the file, but learns that his credentials have been revoked.[145] He goes rogue and makes a plan, involving using his Portal Device to bring a member of The Seven onto Apollo, breaking his oath with the Order.

Zero Crisis Finale[]

Jones uses his Rift Gun on the Zero Point to summon to the Island, The Foundation, The Seven's leader, for help to fix The Zero Point. The Foundation doesn't trust Jones at first, since the IO manipulated them back in Season X, leading to Athena's destruction. After The Foundation has a fight with Jones, Jones tells him that he can get him to Geno, leader of the Imagined Order that controls The Zero Point, and The Sisters, two members of The Seven. It is unknown whether this conversation is about The Seven's Sisters, or their snapshot Imagined Order counterparts, but likely the latter. They seal their deal and succeed at fixing The Zero Point, with The Foundation sealing himself in The Zero Point and creating The Spire to allow it to restore itself.

Chapter 2: Season 6 & Batman/Fortnite: Zero Point[]

Doctor Slone and other IO members fled The Bridge, leaving it mostly abandoned.[146] The Spire Cult later appeared and developed a cult following around The Spire and took the elements of the IO that they found, appropriating their logo. Slone and a group of IO Guards return to Reality Zero, starting the events of Chapter 2: Season 7.[152]

In Reality: NF-1935, villains led by Lex Luthor try to reach to the Sideways Rift opened by the IO, while the Justice League try to defend the rift. Slone wants to investigate Sunny and find out if she is the ringleader of the anti-human sentiment on the Island.[158] The Looper goes to earn her trust by making contact with aliens[159] and help her welcome them.[160] Slone wants Hayseed, one of the island's biggest preppers, on the IO's side.[161] To lure aliens, the IO invents Inflate-A-Bulls. Rook tasks the Looper to hunt down the traitor Riot, alongside Special Forces' help.[76] After searching the southeast corner of the Island, they eliminate Riot and report to command.[163] A major IO security breach happens and Slone wants to investigate. The Looper finds an access card for her and plant wiretraps to listen to "off the record" IO chatter, confirming the existence of a mole.[164] The mole leaks secret information about the IO to the public[165], and the Looper mindwipes the civillains. An existence of an "infiltratior of alien variety" is also found by Slone and the Looper goes to confront and eliminate them.[78] To learn more about the aliens, Slone and the Looper start monitoring their behavior and presence on the Island.[166] Noticing the rise in abduction numbers[167] and the aliens' interest in Slurpy Swamp, the Looper clears the area so the IO can investigate.[168] Desperate for an insider asset, Slone needs to squeeze intel from Joey or even recruit them.[169] The Looper bribes her with cat food and human gifts, and she agrees to try to discourage the aliens from annihiliating the Island.[170]

The IO comes up with a counter attack, but it has been compromized. Slone and the Looper decide to create a backup plan.[171] They collect Alien Devices to hide the energy signature of the Countermeasure Device at Corny Complex and create hundreds of bombs. Marigold helps the Looper to bait the aliens into abducting their bombs and pressure them into making a big move.[172] She "leaks" the location of their secret base to tempt the Mole into sharing it with the aliens,[173] but share it with locals by herself for insurance.[174] The countermeasure devices are prepared to strike the Mothership, but the area it is expected to crash on is populated.[175] The Looper warns everyone to give them time to leave and prevent them from interfering.[176] The Mole activates the devices early but their plan is quickly spoiled by the Looper.[177] Identifying Maven as the Mole, the Looper is sent to eliminate her and does so successfully.[55]

The Looper plants a data scraper on an IO blimp to be collected later. The Seven steal a Tank from the IO to reverse engineer it,[183] leading the IO to develop anti-tank barricades since they believe they are building tanks of their own.[184] Instead, The Origin decides to surprise them by deploying Cow Catchers and Armored Battle Buses.[185] The Imagined asks the Looper to wiretap the IO[186] to learn about a sensitive Imagined Order records cache.[187] As The Seven gather more Loopers to join their side, they send attacks on the locations IO tookover and slowly start to regain the control of the Island, claiming the IO's locations and crashing their Airships along. The Imagined sends a drone to pick up the collected data from the data scraper the Looper had placed earlier.[188] The data the Looper acquired has been corrupted so they use the computers at Daily Rubble to recover a data drive.[189]

Intel reaches Jones that an IO doomsday machine is in production.[190] Loopers sympathethic to the IO start losing their faith in Slone and her forces, giving insider intel to The Resistance.[191] Jones makes a counter propaganda mixtape that is played when the Looper hacks the IO's propaganda intercom.[192] Jones loses his IO credentials,[193] but hacks into a top-secret IO server at Command Cavern to find all the data on The Sisters deleted.[22] Upon further investigation, it is revealed that it was Geno who deleted them.[194] After the IO loses the Battle of Tilted Towers; The Seven corner the IO at Loot Lake, Command Cavern and The Fortress as they work on their mysterious Doomsday Device. Jones installs a seismometer on The Fortress to track its movement.[195] The doomsday device is revealed as The Collider and stands tall in Loot Lake. The Origin locates The Collider's two activation keys[196] Jones and the Looper cut the IO's power, which Jones speculates is used to charge the IO's combat shielding.[197] With the IO systems on safe boot mode, the Origin accesses a holo-table to simulate the effects of The Collider,[198] which reveals its purpose: to destroy everything in The Loop.[199] The Collider shoots out many pulses as it charges up, becoming ready for use.
