The Fox Clan is an ancient group of warriors and hunters which first appeared by name in Chapter 2: Season 5.

Overview

Overview[]

The Fox Clan is an ancient group of proud warriors and hunters originating from Oninoshima, in Reality: 783. Under the protection of the kitsune, the clan members wear Magical Masks shaped like them, giving them the power to manipulate Rifts, and cross Realities.

The Fox Clan initially lived on Oninoshima, however the artisan who made their masks decided to use the same process to make dangerous Masks of Power, which ended up in the hands of demons from the Spirit Realm and triggered the first Shattering War. After the end of the war, and the trauma that followed, the Fox Clan was blamed for the incident and they came into conflict with the River Guard.

Some time later, the Fox Clan, alongside the River Guard, The Luminaries, the Peace Syndicate and The Unseen, founded MEGA City. However by Chapter 2: Season 5 following an unknown event, the clan was annihilated with Vi as the only surviving member. She managed to ally herself with Drift, a young man who had received a mask and the powers of the Fox Clan despite not being a member, convincing him to join forces as his mask also made him a target.

With the help of Catalyst, a version of Drift from an alternate reality, they revived the Fox Clan, which returned to MEGA City. After MEGA City was forcibly relocated to Reality Zero, and the war between the Syndicates was averted, the Fox Clan and the River Guard managed to make peace. Following the destruction of Reality Zero, they returned to Oninoshima, where they confronted their past once more.

History

History[]

Unknown Period of Time[]

The Fox Clan was founded in Reality: 783 on the Island of Oninoshima,[19] and harnessed magic through special masks made by a family of artisans: the ancestors of Daigo, Jade, and Kendo. One day, their mask suppliers created Masks of Power, which contained dangerous capabilities that threatened the whole Island. An event known as the Shattering War broke out between the people of the Island and the Demon Warriors, resulting in most of the masks being destroyed, magic being banished, and the diminishing of the clan's bravery. The Fox Clan was blamed for the incident, and since then became sworn enemies with the River Guard.

Much later in Reality: UN-1, Drift obtains a Fox Clan mask - though it is unknown when he received it and who gave it to him.

Season 5[]

Drift is an ordinary human from California in the "real" world, Earth. After finding and spray-painting a Durrr Burger head in the desert, he is brought to the island by a Rift.

Season X[]

Catalyst appears on the island, brought to it by the unstable Zero Point.

Chapter 2: Season 5[]

The Fox Clan appears to have been attacked and tracked down by an unknown enemy, "something old ... something bad ...". Vi seems to be the only one to have escaped the attacker, appearing as the last known member of the clan's original iteration. She attempts to contact Drift, and asks him to work together up in order to survive, as he too is one of the targets because of his mask.

Vi manages to track down Drift, and the two team up. Catalyst, another Fox Clan Mask bearer, joins them despite not being a member at the time. Together, the three refound the Fox Clan with Vi as its leader, starting a new era for the Clan.

Vi's secret transmissions to Drift:

"///////Establishing Connection ----Beginning Scan---- Possible match found///// Drift? Drift. Come in. Do you read me?"

"I know you don't know who I am, but the Fox Clan is in trouble. We need you."

"We always thought we were the trackers, but something is tracking US down now. Something old. Something… bad."

"They got everyone. I barely escaped. I'm… I'm the only one left."

"Whoever gave you that Fox Clan mask made you a target..."

"If we don't work together - team up - they'll come for you next."

"They're closing in."

"I need to wipe my tracks, but I hope you got this transmission. If you did, I'll see you soon. I hope. ///Connection terminated."

Shortly after the final message, all relevant tweets were deleted.

Chapter 2: Season 7[]

A member of the Fox Clan, Vox Hunter appears on the Island, seemingly one of the original members— like Vi— who escaped the unknown threat before joining the new Fox Clan. He is distinguished from his allies by the fact that he is a digital avatar; recognized as the Fox Clan's dauntless tracker, he is both a cunning warrior and a poet.

Upon encountering The Looper,[20] Vox Hunter urges them to do "something worth writting about". The Looper then starts an "odyssey" with Vox Hunter in tow to document it. After they demonstrate charisma, courage, and face both "victories" and "losses" throughout several "conquests", Vox Hunter remarks that they remind him of the Fox Clan's most powerful warrior, and that the two would get along well.

Afterwards, Vox Hunter decides to write a song about The Looper, asking their name to see what would rhyme with it, before leaving.

Chapter 3: Season 3[]

For a time, Summer Drift roamed Sanctuary's coastline, enjoying the summer. He may have been contacted by the Fox Clan's members, for business.[21]

Chapter 4: Season 1[]

Three Driftwalker snapshots took refuge within Faulty Splits at the abandoned bowling alley, however Joni the Red arrived to hunt them down and kill them.

Later, because of the instabilities of Asteria's reality, Vi, Drift and Catalyst, seemingly working together on something important, were forcibly rifted onto the island, along with a piece of what looks like an IO Surface Hub. After a few seconds, they claim that they "can't be here" and "need to go back", and use their Rift powers to teleport away.

After some time, two of them were involuntarily rifted again; they scout the area and state they can't be on the island while others need them,[22] before rifting away.

After the Rift Gate was sabotaged and created a large Rift leading to Reality: 783, one of them would unwillingly be rifted back onto Asteria once more, wordlessly upset or exhausted for an unknown reason. However this time, they don't leave.

Chapter 4: Season 2[]

As MEGA City is being rifted onto the island, Stray, the brother of the regal River Empress Mizuki, falls out of one of the Rifts, and lands on Asteria, effectively returning to the city. It's later revealed that during an unknown period of time prior to Chapter 4: Season 2, Stray was recruited by Drift, and left behind both his home and the River Guard in order to join the Fox Clan, further raising tensions between the two historic rivals as a result.

During the Syndicate Quests, after being impressed by The Looper, the Fox Clan agrees to attend a dinner hosted by the Peace Syndicate in order to negotiate with the River Guard. All goes well and resentments are cleared, thus they both become allies to each other and the Peace Syndicate, resulting in their longtime rivalry coming to an end.

Kimiko Five-Tails appears on the Island as the guardian of the Fox Clan, though doesn't make herself known until Chapter 4: Season 3.

Chapter 4: Season 3[]

Kimiko Five-Tails, the guardian of the Fox Clan, reveals herself to everyone, including Fennix. Upon interacting, Fennix learns of the Fox Clan's existence, with Kimiko stating that she represents the clan itself.[23]

Chapter 4: Season OG[]

In Chapter 4: Season OG's Reality - an offshoot timeline from Reality Zero - a version of Drift identical to that within the prime timeline is brought to the island by a Rift during the same era as Season 5, mirroring the initial events of Reality Zero. Later, another version of Drift, known as Rift Strider Drift, appears in the timeline.

Rift Strider Drift serves as the leader of the Infinite Drift Unit - a combat group entirely composed of alternate versions of Drift, including Skulldrifter and Fishdrift.

Chapter 6: Season 1[]

The Fox Clan returns to Oninoshima, creating a hideout underneath Twinkle Terrace, a smaller base within Seaport City, and founding the All You Can Catch! restaurant. Various kitsune statues can be found scattered throughout the island as well.

Vi patrols Rolling Blossoms Farm, and reveals that though they seem to have sustained positive relationships with their allies— Mizuki and the River Guard— the emergence of the Oni and various elemental masks across the island seem to trouble the clan, and brings contemplation towards the mending of their historic rivalry. With danger on the horizon, she confides in her worries with Catalyst, and Kimiko Five-Tails. Vi, likewise to their formal rival Mizuki,[24] also shows a disliking towards Daigo and his ancestors, hinting at another historic fued and a threat to their safety.[25]

Admist the re-emergence of the Fox Clan, Vox Hunter has sustained his passion for poetry which Vi indulges in, curious towards what the poets think of their clan.[26] Drift appears to have taken Stray as a direct mentee, though Vi has to remind Drift to train his apprentice.

Though the River Guard too reflects on their history with the clan, neither side seems eager to break their ongoing peace treaty— should none cause any problems for the other. Later, Serge appears on the island as Drift's second apprentice and newest recruit,[13] with Vi welcoming him into the clan, while also trying to keep Drift on track with training his recruits.[27]

Chapter 6: Season 4[]

In the aftermath of Mask Maker Daigo's attempts at summoning his master, the Fox Clan were reminded of the horrors of The Shattering War and fled to other realities,[28] with Drift going to Reality: Unknown, a place that brings back many memories about his past life from his time in Reality Zero.[29][30]

Chapter 6: Mini Season 2[]

Zero Hour[]

Presumably, alongside all of Oninoshima's inhabitants and allies from other realities, the Fox Clan returned to fight the Dark Presence.

Messages

Messages[]

On January 24th 2021, the banner and profile picture of Fortnite's Twitter account was changed to Vi's logo. The Twitter account made a series of tweets regarding the situation of the Fox Clan. It was reverted to its original state only half an hour later.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Through the messages of Vi, it's revealed that Drift was given his Kitsune mask— although he was not an official member of the clan at this time. It's unknown how, why, and who gave it to him.
    • By the time of Chapter 4: Season 2, the Fox Clan seemed to have been completely reborn, and there was no trace nor mention of the unknown enemy who previously attacked them.
    • It is possible that the Fox Clan's attacker was the army of The Last Reality, which is already known by all of the syndicates of MEGA City by the time of Chapter 4: Season 2 suggesting a previous encounter with them.
      • It is also possible the attacker may have originated from Reality: 783 and the time of the Shattering War.
  • Summer Drift considers Driftwalker and Snow Drift as members of the Fox Clan according to his secret dialogue.
    • Although an official member, Vox Hunter doesn't activate the Fox Clan-specific secret dialogue of the Summer Drift NPC; he also doesn't appear with Drift, Vi and Catalyst in their Rift Encounters.
  • The Fox Clan's former historic rival is the River Guard, ruled by River Empress Mizuki.
  • Ninjas seem to be the primary inspiration for the Fox Clan, while Samurai are the primary inspiration for the River Guard.
  • All members of the Fox Clan generally produce rift energy at the level of the eyes of their mask and their shoulders; the color varies according to the member.
    • Serge is the first and only masked member to lack this particularity.
  • There is various Fox Clan related imagery in MEGA City, such as Drift on advertisements and a Kitsune sign.
