The Triarchs are the triarchy rulers of an unnamed, peaceful kingdom, from a realm that once stretched across the stars.[3] They were first introduced during Chapter 4: Season 2.

Overview

Overview[]

The Triarchs are made up of three rulers; all who were once like family,[4] sharing the responsibilities of governing their realm in perfect harmony.[5] They were happy, even in difficult times.

After the unforseen fall of their realm, Triarch Nox fled, while the third Triarch vanished. Triarch Aurora tried to rule on her own, but without the balance of the others, she faltered.[6]

History

History[]

Before Chapter 4[]

The Triarchs lived together peacefully, ruling in harmony. One day, their kingdom unanticipatedly fell. Triarch Nox fled into The Omniverse, while the third Triarch vanished. Triarch Aurora attempted to rule alone but ultimately failed, and their stars went dark.[7]

Afterwards, Triarch Nox descended into a path of true darkness. He encountered an unknown entity, described by Triarch Aurora as a "decaying force," which transformed him into someone unrecognizable. Triarch Aurora had heard stories about him, but the Nox she had known— a kind and peaceful ruler— was gone. In his place was a dangerous being whom she found traces of in torn galaxies and ruined realities.

Chapter 4: Season 2[]

Triarch Nox arrived on the Island, and first appeared in the Syndicate Quests. He became an ally to The Unseen, and notably helped Dahlia communicate with The Last Reality. He also sabotaged the terminals of the Peace Syndicate containing all their intel about The Last Reality[8] and tried to prevent anyone from accessing it.[9] His true goal, however, remains unknown.

Chapter 4: Season 3[]

Era warns Nox that Aurora is coming, claiming to know that he was hiding from her[10] - though Era's suspicions were untrue, as Nox had arrived with a specific purpose.

Chapter 4: Season 4[]

In the Triarch Aurora Quests, Aurora arrives on the Island seeking to reunite with Nox. She enlists the help of The Looper, sharing her story and uncovering clues about Nox's whereabouts along the way.[11] During their investigation, they discover that Nox had entered Kado Thorne's vault to retrieve a relic from their kingdom, before quietly leaving the Island.[12] Aurora believes Nox's actions signal a want to rebuild their kingdom and seek redemption.[13] In turn, she decides to leave the Island and wait for him in their domain.[14]
