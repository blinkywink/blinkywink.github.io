Overview

This article is about the faction found in Fortnite: Battle Royale. For the Jam Track by Jung Kook and Latto, see Seven.
This page may contain speculation and/or fan interpretations.
Due to the lack of official information, some info on this page may therefore be partially incorrect.

“
I don't know who will answer this call. But soon enough, The Seven will stand against the Dark Voyager.
”
— The Order

The New Seven, also known as The Seven, are a group of heroes dedicated to protecting The Zero Point, opposing the Imagined Order, and ultimately destroying The Loop at any cost. For centuries, they studied the Omniverse and fought against the Imagined Order, culminating in their victory during the Zero War, which came at the cost of The Imagined's life.

However, their victory was short-lived. Shortly afterward, The Herald defeated most of the group. The Order and The Foundation vanished, leaving Paradigm as the last active member. She ultimately sacrificed herself to save the Loopers and restore Reality Zero through the Zero Fusion.

Although the members who were Chromed were later revealed to still be alive, they were undergoing a process that altered their very existence into something new. This transformation ultimately resulted in their permanent loss, as AMIE was unable to reach them in time. As a result, only The Foundation and The Order remained.

Following these events, the Dark Voyager began reforging the Zero Point in Reality: 266 by collecting shards scattered across the Omniverse. In response, a distress signal sent by Jones was received by The Order, who then tried to contact any remaining members of The Seven. The Foundation received the call alongside a mysterious "version" of the Visitor, leading to the formation of The New Seven. Unbeknownst to any of the New Seven, this Visitor was actually the Imagined Order's founder, Geno.

They are the central protagonists of Fortnite: Battle Royale, serving as the main protagonists of Season 4, Season X, Chapter 3 and Chapter 7.

Overview

Overview[]

The Seven[]

The iteration of The Seven founded in Reality Zero by The Foundation. Their goal was to stop the Imagined Order, liberate The Zero Point, and destroy The Loop. The Seven are composed of 7 inner members, and supported by outer members who adhere to their cause such as Loopers they freed from the Loop. All seven members of the organization have renounced their original names and taken names that reflect their role or philosophy. They also wear technologically advanced armors, granting them numerous abilities in addition to being able to resist The Loop's memory loss. Similar to members of the Imagined Order, The Seven have lived far longer lifetimes than any normal human. However, with the exception of The Origin, The Imagined, and The Order, the reason for this long lifespan is unknown. The organization met its end after the death of The Imagined, followed by the sudden disappearance of The Foundation and The Order and the loss of The Origin, The Scientist and The Visitor to the Chrome. The last member, Paradigm, later sacrificed herself. Although The Order and The Foundation ultimately made it out alive, they remained scattered, putting an end to the organization.

The New Seven[]

This is an iteration reuniting the surviving members alongside new members.

History

History[]

Before Chapter 1[]

The Foundation has been studying The Zero Point and the Omniverse for a very long time.

Unknown when or how, The Foundation recruits 6 other people with the aim of destroying the IO, and liberating The Zero Point. The last member he recruited was The Origin who was saved from an IO prison after running away from The Last Reality, abandoning his duties as The Cube King. Geno the IO's founder, quickly becomes The Foundation's greatest fear, knowing that they are in control of the Zero Point. The Seven develop sealed environment suits as a way to enter and leave The Loop anytime they want without being affected by the Loop's effects.

Season 3[]

A Meteor is spotted over the island, with its impact zone unknown. Inside is the The Visitor, but this is yet to be revealed until the next season.

Season 4[]

The Meteor strikes the Island, destroying Dusty Depot and creating Dusty Divot. The Visitor, the first member to be revealed, arrives from an unknown world. He came to the island in a metal space capsule embedded within the meteor. Shortly after escaping the capsule in Week 7 of the season. He broke out of the research facility stationed around his landing spot, he went to the Villain Lair near Snobby Shores and used Hop Rocks to modify a movie prop rocket into a functioning vehicle.

Blast Off[]

On June 30th, he launched the Rocket, starting the Blast Off event. During the event, the rocket appeared opened a Rift, sending the rocket into it. The rocket then emerged elsewhere on the map, repeatedly opening rifts until it blasted off into the sky, creating a giant Sideways Rift above the Island.

Season X[]

In Season X, after the Zero Point was exposed from the Season 9 Live Event. It caused further destabilization on the Island. As such, a member of the Seven was manipulated by Imagined Order to deal with it. With an alternate reality Meteor sitting stationary above the newly reformed Dusty Depot, The Scientist emerged from inside.

The Scientist worked to create the Rift Beacons, which opened up six rifts in the sky. During this time, the Scientist created several tapes. They were able to be collected and cryptically described his plans and attempts to remember his plan during the Looping process.

The End[]

In The End event, he launched a rocket into the sky, making a rift which activated the six smaller rifts created by the Rift Beacons. Rockets emerged from each rift, and came together to unfreeze The Meteor and divert it into the Zero Point, causing it to create a black hole and to rearrange the island's matter and form the Apollo and Artemis islands.

Chapter 7: Season 1[]

Pacific Break[]

“
Mission log. This reality feels familiar in so many ways, yet something's not quite right.
”
— The Order

The Order, one of The Seven, arrives on Reality: 266 after receiving a distress signal from Jones while analyzing the aftermath of the battle on Reality: 783. Using a pod encapsulated in a meteor similar to those previously deployed by The Seven, she lands near Classified Canyon.

Upon touching down on the Golden Coast, she records her first mission log, explaining her arrival and noting that the situation appears linked to the shattered Zero Point, as her scans reveal unusually high concentrations of Zero Point energy across the Island. She later travels to Storm Station, where she records a second mission log, observing that someone else is searching for the Zero Shards and that she must uncover their motives. Speaking to herself, she reflects on the fate of The Seven — who are no longer what they once were — before refocusing on the mission and beginning to scout the area.

Lethal Labs[]

After the Dark Voyager seized control of InnoLoop Labs and the Zero Shard, The Looper sought to infiltrate his ranks by sending a message via a holotable found in Lethal Labs, pledging their loyalty. Intrigued, the Dark Voyager assigned them missions to test their usefulness — notably to gather intelligence on the Zero Shards and The Seven. However, this proved to be nothing more than a ploy; the Dark Voyager used The Looper for his own ends before planning to dispose of them, claiming to prefer the loyalty of minions forged over aeons rather than that of a stranger offering to help. He dispatched his minions — Skulldroid and NITE Unit Sniper — to kill them, but The Looper swiftly defeated both in battle and escaped. For the following weeks, The Looper helped various inhabitants of the Island, notably Miles Cross, Carter Wu, Cat Holloway and Lt. Winter.

Setting the Stage with Kingston[]

While The Looper began assisting Kingston, The Order continued her mission and set up camp near Ol' Stumpy, where she recorded a new mission log. She noted that Reality: 266 feels strangely familiar, yet something seems off. The Loop appeared stable, but the Storm had changed — suggesting someone was tampering with it, or that it was a consequence of the fractured Zero Point. She expressed a wish that The Scientist were there to apply his intellect to the problem, but pressed on regardless.

The Order's Mission[]

“
Based on that ship of his, he's connected to the Last Reality. But something feels off. I think there's more going on than meets the eye.
”
— The Order

The Order infiltrated Lethal Labs, now overrun with thugs in service of the Dark Voyager. Leaving another mission log inside, she reported to Jones that she had secured a lock on the last components needed to build the device that would allow her to contact her allies. Before being abruptly interrupted and captured by the Dark Voyager, she noted that he must have a connection to The Last Reality based on the design of his ship — while remaining uncertain that there wasn't more to his origins than what met the eye. She was then imprisoned in Lethal Labs' secret cell — the very same one Carina had heard rumors about and shared with Cat Holloway. Shortly after, the Dark Voyager departed Reality: 266 with his ship.

The Reassembly Events[]

As Carina recruited The Looper to help uncover the truth, she directed them to investigate everything that remained mysterious — including the Storm and the Reboot Vans — while also confiding her wariness of Human Bill and the Henchfans. After having The Looper question Jones and Hope about the Dark Voyager on her behalf, both urged The Looper to persuade Carina to stay out of it, fearing for her safety. Meanwhile, the golden rift began to reform, and before long the Dark Voyager's ship emerged once more. Hope and Jones were stunned to learn he had already secured a second Zero Shard — the ease of it standing in stark contrast to the battles they had fought just to retrieve one — and began questioning the Dark Voyager's true identity and purpose. They told The Looper they weren't ready to confront him yet, but that they were building their forces in preparation for the fight ahead.

Breakpoint[]

As Jones informed The Looper of the coming war against the Dark Voyager and had them train with new weapons and gear, The Looper also explored Lethal Labs' underground facility — where they discovered The Order, imprisoned in the rumored secret cell and sealed inside a cubic barrier that prevented her from rifting out. She asked The Looper to find the blue-haired girl who had been poking around the labs and to retrieve the keycard she had found that would deactivate the barrier. In exchange, The Looper helped Carina track down Bigfoot and Human Bill, and collect gold bars on her behalf. Upon returning to The Order, The Looper, Jones, and Hope successfully freed the spy, allowing her to rift out in search of the remaining scattered members of The Seven — who would soon be needed for the coming fight. Her escape triggered the cell's alarms, bringing Skulldroid and NITE Unit Sniper rushing in to deal with the intruders, only to be defeated once again.

The Stranger[]

“
This Dark Voyager is dangerous.
And we don't know what he's planning to do with the Zero Point shards.
”
— The Order

The following week, The Looper contacted The Order, now patrolling at Classified Canyon. She tasked them with assisting her new mission: constructing a device to contact The Seven — or whoever remained. She explained that the plan was drawn from a database of emergency blueprints left behind by The Scientist, and that the beacon they would build was designed to reach any surviving allies. Following The Order's instructions, The Looper cleared a path for the signal and gathered the materials needed to build the transmitter, which was then installed under the antenna near the Battlewood sign. Once complete, The Order thanked them and assured The Looper that whoever answered the call, The Seven would soon stand against the Dark Voyager and put a stop to whatever he was planning with the Zero Shards. Jones also concluded his training regime, having The Looper sharpen their combat skills and clear debris at the Canyon to prepare a landing zone for incoming allies.

Visitor's Return[]

“
I don't know who will answer this call.
But soon enough, The Seven will stand against the Dark Voyager.
”
— The Order

After some time, a red rift laced with Havoc Energy tore open the sky above the Golden Coast. Jones, Hope, The Order, and The Looper finished preparing the landing zone just in time. As the rift began to glow intensely, a rocket burst through — generating a chain of rifts that gradually cleansed the vessel of the Havoc energy contaminating it. Following a series of complex maneuvers and rift jumps, and narrowly avoiding a collision with The Shape, the rocket stabilized and executed a safe landing on the newly constructed platform at Classified Canyon. The Visitor had arrived.

When The Order made contact with him, both quickly recognized they were not the individuals they had expected to meet. The Visitor deduced that they originated from different realities or divergent timelines. Despite this, he agreed to aid the heroes, affirming that regardless of when or where they came from, The Seven stand united. He was then brought to the main base, where The Order and the others briefed him on the current situation. In return, The Visitor shared his own experiences — recounting his pursuit of the Dark Voyager and the events that had led him there. He then enlisted The Looper's help in improving the new base by testing hiding spots, clearing threats at the Canyon, and evaluating the new Seven gear he had supplied.

“
Fascinating. You are not the Order I expected to meet when I picked up your distress call.
”
— The Visitor

Not long after his arrival, The Visitor grew increasingly uneasy with The Order's approach to investigating the Dark Voyager. Concerned by her methods, he asked The Looper to speak with her and persuade her to reconsider. The Order, however, instructed The Looper to disregard The Visitor's concerns. Using Stealth Splash as camouflage, The Order and The Looper attempted to outmaneuver the Dark Voyager. During the operation, The Order contacted The Visitor again, urging him to assist if he was truly so worried about the plan. He refused, insisting that confronting the Dark Voyager directly was unwise — especially since they still did not fully understand his capabilities. The Order countered that they would never learn what he was capable of without conducting reconnaissance.

As they began sabotaging the Dark Voyager's work at Lethal Labs, The Visitor reached out once more, ordering The Order to stay away from the enemy. She refused, stating she did not take orders from him. In response, The Visitor reminded her that he had pursued the Voyager through the husks of burned-out stars and the ashes of collapsing realities and that if he were so easily defeated, he would have already done so. The Order retorted that they had only just met and that she did not know his capabilities, before cutting off communication.

Later, after The Order dispatched The Looper to investigate the Shards, The Visitor contacted them again. He accused The Order of endangering the mission through her hubris and impatience, remarking that she had become reckless — just like her sister. The Order replied that The Imagined would have understood the stakes and expressed surprise that he did not. The Visitor then asked whether she had learned nothing from losing her. She answered that she had learned one thing: when everything is on the line, it is better to be reckless than timid. The Order then instructed The Looper to await her next call.

First Wave[]

Hope informs The Looper that she's been receiving coded transmissions from The Elites, a team of skilled fighters which Daigo, her former frenemy, is now also a part of. She tasks the hero with scouting The Basement as a possible base for The Elites, also telling them that if they were to impress the team, they may join the heroes in the fight to come.

Meanwhile, aboard the Dark Harvester, the Dark Voyager has kidnapped Fishstick in an attempt to extort information about his homeworld which is home to the third Zero Point Shard needed to assemble the fractured Zero Point. After locating Reality: 7021 among the countless realities in his map, the Dark Voyager opens a rift to reach the planet housing Atlantis and its inhabitants. The Zero Point Shard is pulled by the Dark Harvester from the bottom of the ocean, causing the city to dry up and the fisherman floating above it to be extracted with the artifact. Once inside the ship, the Dark Voyager sends his Trespassers to remove the intruder who is angrily looking at the confused Fishstick.

The Dark Voyager quickly returns to Reality: 266 and extracts the third shard he acquired from his ship, now to be placed with the others at Lethal Labs. Hope starts to worry about the ease with which the Dark Voyager has been able to gather three Zero Point Shards with his ship, comparing it with all the challenges she had to go through to simply retrieve one.

Final Wave[]

As the Second Wave concludes with the third Zero Point Shard placed with the others, the Dark Voyager enacts his Final Wave, starting to charge the Zero Point Shards with the Dark Harvester energy beam. Once charged, the ship shoots a final blow strong enough to generate Zero Point tendrils and opening three shard distortions at Clawsy Lodge, Bumpy Bay and near Looper's Leap. After the blow, the Dark Harvester shortly loses energy, but its systems are seemingly rebooted by Void Walker Lynx through a small Zero Point splinter allowing her master to observe the position of the distortions[24].

The Looper is quickly sent by Hope to investigate the distortions, while Peelians' actors start to feel that the Golden Coast is approaching dark times.

Before Season 2[]

Exalted Return[]

As the shard distortions appear across the Golden Coast, The Foundation arrives in Reality: 266 joining the New Seven and, after getting rid of his old one, reforged tech suits are built for him and The Order. But just as the team of heroes is mostly reunited, three Zero Point Shards are brought on the Island, bringing different realities and merging them with the Island.

The Ice King and his Frigid Fortress cover the northern part of the Island, Reality: 7021's Squibbly Shores takes the place of Bumpy Bay while, near the New Sanctuary, a shard contaminates the waters near Looper's Leap.

The Ice King poses himself as a new threat for the Golden Coast, looking to freeze whoever holds enough power to pose a threat to Reality. among his collection there are the restless spirit Orelia, the warrior Guan Yu, the first Avenger Captain America and The Prisoner, his former knight. While on the Island, the Ice King adds Human Bill and Peely to his throne room prisoners.

Mission Recap[]

The New Seven plan a first strike against the Ice King, but The Foundation is frozen in the process and locked inside the Ice King Vault. Meanwhile, the Dark Voyager, witnessing the arrival of the Ice Kingdom, hopes to forge an alliance with its king and after arriving to Frigid Fortress, he is allowed to walk across the room by the Frozen Knights and present his offer to the Ice King: using his and Lady Windfrost's power to keep the elements in sync[25].

The heroes' priority becomes locating their frozen leader and activate his reforged suit's device to melt the ice and set him free, so as The Order, The Visitor, Jones and Hope find their way through the icy winds, The Foundation is freed from ice and, after taking down one of the Ice King's Frozen Knights, he makes his way through the Fortress' throne room. Once there, the leader dodges ice beams shot by the Ice King that accidentally end up destroying Peely's frozen body and freeing Human Bill from his ice pillar, but the confrontation ends in a tie.

Lore Trivia

Lore Trivia[]

  • Some of The Seven's members originally had different names before they were changed to the current ones.
    • The Visitor was originally called The Stranger. [26]
    • The Scientist was originally named Visitor Volta.
    • The Foundation was originally named The Chosen. [27]
    • The Visitor (Reality Unknown) was originally named The Visitor (Reality 157).
  • The method of inter-dimensional travel The Seven use appears to be meteors, as in all of their appearances before chapter 3 (except for The Paradigm), this is how they have arrived on the Island.
    • All meteors used to arrive on the Island crashed near Dusty Depot in some form.
  • Of all Organizations, The Seven have suffered the worst fate of all.
    • The Imagined was discorporated, each of the molecules of her body are being sent into a different reality.
      • Somehow, the Imagined was active after the events of Collision and Zero War until she went missing, leaving the Imagined Blade behind in the Rave Cave.
        • This is due to the fact The Imagined death was initially not planned. However, during Chapter 7: Season 2 corrected this by taking the events as they happened in Zero War comics
    • The Paradigm sacrificed her life to trigger the Zero Fusion, seemingly fusing with it.
    • The Origin, The Scientist and The Visitor were all chromed, taken to an unknown location, before being sculpted into something new, having their existence rewritten completely.
  • The Visitor (Reality Unknown) is the first member of The Seven introduced as an alternate counterpart of an existing one, instead of an evolution.
    • However, he retains the given name scheme initiated by Paradigm (Reality-659) for remixed Seven members, with the last reality they traveled to upon their introduction added to their titles.
  • In OG Season 4 Greatest Hits Quests, The Visitor (Reality Unknown) claims to remember Risky Reels and Dusty Divot fondly.
    • This indicates that he has already visited one of the diverged timeline of Reality Zero in the past, or that he is a version of The Visitor who visited Reality Zero itself during Season 4.
