The Fire Kingdom was an organization founded by The Prisoner after he escaped Polar Peak. It was first introduced during Season 8. They are the arch-nemesis of The Ice Kingdom.

They served as the main antagonists of Season 7 and Season 8 as well as posthumous antagonists of Season 9 and Chapter 3: Season 2 (Fortnite X Marvel: Zero War).

Overview

Overview[]

The Fire Kingdom was founded by The Prisoner, a former knight and friend of The Ice King who became his enemy after a disagreement over the use of powerful artifacts collected by The Ice Kingdom. The kingdom is primarily composed of The Prisoner, acting as its ruler, the Hybrids he took with him when he escaped from the Polar Peak dungeon, and Ruin, a magma creature created by The Prisoner in the center of the volcano to act as his right-hand man.

History

History[]

Before Chapter 1[]

The Ice King and his brave knights resided on The Ice Moon, collecting items and beings of power deemed too dangerous to exist in The Omniverse. But one day, The King's staunchest comrade & knight of flame, The Prisoner, came up with the idea of using those items to alter and conquer Realities as they want. This led to The King freezing the Prisoner, his three other knights, their collection and their fortresses on the Ice Moon. He then brought them to Reality Zero, where he believed it would be isolated and safe. He turned the ice upon himself in an attempt to cause an eternal slumber.

Season 6[]

Following a cosmic storm, a piece of the Ice Moon, known as Polar Peak, broke off and landed in the ocean of the Athena island. It appeared from the southwest of the Island, getting closer.

Season 7[]

As the snow melted and the Ice King woke up, he sensed danger from the reawakening of his Prisoner and charged his power to unleash an Ice Storm and summon the Ice Legion. Along with his Queen, they guarded the Snow Biome.

However this was in vain, as The Prisoner managed to escape his chains, taking with him the eggs of the Hybrids and by traveling Athena, managed to break his remaining chains and recover his powers.

Season 8[]

The Prisoner creates the Volcano to serve as his castle, forming a throne inside. Some of the Hybrid eggs hatch, and they now serve their self-proclaimed "king", officially forming "The Brood".[4] The Ice King and his forces fight against The Prisoner's army to prevent him from unleashing even worse dangers, as between their kingdoms and the intervention of the Scallywags, pirates led by the feared Blackheart, war enrages between the three of them, possibly for the fate of whatever lies underneath The Vault.[5]

At some point during the war, Ruin is created to act as The Prisoner's right-hand man, bringing forth Ruination and researching the digsites of The Government. In their final duel with The Ice Kingdom, The Fire Kingdom was ultimately defeated, with The Ice King triumphing over his former friend and freezes him underwater for eternity. However, he ultimately fails in his duty, as The Fire King was able to make the Volcano erupt, striking Retail Row, Tilted Towers, and Polar Peak, which leads to a deadlier menace breaking free.

Season 9[]

With Polar Peak melting following The Volcano's eruption, The Devourer lets loose and The Ice King returns to his Moon in shame and defeat, causing deadly snowstorms to wipe out all beings, living or unliving to isolate his castle and prevent anything else from accessing it. Safety measures would also be put in place to detect any living being that would arrive on the Moon, proving to the Ice King that they are too dangerous for The Omniverse. Once again, the eternal ice was placed upon himself. The Devourer, uninterrupted by both kingdoms, would be stopped by The Paradigm and Singularity's Mech built by The Government and the Imagined Order.

Chapter 2: Season 7[]

Inside The Bridge's CSI Room, there lies a conspiracy or research board related to The Seven which also has The Prisoner attached onto it, showcasing a lack of knowledge regarding The Seven. The Prisoner's inclusion could likely be a hint toward The Origin and his flame powers.

Chapter 4: Season OG[]

In Reality: UN-0-C4OG, an alternate timeline of Reality Zero, the piece of the Ice Moon arrives on the Island, but The Ice King and The Prisoner are nowhere to be found. The Polar Peak Castle is already gone and The Devourer seems to have already broken free from the iceberg by the time of its arrival, leaving the Kingdom's state in that reality questionable.

Elsewhere, The Brood still emerges, seemingly separate from the Kingdom, with The Brood's loyal alliance making their move, now evolved with Drakon Steel armor and claws.

OG Chapter 1: Season 7[]

The Ice Moon arrives in Reality: Unknown just it did on Athena, bringing their arch-enemy and former alliance, The Ice Kingdom. Hidden within Polar Peak and the surrounding area lies The Infinity Blade, the eggs of the Hybrids who would become The Brood, and the chains and cell of a new prisoner, Pyreheart. As time progressed, the ice would begin to melt, exposing these ancient items and creatures, with Pyreheart taking the eggs of The Brood.

OG Chapter 1: Season 8[]

Pyreheart, the eternally burning Captain of the endless seas, creates new variants of The Fire Kingdom's old locations for his Crew, leading them and the new Brood in their treasure-hunting escapades in place of the Fire Kingdom, Government, Scallywags and The Ice Kingdom from the original timeline.

Chapter 7: Season 2[]

The Prisoner remains within the eternal ice, now back in the grasp of The Ice King a taken to Reality: 266 as the war between The Ice King and The Seven arises.
