The Omniverse is the collection of every reality, multiverse, and megaverse representing all known franchises and beyond. While initially believed to be the sole result of The Zero Point giving birth to infinite Realities after it sprang into being,[2] it turned out that other beings participated in its creation such as One Above All as well as The Source and its Hands, upon the reveal Fortnite worlds coexisted with Marvel and DC's respective Multiverses.

Overview

Overview[]

The Omniverse refers to the collection of countless and "infinite" worlds created by different beings such as The Zero Point. These interconnected yet distinct universes, referred to as Realities, each follow their own unique rules and are not intended to interact.[3]

Realities can be grouped within structures known as Galaxies,[4][5] also referred to as Multiverses in other media. Galaxies act as containers for Realities that share a foundational set of rules.[6] In some cases, Realities may not belong to any Galaxy, such as Reality Zero, which was floating in isolation in the void during its second and third iterations.[7] Groups of Galaxies or Multiverses, form Megaverses.

Note: The Galaxies in Fortnite's representation of the Omniverse should not be confused with real-world galaxies, as they represent entirely different concepts. In the real world (and in space-themed fictional universes), galaxies are vast collections of gas, dust, billions of stars, and their solar systems, all bound together by gravity. In contrast, Fortnite Omniverse Galaxies refer to containers of entire Realities—in other words, they are representations of Multiverses.

Dimensions, Realms, and Diverging Timelines[]

In certain Realities, additional divisions exist, such as dimensions or realms.[8] This is the case with Reality: 783 with a dimension known as the Spirit Realm. These remain integral parts of a single Reality rather than being separate Realities. However, some locations considered realms, such as The Sideways, are unique Realities in their own right.[9]

Alternate timelines are another phenomenon within the Omniverse. These occur when two universes once identical diverge due to a specific event, forming a new Reality. One example is the divergence between Reality: 616 and Reality 617,[10] caused by timeline interference.

Another significant divergence occurred with Reality: UN-0-C4OG, which split from Reality Zero.[11][12][13][14]

Diverging timelines have also led to the appearance of new locations during different events across multiple seasons.[15][16][17][18]

This concept was further explored in Fortnite: Save the World, where the Long Road Home and Timeless Questlines depicted Dennis Jr., a time traveler from a possible future, returning with the help of Ned to warn Homebase of their impending doom. Doctor Vinderman elaborated that timelines, worlds, and Realities are synonymous.[19][20][21]

Lynchpins[]

During Chapter 5: Season 2, it was revealed that the Omniverse's integrity was maintained by the existence of "lynchpins", maintaining realities together. Very few is known about these lynchpins, however The Oracle revealed that Peely—Jones's best friend— was one of them.[22]

Travel Between Realities[]

Inter-dimensional travel between Realities is possible through various means, including The Zero Point, Rifts, and the Portal Device. Travel may also occur by physically moving through the void between Realities, as demonstrated by The Army of the Last Reality,[23] Kado Thorne,[24][25] Triarch Aurora, and The Ice King.

Risks[]

Interactions between Realities and time travel present enormous risks for the integrity of a Reality, a Galaxy, or even the entire Omniverse:

  • Depending on the case, a time traveler can alter a pre-existing timeline, changing past events, or create a new timeline, thus forming a new Reality. However, excessive time travel and alterations to the past as well as alteration of events not supposed to change can severely damage a timeline. In such cases, time becomes unstable, and the affected Reality is doomed to collapse. These corrupted timelines can even spread to adjacent Realities within the same Multiverse, potentially leading to the collapse of that entire Multiverse.[21] According to Dr. Vinderman, it is necessary to eliminate these corrupted Realities. This scenario occurred in the main timeline of Save the World after Homebase prevented the event that would lead to their doom depicted in Dennis Jr. and Dark Ray's respectives futures.
  • Interactions between Realities can significantly alter their development, often resulting in major catastrophes, as noted by The Foundation. In the worst cases, Realities may collide with one another. This has happened during the Zero Crisis Finale[26][27] as well as during Chapter 2: Season 8.[28]

History

History[]

  • Prologue

The First Big Bang[]

In the beginning, there was Nothing. Then, in a burst of pure creation, The Zero Point sprang into being. Countless realities originated from The Zero Point, each its own universe, with its own multiverse - and as a whole, an Omniverse. In real life, this event is known as the 'Big Bang'. The location of The Zero Point is Reality Zero, the origin reality.

Rise of the Last Reality[]

However, the complete nothingness that preceded the Omniverse had a will of its own. Wishing to return everything to its original state where only it exists, it started to predate the Zero Point and The Omniverse. For this, it gathered an alien army from an unknown empire having conquered the Last Reality, empowered them with forces from other worlds such as Cubes and chose and empowered additional individuals such as The Herald, the whole forming The Army of the Last Reality. Under its orders they began to annihilate one reality after another, all while searching for the Zero Point. Over time the army has grown so large that it has become one of the worst threats in the Omniverse.

Geno and the Imagined Order[]

Geno discovers Reality Zero as well as The Zero Point. Envisioning a threat to the integrity of the Omniverse and obsessed with perfection, he founded the Imagined Order in order to protect and perfect all realities by trying to figure out the perfect organizational system for the perfect civilization. In the process, he enslaved the Zero Point in order to use it to access all Realities but also to face against the unknown threat.

The Ice Moon[]

On the Ice Moon, The Ice Kingdom, led by The Ice King, travelled across realities with allies to collect powerful technology, artifacts, and beings too dangerous to exist in order to protect The Omniverse. However, when The Ice King's staunchest comrade argued to use their collected items to alter realities to their liking, The Ice King froze him and all his knights in his fortress. The Ice King brought The Ice Moon to Reality Zero, a reality he believed safe, before freezing himself to sleep forever, leaving The Ice Moon to orbit Reality Zero.

Misinterpretations

Misinterpretations[]

  • The term "Metaverse", which refers to Fortnite as a platform akin to ROBLOX, is mistakenly used by many to refer to the Omniverse.
  • Following a statement of The Foundation "Each its own universe... Its own Multiverse." in Batman/Fortnite: Foundation, a part of the community believed that the statement meant that that each Reality has a Multiverse inside of it, and thus differ from Marvel and DC model. However, it has been proved several time that this interpretation was wrong.
    • In the very same comic book this statement was made, The Batman Who Laughs stated that the World Forge forged Realities.[35] In DC Comics it's explained that the World Forge actually forged each Earths of DC Multiverse and then from the DC Dark Multiverse. DC Multiverse's Prime Earth was also revealed to be Fortnite's Reality: NF-1935.
    • In Fortnite X Marvel: Zero War, Fortnite's Reality: 616 was later revealed to be Marvel's Earth-616 belonging itself to the Marvel Multiverse.
      • It must be noted that for both DC and Marvel, Earths designates Realities. Since Marvel, DC and Fortnite share the same definition of what is a Reality, a Multiverse can't be inside a Reality in Fortnite.
    • In Fortnite: Save the World Timeless Questlines, Dr. Vinderman Books to terminate Realities explain that Realities and Timelines are the same, and that a corrupted Reality may infect all adjacent Realities of a same Multiverse, which could lead to the end of that same Multiverse. Contradicting the interpretation again.
    • It can be concluded that The Foundation likely meant that each Reality may belong to a Multiverse.
