Overview

This article is about monsters in Save the World. For monsters in Battle Royale, see Monsters (Battle Royale). For unused monsters in Save the World, see Unused Monsters (Save the World).

Monsters are the primary antagonists in Fortnite: Save the World.

Overview

Overview[]

Monsters have a variety of different behavior patterns, but their main actions are damaging players, Wildlife, Buildings, and Mission objectives. They spawn from The Storm during objective defense stages, Encampments, Storm Chests, etc, but can also be found sleeping around the zone during Missions. When sleeping, they will be awoken if a player approaches them, attempts to loot nearby containers, harvests objects, or attacks them or other nearby monsters.

Starting in mid-Plankerton, Monsters can become Elemental. Elemental Monsters have strengths and weaknesses, such as increased Damage Resistance to certain Weapon elements, etc. In earlier versions of the game, their homeworld is an unused hub called Limbo.

Types

Types[]

There are two primary types of Monsters that spawn during missions: Husk Monsters and Mist Monsters.

Husk Monsters[]

Main Article: Husk Monsters

Husks make up the majority of the enemies encountered in Save the World. They deal low to moderate damage and have low to moderate amounts of health. They come in ten different varieties with different appearances. All are divided into three distinct AI-attack patterns.

Mist Monsters[]

Main Article: Mist Monsters

Mist Monsters are rarer than Husks and tend to have higher health and deal higher damage. They have more varied appearances and attack patterns than regular Husks. Mist Monsters except the Flinger don't attack the objective directly, but instead focus on allowing other Monsters to do so. They come in four varieties:

Boss Monsters[]

Main Article: Mini-Bosses

Epic Mini-Bosses are more powerful than average Husks and come with a random set of buffs and modifiers. They appear in various game modes, including Mutant Storm Missions, Endurance, Wargames, Ventures, Dungeons, Hit the Road, Frostnite, and Survive the Horde. These Mini-Bosses are distinguished by their bright purple glow and are marked on the minimap by a white skull icon. Defeating a Mini-Boss guarantees the drop of various Crafting Materials and a Supply Cache that may vary in type and rarity. Below are the possible variants of Epic Mini-Bosses:

Storm King[]

Main Article: Storm King

The Storm King is the second Boss in Save the World that was introduced in Update v7.00, having been in development for over a year. His attack patterns include summoning Husks, firing a powerful laser, lifting and throwing large rocks, and summoning meteors. It was announced that players who defeat the Storm King by January 1st 2019 will receive a Storm King pin as a reward.

The Storm King was added at the end of the Canny Valley Campaign and can be fought during and after the Battle Royal Quest.

A stronger version of the Storm King known as the Mythic Storm King was added in Update v11.20. He can be found at the end of Twine Peaks. Defeating him is the only way of unlocking Schematics from the Storm King Weapons Set.

Main Article: Mythic Storm King

Hyde[]

Main Article: Hyde

Hyde is the first human Boss that was introduced to Save the World in Update v18.20. He only appears and can be battled in The Lab Dungeon. He wields a Husk Grinder and throws fire and ice flasks to combat players who attempt to thwart his inhumane scheme. He possesses high damage resistance (or Armor) that can only be weakened by depositing 2 BluGlo to each the six coils around the Boss room.

Unlike the Storm King and his Mythic counterpart who can be fought at anytime after being unlocked, Hyde, along with Dungeons as a whole, is available for a limited time each year, specifically during the Flannel Falls and Hexsylvania Venture Seasons.

Corrupt Vending Machine[]

Main Article: Corrupt Vending Machine

Corrupt Vending Machine is the first non-living monster and Boss that was introduced to Save the World in Update v30.40. It only appears and can be battled in the In-Vending Doom Mission. It generates a shield around itself while throwing pink Slurp Juices that spawn a poisonous cloud on impact. The only way to get rid of the shield around the Corrupt Vending Machine is to destroy the pipes around the map.

Unlike the Storm King and his Mythic counterpart who can be fought at anytime after being unlocked, the Corrupt Vending Machine, along with Outlandish Oddities as a whole, is available for a limited time each year, specifically during the Blasted Badlands Venture Season.

Monster Effects

Monster Effects[]

Monsters can be affected by Weapons and Hero Perks in many ways, causing them to have certain effects. These are the various effects can be inflicted on a Monster:

Effect Description
Afflicted When afflicted, the enemy takes X% damage over time until the effect wears off, usually after 6 seconds.
Slowed When slowed, the enemy's movement speed and attack speed is slowed, and shackles are applied on its feet for a few seconds.
Snared When slowed, the enemy's movement speed is reduced for a few seconds.
Stunned When an enemy is stunned, it causes them to not react for a short amount of time, causing stars to appear around its head.
Knocked When an enemy is knocked back or down, it cannot react for a period for a brief period of time.
