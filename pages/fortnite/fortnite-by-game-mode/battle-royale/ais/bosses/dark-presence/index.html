The Dark Presence, also known as the Demon Master, was the demon lord of Spirit Realm that was first introduced in Chapter 6: Season 1. He was the leader of the Demon Warriors and the master of Shogun X, The Night Rose, Mask Maker Daigo, the Spires, and The Swarm.

Due to the danger he posed to the Omniverse, especially after acquiring and mastering one of the Seven Shards of the Zero Point, a large-scale operation uniting heroes and villains from countless realities of the Omniverse was launched to destroy him once and for all.

He was a major antagonist in Fortnite: Battle Royale and LEGO Fortnite, serving as the main antagonist of Chapter 6 and LEGO Fortnite: Expeditions. His body was destroyed in Zero Hour event and he was later "decommissioned".

Overview

Overview[]

The Dark Presence was a very powerful demonic entity who resided in the Spirit Realm, often described as the darkness, the malevolent force, the monster, the creature and many others, though his true nature remained unknown. He possessed enough power to bind Shogun X, The Night Rose, and Daigo to his will, while commanding the Spires, the Swarm, and a vast demonic army. The demon was also aware of the Spirit Siblings—Daigo, Kendo, and Jade—and their bloodline, which descended from the original Mask Maker who forged the Masks of Power. These masks, now wielded by demons, played a central role in igniting the Shattering War many ages ago.

Personality

Personality[]

The Dark Presence is described as malice and ambition incarnate,[3] a Hungry Shadow and as a being full of rage and pride.[4] According to The Night Rose, her former captor is deadly and patient when it want to be, but volatile and vicious otherwise.[5] The Dark Presence intends to anchor the Spirit Realm to Oninoshima and unleash a march of destruction. He doesn't care about his servants, discarding them once they fullfilled their purpose, like he did with Shogun X who was sacrificed to make Daigo follow a dark path, then Mask Maker Daigo himself after the ritual for his summoning was successful, by throwing him onto a mountain.

Appearance

Appearance[]

The Dark Presence manifests as a towering, semi-humanoid eldritch horror creature with four tentacle-like arms—two matching his proportions and two smaller. Each hand ends in four fingers. His torso is heavily muscled, marked at the center by a huge suction hole with a magical star-shaped emblem on top where he embedded the Zero Point Shard.

His head retains a vaguely humanoid shape, crowned with what seem to be cracked mask with four horns, and lined with sharp, oni fangs. He has two pairs of glowing purple eyes.

The rest of his form is entirely composed of tentacles: roughly ten extend from the lower portion of his body and function as legs, while many others sprout from his head, like writhing hair, and from his back. There are a total of 6 big tentacles on his back and one of them still has the visible damage from Super Showdown event.

These tentacles are deep purple, etched with spiral patterns reminiscent of those seen on Cube Monsters, Mist Monsters, and objects tied to the Storm and the Corruption Cubes. Embedded within them are luminous yellow eyes with vertical or star-shaped pupils in various sizes. The tendrils emit a vibrant purple radiance, traversed by pulsing blue veins that never cease their motion.

History

History[]

Before Chapter 6[]

The Dark Presence bound Shogun X to his will in the Spirit Realm, and therefore became the leader of the Demon Warriors. Then through his new servant, he also bound The Night Rose for her to act as the former's lieutenant.

Chapter 6: Season 1[]

A Shard of the Zero Point crashes onto Oninoshima, closely behind The Looper, Jones and Hope who had arrived from other reality. Upon its impact, it created a Reality Wave that brought back once-banished Magic, including the dark magic which caused the Shattering War a long time ago. As a result, the previously abandoned portals are reopened for the demons of old to return across the Island[8] - including Shogun X, the Demon Warriors' commander.

The demon retrieved the Shard and brought it into the Spirit Realm, giving it to the Dark Presence for the preparation of his plan. For reasons unknown, the entity has a particular interest in the bloodline responsible for the Masks of Power, and wants to claim all of them.

Once Jade was captured and imprisoned in the Spirit Realm, she encountered the being with the Shard, and learned that he wanted to take the Island.

The Confrontation Event[]

While Daigo was carrying out his ritual to save Jade, he discovered the existence of the entity. The darkness contacted him, whispering that he knew of him, his family, his lineage, and wanted to take him. After the Confrontation, which resulted in the defeat of Shogun X and Jade's liberation, Daigo went on a quest to singlehandedly fight the being.

Chapter 6: Season 2[]

Jade informs The Looper that Daigo became obsessed with the Dark Presence of the Spirit Realm,[9] while The Night Rose warns that his presence is growing in the heart of Oninoshima.[10]

This led Daigo to follow a dangerous path, first seeking to repair the dangerous, old Mask of Power dating back to the Shattering War era. His goal was to use its power to forcefully open a passage to the Spirit Realm through a portal, enter it, defeat the entity and take the Zero Point Shard.

Daigo's Fate Event[]

Daigo allied himself with Fletcher Kane, to whom he gave Masks of Power for his Alpha Guards and promised ultimate power in exchange for the gold he needed to repair the old mask. Once done, the duo went to Magic Mosses to place and open a portal, with Daigo performing a ritual while Fletcher Kane protected him from any interference. Kendo and Jade expressed their shock at Daigo's actions, while The Night Rose understood that he fell to Dark Presence's thrall.

While Fletcher Kane was defeated by The Looper, Daigo betrayed him and abandoned him to his fate, calling it pragmatism, as only he could travel to the Spirit Realm. However, Daigo's arrogance and recklessness cost him dearly as the Dark Presence, through another Puppeteer, corrupted his heart and mind, transforming him into a fearsome demon under the name Mask Maker: the new commander of the Demon Warriors.

Chapter 6: Season 3 / LEGO Fortnite:Expeditions[]

Controlling Daigo's heart and mind, the Dark Presence charged him with finding and excavating the ancient Spires, and deploying them to absorb the energy of Oninoshima during a new invasion, all in order to progressively anchor the Spirit Realm to the Island and pursue his goal: the destruction of this world. In the process, the Storm King's minions also joined the Dark Presence's army.

Later in the season, Hope senses waves of raw rage, pride and malice coming from the dark presence. She informs that the presence is mixing his energies with the Zero Point Shard.

Super Showdown[]

Daigo performed his ritual, aiming to achieve what he viewed as his ultimate destiny and summoned his master before being violently discarded; The Supernovas arrive at Demon's Domain, ready to fight the creature. The tentacle proceeds to unleash a barrage of the Zero Point Shard's power on everyone. After the battle, the defeated tentacle retreats back into the portal.

The heroes think they've won, however three additional tentacles emerge from the portal, notably stronger than before. With their energy pulse, the tentacles lock everyone in the air, while two of them converged their power towards the third, ready to launch a super-powerful attack. However, the team's hope returns as Superman intervenes, cutting the third tentacle that was going to attack, letting it crash far to the ground, stopping for a brief moment to greet The Looper before attacking another tentacle with his red laser and delivering a powerful blow to the last remaining, sending them back to the Spirit Realm. He then destroyed the Spires to close the portal for good, and left.

The cut off tentacle begins to rot, boiling from the inside and spreading its blood into the ground surrounding the area. Strange insect holes emerge from the blood of the tentacle, which expel small, purple, glowing bugs.

Chapter 6: Season 4[]

As an unplanned contingency, the severed Tentacle of the Dark Presence gave birth to Onimorphs specimen of The Swarm, an invasive species of insectoid demons from the Spirit Realm, and began infesting Oninoshima. However, following the multiple conflicts the Island faced, the veil separating the Island with the Spirit Realm was weakened, allowing the Dark Presence to send more of them from the Spirit Realm in order to infest Oninoshima and shatter the veil for good.

After months of bug infestation, the Islanders managed to regain the upper hand, with the Dino Megazord finally crushing the remaining Queens in a decisive battle at The Hive, ending the Swarm War. The island was restored—former hives became peaceful sites like Bugswatter Beach, commemorating the conflict.

However, Dr. Theodore Flyesbee noted that The Swarm wasn't actually defeated but that they retreated, before adding they given how they were on their way to destroy Reality: 783 it wouldn't make sense for them to leave unless something even more dangerous than them would arrive soon, prompting him to leave this world. As several member of O.X.R. and of Hope's group could tell, it was only the calm before the storm as the Dark Presence was preparing his entry onto the island.

Welcome, Our Alien Overlords[]

Some time later, a mysterious anomaly taking the form of a tree appeared on the northwestern corner of Oninoshima, eventually growing into a large tree outfitted with a treehouse. Over time, a mysterious dark aura appeared over the treehouse, progressively worsening the instabilities of the veil separating the Island from the Spirit Realm.

While Hope sent The Looper to study the anomaly, she and Jones prepared a countermeasure to permanently seal the portals prior to their planned invasion of the Spirit Realm, with Jones enhancing his Portal Device to do so. When the instabilities reached their critical point, Hope and Jones rifted to a control center that had been set up nearby and proceeded to seal the breach and prevent the Dark Presence from using this opportunity to invade the Island. The portal was closed for a moment before reopening into a larger portal that finally allowed the demonic creature to cross over.

As a battle erupts, with the Dark Presence wielding the full power of the Zero Point Shard planted in his chest, Kang and Kodos are captivated by the sight of the creature as they have hoped to find a reproductive mate. They attack Islanders and abduct Hope and Jones to gain the demon's favor, but Hope overpowers them and gains control of their saucer to attack the Dark Presence. Her attack is parried and the saucer is thrown into an inter-reality wormhole by the Dark Presence, but Jones noticed the Zero Point Shard in the Dark Presence's chest, and Hope was able to use the tractor beam to steal a piece of it and take it into the wormhole.

Chapter 6: Mini Season 2[]

Zero Hour[]

The Dark Presence has corrupted a majority of Oninoshima with the central part of the island turned into broken wasteland. At the center of the storm, The Dark Presence emerges from the corrupted ruins of Battle's End to greet Hope and Jones with a menacing roar. The battle begins with Jones leading an omniversal army on to fight against the Dark Presence and his swarm of Onimorphs.

While the coalition was about to lose, Jones summoned Revin the Cube, hoping that the two evil threats would destroy each other. Believing him to be a new enemy, the Dark Presence directly attacked Revin, who retaliated, allowing the entire coalition to attack while he was stunned by Revin's continuous discharge.

However, while Revin was pushed back and defeated, about to explode and annihilate everything, Mecha Team Leader managed to return in the fight, and threw Revin right at the Dark Presence, using Revin's reality-annihilating properties when exploding to destroy the Dark Presence once and for all.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • The Dark Presence wasn't the original intended name of the Character.
    • This name was initially adopted by the community because of the description of the being made by Daigo near the end of Chapter 6: Season 1, referring to him as a "dark presence within the Spirit Realm".[11]
      • Following the popularity of the name, it was finally adopted as the official name by Epic Games during Chapter 6: Season 4, turning "dark presence" into a proper noun in all NPC dialogues.
    • Insider leaks released during Galactic Battle revealed he was known internally by Epic Games as the "Demon Lord".
    • Welcome, Our Alien Overlords and Chapter 6: Mini Season 2 Simpson Shorts refers to him as the The Demon and Demon Master instead.
      • They likely didn't had time to and to re-dub the event and the shorts prior to the release of the collaboration.
  • In terms of combat ability and destruction, the Dark Presence is the strongest villain ever to appear in Fortnite's history.
  • The Dark Presence's design seems to have been inspired by Cthulhu Mythos's Cosmic Entities such as Cthulhu, Azathoth or Shub-Niggurath.
  • The Dark Presence's role in Chapter 6 is similar to Pandora's Box's role in Chapter 5.
    • Both are omnipresent in their respective Chapters, but act indirectly, except during Live Events.
    • The entity seeks Daigo to find him and lead him toward a dark path, similar to how Pandora's Box manipulated Megalo Don and Doctor Doom to take its power in order to control them.
  • While initially referred to as "it" by The Night Rose,[3] Daigo reveals that the Dark Presence is a male.[12]
  • According to event's files, The Dark Presence NPC in Zero Hour's max health is 1,000,000,000.
  • The Dark Presence was once suspected to be the Emperor Palpatine, however the Teaser Trailer of Galactic Battle revealed in Star Wars Celebration LIVE! 2025 - DAY 3 contradicted this theory, as he traveled aboard the Death Star to Oninoshima.
  • It is believed that the Dark Presence has a connection with the Storm King:
    • In LEGO Fortnite:Expeditions, it is revealed that Mask Maker Daigo had both Spirit Realm and Storm King's beasts under his orders.
    • The Storm King demonstrated mind control abilities as well as telepathy, corresponding the powers displayed by the Dark Presence.
    • The Dark Presence was described as "malice and ambition" incarnate, as well as seeking to destroy worlds; this is similar to the Storm King.
    • The Dark Presence displays a spiral pattern and a purple color, as well as yellow eyes, like most Storm Monsters.

The Tentacles were hostile Bosses introduced during Chapter 6: Season 3. They could be found in Demon's Domain during the Super Showdown and Zero Hour Live Event.

Behavior

Behavior[]

The Tentacle will indiscriminately attack all players and Characters on sight. It randomly targets anybody that is within the arena.

The Tentacle itself has no weakpoint, but during the course of the fight, members of The Supernovas will come in and deal massive damage to it.

Attacks

Attacks[]

  • Zero Point Electric Blast
    • Fires three short electric blasts from its tentacle, dealing 10 damage to players and The Supernovas. 25 damage against environment, and 500 against player Buildings.
    • On impact, the blast briefly creates a big electrified field that deals 2 damage per mili-second if a target was hit.
  • Zero Point Energy Beam
    • Fires a large scorching beam that sweeps the arena twice, dealing 30 damage against players and The Supernovas, 3 damage against environment. and 100 damage against player Buildings.
  • Zero Point Energy Bomb
    • Hurls a big energy bomb down towards the arena, dealing 60 explosive damage against players and The Supernovas, and 500 against player Buildings.
  • Explosive Homing Spheres
    • Conjures five portals around itself, scattering small homing projectiles from the portals that implode on impact, dealing 50 explosive damage against players and The Supernovas, 25 damage against environment. and 350 damage against player Buildings.
  • Zero Point Energy Shockwave
    • The tentacle swiftly taps the ground once, then two more times. Unleashing an energy wave that sends all players flying upward on impact, dealing 300 damage to player Buildings while also ignoring The Supernovas.
  • Tentacle Slam
    • The tentacles slams itself on the ground, damaging nearby players.
