Overview

The Music Pass (known as the Festival Pass until Chapter 2 Remix) is a seasonal cosmetic item bundle in Fortnite Festival introduced in Chapter 5: Season 1, which grants the player rewards in exchange for XP (formerly Festival Points).

Music Passes can be purchased for 1,200 V-Bucks (formerly 1,800 V-Bucks and 1,400 V-Bucks) or unlocked with Fortnite Crew.

The Music Pass is structured similarly to the Battle Pass and other Fortnite Crew passes, with a single rewards track that contain both free and premium items. Like the other passes, Music Pass items are not exclusive to the season in which they are offered and may be added to the Item Shop sometime after the season ends, though players are unable to access Music Passes from past seasons.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Prior to Season 7, the Music Pass used the same layout as Event Passes introduced in Find the Force.
  • The Microphone is the only instrument type that has not yet been featured as one of the last 3 rewards in any free or paid tier Music Pass track.
    • It's also the only one to not be the final reward of a free or paid Music Pass track.
  • Prior to Season 6, the Music Pass was called the Festival Pass.
    • At the time, the paid track cost 1,800 V-Bucks, this was lowered to 1,400 V-Bucks.
    • Each reward tier cost 1,000 Festival Points, up to 11,000 Festival Points total.
      • This was replaced with each reward tier requiring 160,000 XP, up to 1,760,000 XP total.
    • The Festival Pass was only listed when Fortnite Festival was selected as a game mode, however with Update v32.00 this changed so that the Music Pass would appear regardless of what mode was selected.
  • Since December 1st 2024, the Music Pass has been included as part of Fortnite Crew. Access to the Premium reward track is only available while a subscription is active.[1]
