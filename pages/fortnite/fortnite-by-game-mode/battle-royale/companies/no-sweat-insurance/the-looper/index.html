Overview[]

“

— The Looper[18]

The Looper (also known as Hero or Outlaw) is the player character in Fortnite: Battle Royale and a central figure in the events of Reality Zero. Once a human agent of The Army of the Last Reality, they were a skilled operative sent on an unknown mission by Kymera. At some point in the distant past, they became trapped within The Loop during Season 1, resulting in the loss of their memories and identity.[11]

Over time, The Looper developed awareness within The Loop and began interacting with other Loopers, gradually carving out a reputation in the Island's underworld. Despite their mysterious past, they consistently sided with heroes such as Jones and The Seven, with whom they became acquaintances and close friends, against major threats. This led them to ultimately becoming one of Reality's most influential defenders.

After the end of Chapter 4, The Looper began traveling to new realities, following and assisting their old friend, Jones and his mentee, Hope in their adventures to protect Reality.

They are the main protagonist of Fortnite, being the main protagonist of LEGO Fortnite and the overarching protagonist of Fortnite: Battle Royale serving as:

  • A minor character during Chapter 1.
  • The main protagonist of Chapter 2.
  • The deuteragonist of Chapter 3.
  • The main protagonist of Chapter 4.
  • One of the three main protagonists of Chapter 5 alongside Hope and Jones.
  • One of the main protagonists of Chapter 6 alongside Hope, Jones, The Night Rose, Midas and the Spirit Siblings.
  • One of the three main protagonists of Chapter 7 alongside Hope, Jones and The Seven.
Summary

Summary[]

The Looper is a powerful and skilled human warrior from The Army of the Last Reality sent on a mission by Kymera in Reality Zero a long time ago for an unknown mission[11]. But after entering The Loop, they forgot everything. After their memory loss, The Looper became merely one of the many other loopers trapped in the The Loop, having no awareness of The Loop, they apparently remained a simple observer during the whole first Chapter.

From Chapter 2, The Looper starts to act as a mercenary and begins to be noticed by important characters and organizations[19], becoming a precious ally or a threat for them. Their various heroic actions over time as well as their prowess in battle have earned them the respect of both allies and enemies alike, as well as receiving the title of "Hero", making them a central figure of The Island.

Unlike Jones who has wandered from his path [20], The Looper seems to have a predetermined fate that they follow, as it was prophesied eons ago that they would confront and dethrone Zeus, before facing a worse threat.

Throughout their journey, The Looper forged numerous alliances, beginning with Jones during the Zero Crisis, where their partnership grew into a profound mutual trust. Together, they later stood alongside The Seven, with The Looper gradually forming bonds with several of its members, most notably The Scientist, The Imagined, The Origin, as well as Paradigm and AMIE, the latter serving as their partner until the end of Chapter 4: Season 1.

In the seasons that followed, The Looper often operated independently, aside from brief collaborations with Jones during Chapter 4: Season 4 and Chapter 5: Season 1, or worked alongside outsiders such as Evie and The Oracle. Ultimately, however, they returned to Jones when he called upon them at Hope's request, reuniting with their longtime ally and resuming their shared mission to protect Reality, with Hope by their side.

Personality

Personality[]

The Looper is a skilled fighter with great combat experience however they're depicted as a cynical and sometimes world-weary person who often thinks out loud. They sometime show little respect for their hierarchy, showing a rather rebellious and nonchalant side, though once they're in a work, they do it fully.[31] As noted by Rick Sanchez they would do anything as long as they get paid and that they complete their missions, without ever questioning their orders, revealing its one of the reason the Imagined Order actively wanted to recruit them.[32]

The Looper frequently uses sarcasm and dry humor, often to undercut others or express frustration with their situation. They often talk with an informal, conversational tone. Despite their sarcasm, they have a softer side that occasionally peeks through, as shown with Noir and Sgt. Winter[33] they connect with others' emotions, even if they're reluctant[34] to fully express vulnerability and kindness.

Unlike the other loopers, it's shown they are calm, thoughtful and endowed with great integrity, showing abilities of analysis of the situation despite the lack of information notably during Wintervestigation, Demon's invasion and during Zero Crisis Finale[35].

The Looper demonstrates a sense of justice, rallying to factions and people wishing to protect the Island and showing great loyalty to others, however they are ruthless with those designated as their enemy or traitors, they feel no remorse to kill people, harm innocent people and and deceive their allies/friends if it's for their mission/greater good.[36] The Looper can also be manipulative, deceitful, and deceptive. They had some sort of alliance with a Kymera during Chapter 2: Season 7[37] and revealed the Imagined Order's plan to them.[38] After Slone's betrayal during Operation: Sky Fire, The Looper continued to take advantage of the Imagined Order's resources as a "member" while revealing compromising information to Charlotte.[39]

This is also the case in the Hope/Midas alliance where The Looper is both a long-time friend and ally of Hope, but at the same time a mercenary with ties to Midas, since his escape from the Underworld, and then the Fletcher Kane affair. The Looper hid their connection to Midas from Hope and pretended to be surprised at the reveal of Hope and Midas alliance, while acting on Midas' behalf, lying to their friends to get information the group didn't want to give Midas, while pinning the blame on Skillet, who Midas himself thinks the information is coming from. This phase of their personality fits well with their two most used nicknames: Outlaw and Hero. They nevertheless remain fully loyal to their friends.

Despite being noted to be calm, The Looper is known to other characters for their unpredictable and explosive fighting style[40] in stark contrast to their personality, as well as their thirst for challenge and adventure[41], being completely unafraid of risks according to Odyssey[42].

Unknown Time Period[]

Very little is known about The Looper's past, however it is known that they were one of the few human affiliated with The Last Reality, they had befriended a Trespasser. Before arriving in The Loop, they received instructions from Kymera however when entering The Loop they lost their memory about their mission and everything else.[73]

Pre-Season[]

The Looper is one of the first Loopers trapped inside The Loop having existed since the beginning of the game's life cycle, but doesn't properly get involved with forces outside of The Loop until much later.

Season 1 - Season X[]

The Looper witnessed all the events that occurred on the Island, however their involvement is unknown. It is just as possible that they acted as it is possible that they remained only simple observer.

Chapter 2: Season 1[]

The Looper witnessed the black hole and the creation of Apollo, intercepting secret communication from The Imagined Order, indicating the Redacted: D4 team's deployment. Their skills and abilities as a looper began to be recognized and they gained notoriety, drawing attention to them.

Chapter 2: Season 2[]

The Looper is recruited as a secret agent by The Agency after being highly recommended.[74] During The Device Event, The Looper goes into The Bridge, and meets Agent Jones, but they are quickly sent back to the Apollo Island. This marks the Looper's first known interaction with someone outside of The Loop. After that The Looper began to become more aware of The Loop and their behavior changed, deciding to search for answers.

Chapter 2: Season 3[]

After GHOST's defeat during Chapter 2: Season 2, The Looper takes refuge at The Fortilla with other members and will continue the war against SHADOW.

Chapter 2: Season 4[]

During Fortnitemares: Midas' Revenge, eliminated Loopers were turned into an undead form, known as Shadows by Shadow Midas. This is a result of Shadow Midas hacking the Spawn Drones so they do not pick up the body and fly off as usual, instead spawn the eliminated Looper as a Shadow.

GHOST and SHADOW went dark and The Looper therefore ceases their activities as a secret agent. During The Devourer of Worlds Live Event, Tony Stark saved them following the destruction of Helicarrier 64 and placed them in one of the millions of Battle Buses loaded with Gamma Bombs. They finally send Galactus back to Reality: 616 where Thor would kill him off in the main continuity. The reason for Tony's interest in The Looper is however unclear, especially since it seems that he did not need them, because all the Buses were on autopilot. During that time, The Looper was observed by the Imagined Order.[75]

Chapter 2: Season 5[]

The Looper becomes a bounty hunter, and they work with the Characters on the Island and the other Bounty Hunters. The Looper demonstrated that they could actually speak in The Loop as well as keep their memories, Bunker Jonesy gradually gave them the clues to escape from The Loop. By working with the Hunters, like Lexa, and by interrogating the IO Guards, they were able to learn more about the current situation and about the Imagined Order.

Chapter 2: Season 6[]

During the Zero Crisis Finale, they managed to make their way through the Loopers who were killing each other and helped The Foundation and Agent Jones to fix The Zero Point and save reality. After Zero Crisis, The Looper decided to continue helping Jones and The Foundation, they began to hunt for anomalies.[76] Later, they were contacted by Tarana[77] who found a message from Jones for them asking to "Listen to the Crystal Song". Since the recording concerns The Spire, Raz decides to steal it and help The Looper in order to serve his interests. He helped The Looper infiltrate The Spire in order to decipher the message of the crystals. This message comes from The Foundation and asks The Looper to find Jones.

Both Raz and The Looper searched for Jones, talking to several of his Snapshots to find the Jones that The Looper encountered during Zero Crisis Finale. Some of them revealed some secrets about The Loop notably about the lose of memory and the snapshot phenomenon as well as their mission when coming here, but others warned that Jones was doomed and that they could only get help from "The First Jones". Once finding Jonesy The First, the later note that Jones asked the right person for help, speaking about The Looper. However, he wanted to test The Looper's strength first. After The Looper won the duel, Jonesy The First explained the truth behind The Spire. Saying that it will make people powerful, but that it will corrupt their minds. He told them to not let anyone powerful be corrupted by The Spire or Jones wouldn't stand a chance and to hurry to The Spire before it get more attentions from something or someone unspecified.

While The Looper was warned by Jonesy The First, Raz steals a fragment of the Spire's core and becomes Glyph Master Raz. Rebirth Raven, Lara Croft, and Jonesy the First warn that Raz's Power is attracting "unwanted attention" and ask The Looper to stop him and take The Spire artifact from him. They defeat Glyph Master Raz and collect The Spire Artifact, but Jonesy The First says that it's not the end.

At an unspecified time before The Last Reality's attack began, The Looper finally managed to escape The Loop.[78][79] They investigated damaged telescopes at the request of Bunker Jonesy, who suspected that people didn't wanted Islanders to watch the sky. Later, Farmer Steel contacted The Looper about a Downed Black Helicopter which crashed into his corn fields, after investigating The Looper managed to restore communication and heard "India Oscar field decon truck inbound, OPSEC level 3 is in effect, repeat, OPSEC 3 now a GO.", after revealing this to Farmer Steel, the latter confirm this is in fact the Imagined Order.

Bunker Jonesy later recontacted The Looper, about radio starting to ask him question, turns out to be someone named Mari, who used an authorized channel for her radio broadcast, in order to investigate the signs about aliens. Several interveners such as Turk from E.G.O and Dennis also intervened. However, Mari attracted the attention of the IO who ordered her to stop. However after her refusal, she was forced into silence by the IO during an altercation that everyone could hear on the radio, worrying Turk. Mari later returned, but announced she would put a pause to all of this. Around the same time, The Looper received a mission order whose sponsor was unknown. They were asked to terminate all unauthorized broadcast. During their mission, they found strange TV, with a logo, the same as that of the corn circle of Colossal Crops, worrying the sponsor of the mission.

Chapter 2: Season 7[]

Doctor Slone recruited The Looper as covert ops to resolve internal problems at the Imagined Order such as moles in the shadows. Slone sent them on missions ranging from recon, combat, infiltration, investigation, sabotage, classified data recovery etc...

During week 8, Slone sends The Looper to identify the infiltrator, the surprise was that this infiltrator was the Trespasser who had become friends with The Looper in the past, asking if The Looper remembered him after all this time. The thought they were playing comedy when they said no, until he realizes that they really forgot everything when The Looper accused him of being "one of them" to which he replied "Them? You mean Us?" confirming that The Looper was actually a member of The Last Reality. He says afterwards that they must give it time and that they will remember Kymera's directives.

During week 12, Slone contacts The Looper telling them that she believe that the counterattack plan has been compromised and summons them to decide on a backup plan together. This backup plan is revealed to be Operation: Skyfire, an operation that involves getting bombs inside the Mothership and detonating them. During Week 14, The Mole tries to trigger the Mothership Countermeasure Device earlier than expected and Doctor Slone sends The Looper to cancel the trigger. Slone discovers that the mole is Maven, she then sends The Looper to confront her and The Looper defeats her.

A few days before the Operation: Sky Fire Live event, Mari contacted the Looper to express her distrust of Doctor Slone as well as her concerns about the hypothesis that the Mothership contained something that even Slone wouldn't know about. She tells them to be very careful because "Whatever you think the worst thing ever is... This might be way way [interrupted transmission]."

Operation: Sky Fire[]

The Looper joined the Imagined Order strike team to infiltrate the Mothership to "deliver IO's final message". Collision[]

The Looper alongside 3 other loopers gather inside of the Mecha Strike Commander as the Mecha Weapons Team. They receive a mission briefing by The Origin. Afterwards, The Paradigm enters and initiates the launch sequence, with the Mecha Weapons Team entering the battle stations. The Mecha heads for Reality Zero, and the Mecha Weapons Team assist The Paradigm by destroying incoming asteroids and Abductor debris. The Mecha reaches Reality Zero, landing at Synapse Station, where they are greeted by battalions of the Imagined Order's tanks with shields. As the Loopers destroy the tanks, the Mecha is struck by a laser strike from The Fortress. In a quick time event, the Mecha fires a laser back at The Fortress, destroying the location. As the Mecha continues heading for The Collider, it is hit by a barrage of missiles coming from IO Airships, heavily damaging it and incapacitating it. With The Mecha needing repairs in order to move again, Agent Jones sends Peely in to deliver a Silky Tanker using a Mudflap. The Mecha is healed when it crushes the truck in its fist and The Paradigm pours the Slurp onto it, hurling the truck into an IO Airship when it finishes. Agent Jones and the Ground Team manages to take down the shield protecting The Collider, and The Mecha pulls out its Light Blade, slashing through the remaining IO airships. Once The Mecha reaches The Collider, The Paradigm diverts all power to the Light Blade, and prepares to destroy the Collider. However, the ground beneath The Mecha collapses, and the Mecha falls into the ground below as The Paradigm and the Loopers are ejected from the Mecha. However, they are attacked by Doctor Slone piloting a tank alongside IO guards and a B.R.U.T.E.. As the group fights their way towards the Collider, the Zero Point has been exposed, and it begins to rise towards the top of the Collider, shifting realities along the way. The group is stopped by Slone's tank, and as Jones distracts Slone with a speech, The Paradigm manages to get the Mecha back online, crushing Slone and her tank with the Mecha's fist, which destroys part of the bridge. The Paradigm then lifts the Loopers, Jones, and The Foundation upwards towards the top of the Collider, with the latter two jumping into the Zero Point when it focuses on Geno. The Loopers then destroy the crystal detonators, and the Zero Point falls back below. The Paradigm protects the Loopers as the Collider collapses.

He was then brought to the main base, where The Order and the others briefed him on the current situation. In return, The Visitor shared his own experiences — recounting his pursuit of the Dark Voyager and the events that had led him there. He then enlisted The Looper's help in improving the new base by testing hiding spots, clearing threats at the Canyon, and evaluating the new Seven gear he had supplied.

Interference[]

Not long after his arrival, The Visitor grew increasingly uneasy with The Order's approach to investigating the Dark Voyager. Concerned by her methods, he asked The Looper to speak with her and persuade her to reconsider. The Order, however, instructed The Looper to disregard The Visitor's concerns. Using Stealth Splash as camouflage, The Order and The Looper attempted to outmaneuver the Dark Voyager. During the operation, The Order contacted The Visitor again, urging him to assist if he was truly so worried about the plan. He refused, insisting that confronting the Dark Voyager directly was unwise — especially since they still did not fully understand his capabilities. The Order countered that they would never learn what he was capable of without conducting reconnaissance.

As they began sabotaging the Dark Voyager's work at Lethal Labs, The Visitor reached out once more, ordering The Order to stay away from the enemy. She refused, stating she did not take orders from him. In response, The Visitor reminded her that he had pursued the Voyager through the husks of burned-out stars and the ashes of collapsing realities and that if he were so easily defeated, he would have already done so. The Order retorted that they had only just met and that she did not know his capabilities, before cutting off communication.

Later, after The Order dispatched The Looper to investigate the Shards, The Visitor contacted them again. He accused The Order of endangering the mission through her hubris and impatience, remarking that she had become reckless — just like her sister. The Order replied that The Imagined would have understood the stakes and expressed surprise that he did not. The Visitor then asked whether she had learned nothing from losing her. She answered that she had learned one thing: when everything is on the line, it is better to be reckless than timid. The Order then instructed The Looper to await her next call.

First Wave[]

Hope informs The Looper that she's been receiving coded transmissions from The Elites, a team of skilled fighters which Daigo, her former frenemy, is now also a part of. She tasks the hero with scouting The Basement as a possible base for The Elites, also telling them that if they were to impress the team, they may join the heroes in the fight to come.

Meanwhile, aboard the Dark Harvester, the Dark Voyager has kidnapped Fishstick in an attempt to extort information about his homeworld which is home to the third Zero Point Shard needed to assemble the fractured Zero Point. After locating Reality: 7021 among the countless realities in his map, the Dark Voyager opens a rift to reach the planet housing Atlantis and its inhabitants. The Zero Point Shard is pulled by the Dark Harvester from the bottom of the ocean, causing the city to dry up and the fisherman floating above it to be extracted with the artifact. Once inside the ship, the Dark Voyager sends his Trespassers to remove the intruder who is angrily looking at the confused Fishstick.

The Dark Voyager quickly returns to Reality: 266 and extracts the third shard he acquired from his ship, now to be placed with the others at Lethal Labs. Hope starts to worry about the ease with which the Dark Voyager has been able to gather three Zero Point Shards with his ship, comparing it with all the challenges she had to go through to simply retrieve one.

Final Wave[]

As the Second Wave concludes with the third Zero Point Shard placed with the others, the Dark Voyager enacts his Final Wave, starting to charge the Zero Point Shards with the Dark Harvester energy beam. Once charged, the ship shoots a final blow strong enough to generate Zero Point tendrils and opening three shard distortions at Clawsy Lodge, Bumpy Bay and near Looper's Leap. After the blow, the Dark Harvester shortly loses energy, but its systems are seemingly rebooted by Void Walker Lynx through a small Zero Point splinter allowing her master to observe the position of the distortions[104].

The Looper is quickly sent by Hope to investigate the distortions, while Peelians' actors start to feel that the Golden Coast is approaching dark times.

Trivia

Trivia[]

General

  • The term "Looper" refers to anyone who was once trapped inside The Loop. Since the player character's real name has never been revealed, they are given various nicknames by the characters of the Island — the most common being "Looper".
    • Although "The Looper" was originally a fan-created name (in response to characters repeatedly calling them "Looper"), the LEGO Fortnite secrets books, The Looper Trilogy, canonized "The Looper" as the official name of the character.
  • The Looper appears as a first-person character in the trailers for Chapter 2: Season 1 [105] and Chapter 2: Season 2.[106]
  • While the Looper initially spoke very rarely outside of a few isolated interactions, this changed in Chapter 6. From then on, they were given dialogue bubbles during quests, allowing players to read the Looper's own thoughts. Conversations with other characters became more frequent, and The Looper's dialogue was given more personality, replacing the older, generic lines.

Lore

  • The Looper has taken on the roles of several mythological figures.
    • During Chapter 5: Season 2, it was revealed that they were fated eons ago to dethrone Zeus, playing the role of Zeus and Metis' unborn son from Greek mythology. By the end of the season, The Looper fulfilled this fate by dethroning Zeus.
      • In Greek mythology, Zeus, upon learning of the prophecy of his downfall, swallowed Metis to prevent it from happening.
      • In another version of the myth, it was the offspring of Zeus and Thetis who was destined to dethrone the king of the gods.
    • In Chapter 6: Season 1, The Looper played the role of Urashima Tarō, from the Japanese folktale. They aided The Great Turtle and were rewarded by Ryūji, who embodied both Ryūjin and Otohime, with a chest representing the tamatebako.
  • The Looper became so significant to the Islanders that someone wrote an entire trilogy of books about them in LEGO Fortnite.
  • Chapter 2: Season 5 revealed that The Looper could retain memories from previous Loops and even speak while inside the Loop.
    • It remains unknown when they first acquired this ability.
  • After discovering that they were once a member of The Last Reality but had no memory of it, The Looper chose to keep this secret, continuing to oppose The Last Reality's army while pretending nothing had happened.
    • To this day, they appear not to recall Kymera's directives — unless they are deliberately feigning ignorance, which could hint at a future betrayal.
      • It is, however, implied that they were the one who warned the Aliens about Operation: Sky Fire.[107]
    • Their immunity to Chrome may be connected to their past ties with The Last Reality, though another explanation is possible, since The Origin, the former Cube King, was not immune.
  • Some characters are aware of the quest system The Looper follows — such as Lexa, Raz, and Wrath (without breaking the fourth wall). Wrath's entry in the Character Collection Book even notes that he knows the dangers The Looper faces if they fail their quests.
  • Curiously, while nearly everyone else calls them "Looper," all of MEGA City's Syndicates refer to them as "Hero" — even before The Looper saved the city.
    • Later, during Chapter 5: Season 2, The Pantheon also adopted this title.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • The Looper is considered one of the most powerful fighters in the entire game.
    • They are frequently shown to be capable of wielding any weapon and piloting any vehicle with expert-level skill.
    • Their strength and agility are so extraordinary that The Oracle compares them to the heroes of Greek mythology.
    • In unused Chapter 2: Season 7 Week 12 dialogue, Doctor Slone describes the Looper as one of her very best Loopers, suggesting that they may be the greatest fighter in the Loop.
    • Both Lexa and Raz state that the Looper's abilities and knowledge could make them the true master of The Loop and The Island. This is especially significant because, at that time, the Loop was filled with extremely powerful combatants such as the Hunters (including Barry Allen, Kratos, Predator, and Master Chief), reinforcing the idea that the Looper ranks among the very strongest.
      • The Looper even managed to defeat and kill Glyph Master Raz, a fighter so powerful that his power attracted unwanted attention from an Omniversial threat. Notably, this was achieved while The Spire prevented the use of any form of technology.[1]
    • The Looper has fought and defeated gods, cosmic entities, and other formidable beings — including Cerberus, Ares, Zeus,[2] and The Herald[3] — entirely on their own. They also demonstrate immunity to psychic attacks and to Chrome, feats unmatched by any other character.
  • Despite these incredible abilities, there are clear irregularities in the Looper's combat performance, suggesting they may be deliberately holding back. This makes their true potential and power unknown.
    • This was noted especially during Death Star Sabotage with their fight against Emperor Palpatine along with Jones and the Strike Team, where they "couldn't do anything". In this same event, they withstood several deadly attacks, which nevertheless decimated almost the entire Strike Team, before having a fatal fall from the Death Star to the Island and then emerging completely unharmed.
  • The Looper's power is unusually high given that they are confirmed to be an ordinary mortal human by The Last Reality, IO technology,[4] and even the Gods themselves. Their superiority over beings such as deities and cosmic entities implies that the Looper may be concealing some deeper secret.
