Overview

This article is about a Mode available for a limited time only.

“
In Delulu, betrayal could make or break a player’s chance at a Victory Royale! Proximity voice chat was used to forge alliances on the fly—or to break them just as fast. Players were advised not to forget to watch their backs…
”
— In-Game Description

Delulu was a Zero Build Limited Time Mode in Fortnite that was introduced in Update v37.30.

Gameplay

Gameplay[]

Strategy

Strategy[]

This section outlines a strategy.
Feel free to add tips, tricks and general advice about the topic.
  • Communication was the core of Delulu, whether it was with your Squad or other players. Those who rarely communicated were more likely to attack you when you least expected it. As such, players were encouraged to eliminate those who did not have an open mic.
  • If players found a Supply Drop or a Chest that required multiple players, they could still use Chickens to open them.
  • The O.X.R. Rifle was the main long-range weapon of this mode, and players were advised to pick it up whenever possible.
  • Ammo could be tight on occasion in this mode, so players were advised to communicate with their team when they ran low.
  • Rifts of any kind, Launchpads, Shockwaves, and Grapplers were necessary for rotating and getting around. Players were encouraged to make sure these items were distributed to everyone on their team.
  • If players revealed they had a valuable item via mic, others were advised to consider either teaming with them or taking them out.
  • Delulu was a mode built around voice chat, and players were encouraged to use it. Getting to know teammates’ play styles and skill levels helped form strategies when they were needed. Players were advised to almost never take out a teammate unless it was absolutely necessary.
  • One way to win Delulu was to collect a large amount of white healing items throughout the match and then use them to out-heal the Storm’s damage once the safe zone completely disappeared. Players were advised to stay away from enemy players, however, as there was never any guarantee they would choose friendship over an attack.
    • This strategy worked especially well with Floppers, Chug Jugs, and Med-Mist Smoke Grenades.
      • Because Floppers were much easier to obtain, players were advised to fish at the pond near Carmine Lodge or in the river under Hiker’s Haven. It was also important to remember that a fishing hole was not required to obtain Floppers—as long as there was water, they could be caught by fishing.
      • Players were encouraged to search every chest they could find for Chug Jugs and Med-Mist Smoke Grenades. The latter could also be found as floor loot, but it was not very common.
    • An optimal loadout for a Delulu heal-off play was as follows:
      1. Shockwave Grenades/Rift-To-Go/Lawless Rift Launcher: Since opponents were likely to fight, a quick way to escape an unintended encounter was necessary to avoid elimination
      2. Med-Mist Smoke Grenade(s)/Floppers: These items were most optimal for the heal-off, as they allowed players to hide from enemies if they tried to shoot them. Additionally, the health they provided could stack with other healing items in case players did not have enough health.
      3. Chug Jug/Floppers: Chug Jugs were unique for their ability to recover both health and shield, rather than just one aspect of HP. If players were lucky enough to find one, it was recommended to save it for the heal-off. Beforehand, it should only be used if they lacked a significant amount of shield and there were no other shield items nearby.
      4. Friend Zone Bubble: If players were unable to escape hostile opponents, this item could and would protect them from receiving any kind of damage as long as they remained within its radius. They were advised to try to make amends with whoever was attacking them while it was active and to be watchful of the bubble itself for the flashing effect signaling the end of its duration.

Chapter 6: Season 4[]

Update v37.30[]

  • Introduced.
  • September 18th 2025 Hotfix:
    • Added Common rarity of the Flint-Knock Pistol.

Update v38.00[]

  • Moved Delulu to Springfield Island.

Update v38.10[]

  • Added Friend Zone Bubble.
  • Added Changing Booths.
  • Added a Jam Track player, allowing players to play Jam Tracks in full without starting a Jam Circle.
  • Once a match reaches the final 20 players, a random number will now be chosen that determines how many players can earn a Victory Royale.
November 21st 2025
  • The Battle Bus has been enabled.

Update v39.10[]

  • Moved Delulu to the Golden Coast.
  • When a player gets eliminated, they now turn into a ghost that can updoot alive players.

  • Delulu was made available every day during Winterfest 2025.

  • Delulu returned to a weekends-only mode.
January 19th 2026
  • Removed Delulu.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Delulu is short for "delusional".
  • Creators in the Support-A-Creator Program were given early access to Delulu on September 18th 2025.
  • Prior to release, Delulu was also known as "Party Crashers".
  • Delulu was originally going to be available for only two weekends: September 19th and September 26th 2025.
    • With the launch of the KPop Demon Hunters collaboration, Delulu was extended until the end of 2025.
    • Delulu was extended into 2026 with the BLEACH collaboration.
    • Delulu's final weekend was held on the weekend of January 16th 2026.
  • The Legacy Bandage is only available by shooting the Bandage Bazooka and any surface.
