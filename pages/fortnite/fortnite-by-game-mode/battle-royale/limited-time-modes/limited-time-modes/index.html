Overview

This article is about limited-time gamemodes. For the permanent modes, see Core Gamemodes.

Limited Time Modes, or LTMs for short, are a type of playlist available in Fortnite. They are modes that were only intended to be available for a limited amount of time; they do not include permanent modes that were later shut down.

Some LTMs are available for a pre-determined fixed set of time before they become inaccessible (for example, the Endgame LTM was only available for 2 weeks). Other LTMs are rotational, meaning that they are only accessible during certain periods (for example, Horde Rush is available during every Fortnitemares); these are known as Core LTMs due to their continued availability.

Most LTMs are based in Battle Royale and its adjacent modes, applying various gameplay changes to the mode (such as changing the lootpool to only spawn Legendary weapons). However, some LTMs provide a unique gameplay experience that is not based in Battle Royale, such as Crown Jam.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Chapter 1 and 2 introduced the majority of LTMs, adding nearly 100 collectively. In comparison, Chapter 4 introduced the least amount of LTMs, at just 1.
  • Several items are exclusive to LTMs; for example, the Revive Grenade has only ever been accessible in the Bodyguard LTM. Some of these items were later added to Fortnite: Creative, such as the Dual Suppressed Pistols.
  • Since Chapter 6, Power Hours have served as a replacement to Battle Royale-based LTMs.
  • Several LTMs have been turned into permanent modes:
    • Team Rumble was originally an LTM, but it was incorporated as a core gamemode in Season 8.
    • Trios was originally an LTM (and as a permanent Arena mode), but it was incorporated as a permanent mode as of Chapter 2: Season 5.
    • Blitz Royale was originally supposed to be an LTM that was in-game for 4 weeks. This was then extended by 4 weeks, before later being extended again indefinitely (making it a core gamemode).
