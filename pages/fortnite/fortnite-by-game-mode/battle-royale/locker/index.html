Overview

The Locker is a Lobby feature in Fortnite. It is where the Player can manage and equip their owned Cosmetic Items. Some types of Cosmetic Items may not be available to use in all Game Modes. Players can access the Locker in-game with the Changing Booth, only in Creative.

Overview

Overview[]

  • The Locker is divided into different sections. Each section contains unique and related types of Cosmetic Items that can be selected for use, and are organized in the following order:
    • Character: Outfits, Back Blings, Pickaxes, Gliders, Kicks, Contrails and Auras;
    • Emotes: Emotes;
    • Sidekicks: Sidekicks;
    • Wraps: Wraps;
    • Lobby: Banner Icons, Lobby Music and Loading Screens;
    • Cars (Sports Car and SUV / Truck): Car Bodies, Decals, Wheels, Trails and Boosts;
    • Instruments: Guitars, Bass, Drums, Microphones and Keytars;
    • Music: Jam Tracks: Jam Tracks / Moments: Intro Music and Celebration Music;
    • LEGO® Kits: Builds and Decor Bundles.
  • Some types of Cosmetic Items can be equipped in place of another type, mostly because they function similarly, lack a unique type, or simply do not have their own slot in the Locker. The latter often occurs because Cosmetics from that type either replace the main one or can be accessed in the same way in-game. Most of these "secondary" types can be filtered through the Sort Filter menu. Below, Cosmetics are listed as the main cosmetic type and the other types that can also be equipped in that same main type's slot.
    • Back Bling: Pets;
    • Emote: Emojis, Sprays and Toys;
    • Lobby Music: Jam Tracks;
    • Intro Music: Lobby Music and Jam Tracks;
    • Celebration Music: Lobby Music and Jam Tracks.
Features

Features[]

The Loadouts Interface
The Presets Interface
  • Loadouts are a combo of all currently equipped cosmetics saved under a single bundle. It can store up to 200 different Loadouts. Options for viewing and saving Loadouts can be found at the bottom right of the Locker. Aside from equipping, they can be created, renamed, overrided, and deleted. LEGO® Kits cannot be equipped and, therefore, cannot be part of a Loadout.
  • Presets allow the player to store different combos of cosmetics from specific sections of the Locker, so the player can quickly change them without having to manually select each one. Each section (except for LEGO® Kits, which cannot have Presets) can store up to 100 Presets. The Presets can be created, renamed, overrided and deleted.
  • Favoriting can be done in order to make a selected variety of cosmetics stand out in the Locker and/or to filter what items can be equipped when selecting to Random a cosmetic type. The Player can view all favorited cosmetics by selecting "FAVORITES" in the filters. Favoriting or unfavoriting can be done by opening the three-dot drop-down menu on a cosmetic and selecting "Favorite Item"/"Unfavorite Item". LEGO® Kits cannot be favorited.
  • Cosmetics can be Archived, meaning that they will no longer be viewable in the Locker. They can be viewed and equipped again by selecting "ARCHIVED" in the filters. Archiving favorited cosmetics will automatically un-favorite them. Archived cosmetics are excluded from the random cosmetic selection. Archiving or unarchiving can be done by opening the three-dot drop-down menu on a cosmetic and selecting "Archive Item"/"Unarchive Item". LEGO® Kits cannot be archived.
  • Inside the Locker, it is possible to search for Cosmetics by typing their name in the Search Bar. It can also be used as a filter, by typing their series, set, specific type (E.g.: knife; axe; bat), chapter and season of release, and many others. There is also a Sort Filter option that allows to change the way Cosmetics are sorted and filtered. It is possible to sort by Latest Acquired, Sets, A-Z, Series and Season, and filter by All, Recent, Favorites, Archived and others depending on the type of Cosmetic Item. Some of these options are not available for specific types. An option to Hide Unsuported Items (such as Age Restricted Cosmetics for the current experience or cosmetics that only appears when equipped along a specific Outfit) is also present.
    • Some cosmetic types can be filtered by specific and exclusive categories. E.g.: Selectable Styles, Unlockable Styles, Enlightened, Reactive, Built-In Emote and Kicks Usable for Outfits; Emotes, Traversal, Group, Emojis, Sprays and Toys for Emotes; Styles and Reactive for Pickaxes; and more.
  • Some Cosmetics are Age Restricted, meaning they have an age rating higher than E or E10+ (or regionally equivalent). If a Player equips one of these Items and joins an Island below the designated rating threshold (E or E10+), their equipped Item will be replaced with one that's compatible with the Island's rating. For example, their Outfit will appear as a default Outfit.
  • The Changing Booth is a Creative Device that allows players to change their cosmetics while in-game. Despite reflecting the Locker, the Cars, Jam Tracks, and LEGO® Kits tabs cannot be seen, and thus their respective cosmetics cannot be viewed or changed while using it.
Cosmetic Compatibility

Cosmetic Compatibility[]

Each Game Mode in Fortnite has unique cosmetic support. Cosmetic Types that are not available to use in a mode will be grayed out once the mode is selected, while displaying the warning "{Type} items are not usable in the current experience". If an unusable cosmetic from a supported type is equipped, another warning will be displayed: "The {Type} {Cosmetic Name} is not usable in the current experience. A default {Type} will be used in its place".

  • Kicks can only be used with supported Outfits.
  • Banners, Lobby Music and Loading Screens are always available for use, regardless of the selected mode.
  • Decor Bundles and Builds are exclusive to LEGO Fortnite. However, they will never be grayed out, no matter which mode is selected, and viewing their tab will display that "LEGO® Kits can not be equipped. Once you are in a relevant Fortnite experience, you can use all of your owned Kits".

Battle Royale Playlists[]

  • In Battle Royale and its variants, almost all Cosmetic Types can be used, except for Auras.

Save the World[]

  • In Save the World, only Outfits, Back Blings, Pickaxes, Kicks, Emotes, Sidekicks, Wraps, Bass, Guitars, Drums, Keytars, Microphones, and Jam Tracks can be used. Wraps can be used on the Hoverboard and on Weapons (except Melee Weapons).
  • The Save the World Locker includes an exclusive feature that allows players to use the appearance of the currently active Hero as their Outfit, along with that Hero's respective Back Bling.
  • Some Reactive Cosmetics may not function properly in the mode.

LEGO Fortnite Playlists[]

  • In LEGO Fortnite Odyssey and LEGO Fortnite Brick Life, only LEGO® Style Outfits, Emotes, Sidekicks, and Jam Tracks can be used, along with the mode's exclusive Cosmetic Types: Builds and Decor Bundles.

Rocket Racing[]

  • In Rocket Racing, only Outfits, Car Bodies, Decals, Wheels, Trails, and Boosts can be used.

Fortnite Festival Playlists[]

  • In Fortnite Festival and its variants, only Outfits, Back Blings, Kicks, Auras, Emotes, Sidekicks, Bass, Guitars, Drums, Keytars, Microphones, and Jam Tracks can be used.
    • Despite not being grayed out while Festival Jam Stage is selected, Auras cannot be used in the mode.

Creative[]

In Creative, the usable cosmetics vary based on the currently selected experience. However, the Locker will always gray out Cosmetic Types based on the type of the island, not on the actual compatibility with that island. Therefore:

  • Fortnite Islands have the same compatibility as Battle Royale;
  • LEGO Fortnite Islands have the same compatibility as LEGO Fortnite Odyssey (although Builds and Decor Bundles are always unusable);
  • Rocket Racing Islands have the same compatibility as Rocket Racing.
  • Fall Guys Islands only supports Bean Style Outfits.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Since the v30.00 Update, when any cosmetic in any of the Cars subtabs is viewed, the background color of the Locker changes to a much lighter shade of blue, bringing similarities to the background from before the Locker's major revamp in Update v28.00.
    • This also affects the background of other instances where a car-related cosmetic can be viewed, such as when previewing one in the "claim rewards" screen and in the Item Shop.
    • The Changing Booth also has a different background color from the one used in the Locker; however, this background applies to all cosmetics.
  • At the start of Update v30.20, the Rap Boy Outfit couldn't be equipped or seen in the Locker due to it being re-encrypted. It was only decrypted four days later, along with the release of the Houdini Emote.
    • A similar situation occurred in Update v31.40, with the Omni-Claw Pickaxe being re-encrypted along with the Edward Scissorhands Set cosmetics. However, it was decrypted just a few hours after the update, unlike the Rap Boy Outfit, which remained encrypted for a much longer time.
  • In Chapter 2: Season 3, it was announced that Save the World would no longer be able to support all upcoming Cosmetic purchases from Battle Royale over the coming months.
    • However, all Cosmetics introduced from Chapter 2: Season 3 onward are still compatible with it, including most new Cosmetic Types with Battle Royale support.
