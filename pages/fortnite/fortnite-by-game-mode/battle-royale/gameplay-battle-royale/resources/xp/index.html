Season XP, also known as Battle Pass XP or simply XP, is a form of Experience Points in Fortnite that can be obtained by playing most modes and completing Quests in each.

Season XP is used to level up Passes, including the Battle Pass, the LEGO Pass, the Music Pass, and the OG Pass, which progressively unlock Cosmetic Items.

Changes Throughout Seasons

Changes Throughout Seasons[]

The XP System has gone through several changes since its introduction in Season 1. The tier progression system replaced the XP system in Season 3 before reverting back in Chapter 2: Season 1.

In Chapter 2, the Season XP bar is displayed on the bottom of the screen. The bar is yellow when the player has Supercharged XP.

Prior to Update v18.40, the only way to gain Season XP in Creative was through playtime. The Accolade Device in Creative was introduced and it allows players to earn XP through various methods. Creative and UEFN experiences with Accolades enabled display the icon on the playlist selection menu.

Save the World also received Season XP support in Update v18.40, and has its own daily (now weekly) XP cap.[1]

When UEFN was released, no Season XP could be earned when playing experiences made in UEFN. Playtime XP was later enabled. On August 28th 2023, the Accolade Device was added to UEFN.

After the launch of Chapter 6, Fortnite now has 4 different Passes that can all progress at the same time with Season XP.

Ways To Earn

Ways To Earn[]

Season XP can be earned:

  • In Battle Royale and Zero Build matches, including Ranked, Tournaments, Team Rumble, and Limited Time Modes, by earning Accolades and completing Quests.
  • In Save the World, by completing Missions and Quests.
    • XP is earned regardless of mission outcome. There is a weekly XP cap of 4,000,000 as of Chapter 6: Season 1.
  • In Rocket Racing, Fortnite Festival, LEGO Fortnite by completing Quests, or through playtime.
  • In Creative and UEFN experiences with the Accolade Device, or through playtime.
