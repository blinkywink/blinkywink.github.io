Overview


Elimination, commonly refered to as Killing is a Gameplay Mechanic in Fortnite: Battle Royale. It describes how a player is removed from the playable game, unless they are Rebooted.

Description

Description[]

Elimination is the act of removing all of a player's hitpoints. Elimination in solo modes is immediate, but in Squad based modes (Duos, Trios and Squads), players are instead knocked. Eliminated players drop all of their items onto the ground, plus a small amount of Bars. In Squad based modes, players also drop a Reboot Card, which can be used to reboot a player and bring them back into the game. Players can be eliminated through several different methods, whether self inflicted, by a squad member, or by an opponent.

Eliminated players can spectate other players in their squad, if the squad remains, or any player left if the squad has been eliminated. Eliminated players will show in the Elimination Feed upon being eliminated. If a player knocks an opponent, they will receive elimination credit upon their elimination, regardless of who eliminates the knocked player (This is not shown in the Elimination Feed).

AI, such as bosses, or henchmen, can be knocked.

In Team Rumble[]

In Team Rumble, despite being a squad based mode, players do not get knocked, but instead die immediately as respawning is enabled. Furthermore, players will not drop any inventory items, and ammo and bars will spawn from where they were eliminated, rather than being deducted from said player's resources.

Fortnitemares 2020[]

In Fortnitemares 2020, players would not be knocked, and even in solo modes they would turn into a Shadow. Players could then obtain a Victory Royale by working with other shadows to eliminate all remaining players.

Down But Not Out (DBNO)

Down But Not Out (DBNO)[]

Down But Not Out (DBNO for short), or the Knocked State is a state in which the player goes into when eliminated in a standard team-based mode, or, most recently, while holding a Self-Revive Device in both team-based and solo modes. Knocked players cannot use items or use any movement abilities aside from basic movement, and basic swimming.

Knocked players gain temporary invulnerability for a few seconds after being knocked, to prevent instant elimination. They have 100 health, which decreases at a rate of 2 health per second. This health can be replenished using healing methods like a Campfire, Chug Splash, or other items that allow healing of teammates. Knocked players cannot have Shield, but if eliminated by fall damage or storm damage, they will retain the Shield they had prior to being knocked, upon being revived. Players are revived with 30 health, regardless of their health prior to being knocked.

Knocked players can be revived by teammates by holding the Interact key for 10 seconds (5 seconds in Reload playlists) on the downed player. The revival process speeds up if multiple teammates assist. Knocked players can also be carried and thrown. A squad will be eliminated if no more than two members remain standing, and the last standing player is eliminated. Alternatively, knocked players can use a Self-Revive Device by pressing the interact key and waiting for 10 seconds.

History

History[]

Pre-Season[]

  • Update v1.6: Added Revive, Knocked and Elimination mechanics with the first patch of the game.
    • Friendly Fire is on, meaning squad members can eliminate other squad members.
  • Update v1.7: Revival progress is now shown in the UI for the entire team.
  • Update v1.7.1: Changes:
    • Eliminations will now be awarded to the player who downs an enemy regardless of who finishes off that player in the DBNO (Down, But Not Out) state.
    • Increased health after being revived from the DBNO state (25 to 30).
    • Shields will no longer protect you from damage while in the DBNO state (You will keep any remaining shield if a teammate revives you).
    • Friendly fire damage will now display the appropriate visual hit effects
  • Update v1.7.2: Players no longer display their weapon while DBNO (Down, But Not Out.)

Season 1[]

  • Update v1.8: Introduced Slurp Juice in Epic Rarity. It heals 25 health and 25 shield at 1 tick per second, for 25 seconds.
    • This allows players to heal whilst knocked for the first time.

Season 2[]

  • Update v2.1.0: Introduced the Cozy Campfire in Rare.
    • This allows players to heal knocked players and keep them alive for longer, for the first time.
  • Update v2.2.0: Removed Friendly Fire.

Season 5[]

  • Update v5.20: Slurp Juice no longer continues to heal players who have been knocked.

Season 6[]

  • Update v6.20: Introduced Cube Monsters.
    • These are the first AI to be added, and thus eliminated.

Chapter 2: Season 1[]

  • Update v11.00: Changes:
    • Knocked Players can now be picked up and thrown.
    • Knocked Players can swim.
  • Update v11.30: Introduced First Order Stormtroopers at TIE Fighter Crash Sites.
    • These mark the first AI which can be knocked.

Chapter 2: Season 2[]

  • Update v12.00: Introduced Shakedowns, which are performed on a knocked Opponent, including some AI.

Chapter 3: Season 1[]

  • Update v19.00: Changes:
    • Knocked Players move much faster
    • Knocked Opponents can no longer be shaken down.
Trivia

Trivia[]

  • When a player is eliminated, a Spawn Drone appears to pick up the body and quickly flies away.
  • Players who are knocked can use ziplines, and gain the anti-gravity effects of them.
  • Squad members can eliminate other squad members by either destroying a structure to cause elimination by fall damage, or set them on fire.
  • When a player is eliminated, there's a chance that eliminated player will drop a victory crown. This will only happen if said eliminated player has at least one in their inventory. When it does happen an icon of said item will pop up for everyone (except spectators of viewing another player upon elimination) on their screen, it'll disappear when someone picks it up.
