Mikufans, it’s time to grab your glowsticks (or should we say leeks?!). Hatsune Miku (初音ミク), one of the biggest virtual pop stars in the world, is bringing her futuristic sounds to Fortnite as the Icon for Festival Season 7!

The singing synthesizer turned global superstar is featured in more than 100,000 songs and has beamed her way onto stages around the world. Now she’s heading to the Festival stage!



Unlock a Hatsune Miku Outfit, Instruments, and more in the Music Pass, which is now a single track of both free and premium rewards (matching the other Passes in Fortnite). You’ll still unlock free rewards by earning XP in any Fortnite experience, and purchasing the Music Pass will enable you to unlock premium rewards the same way as you do free rewards. You can purchase the Music Pass for 1,400 V-Bucks or get it through Fortnite Crew!

The sound of the future is almost here: Fortnite Festival Season 7 begins with v33.20 on January 14 and comes to a close on April 8, 2025.

Meet on the Main Stage in Local Multiplayer

Meet on the Main Stage in Local Multiplayer[]



The launch of Festival Season 7 comes with a new local multiplayer feature on PlayStation and Xbox! In a local play session of Festival Main Stage, up to four players can play together on the same console. All of the players need to party up in the Lobby then enter the Festival Main Stage together.

Each local player must be signed in with both an active Epic Games account and platform account to use this feature. (Players cannot play using platform guest accounts.) The Epic Games account you’re signed into must allow T-rated experiences. Get the band back together!

May Your Digital Dreams Come True in the Season 7 Music Pass

May Your Digital Dreams Come True in the Season 7 Music Pass[]

This is part of the new unified progression system where you can progress all Passes in Fortnite by earning XP in any experience you want to play! Check out the Progress Your Fortnite Passes by Playing Any Experience blog post for a recap.

New Battle Stage Modes!

New Battle Stage Modes![]



The Battle Stage is getting four new modes that test your expertise on specific song parts — allowing players to compete on a more even playing field:

  • Lead Only - Only the Lead and Pro Lead parts are available.
  • Drums Only - Only the Drums part is available.
  • Vocals Only - Only the Vocals part is available.
  • Bass Only - Only the Bass and Pro Bass parts are available.

These new modes also have scoring that's more consistent with how song parts work on the Main Stage, so be careful not to break that 6x score multiplier on Bass or Vocals!

Equip Instruments as Pickaxes and Back Blings

Equip Instruments as Pickaxes and Back Blings[]



Starting with v33.20, you can equip some purchased or earned Guitars, Basses, Keytars, Mics — and the drumsticks from some of your Drums — as a Pickaxe or Back Bling. Check your Locker to see which of your Instruments can be equipped in new ways!

Fortnite Festival Content Creation

Fortnite Festival Content Creation[]



Starting with Festival Season 7, monetization of VOD content featuring eligible Jam Tracks will be limited to the first seven days of the Season. No takedown notifications should occur during or after the seven days.

Beyond this initial monetization period, content creators should expect to receive monetization claims by the respective rights holders.

The eligible Jam Tracks for Season 7 are:

  • Ashnikko ft. Hatsune Miku - “Daisy 2.0”
  • Anamanaguchi, Hatsune Miku - “Miku”
  • ryo (supercell) ft. Hatsune Miku - “World Is Mine”

More info:

  • In order to monetize your Festival content, you need to link your YouTube and Support-A-Creator (SAC) accounts.
  • IMPORTANT: When uploading the content to YouTube, be sure to upload it as Unlisted or Public. If you upload the video as Private, it's possible you could receive a monetization claim on your video.
  • For uploading the content on other platforms, refer to the channel’s platform policies for tips on how to avoid copyright strikes.

Although unlikely, if you do receive a claim on any SAC content, there may be a short delay in YouTube processing the release of the claim. Please do not dispute the claim during this time.

Not in SAC? Don’t worry, you can still share your experiences featuring the Jam Tracks. However, you will not be able to monetize this content on YouTube.

Please note! 🎵 For Twitch creators streaming gameplay featuring the eligible Jam Tracks, we cannot prevent your VODs or clips from getting flagged by third-party copyright detection systems. The general recommendation is to disable VODs/clips when streaming gameplay featuring the Jam Tracks. For more information, see Twitch’s DMCA & Copyright FAQs help page.

Happy New Year, Commanders! 🎆 We’re kicking 2025 off with some good news. The year’s very first batch of fixes and improvements is now live, including but not limited to:

Bug Fixes

Bug Fixes[]

  • Mission Alerts now function as intended and reset correctly.
  • The ability to use the D-Pad Up / Down controller inputs after completing a Mission is now restored.
  • The “Fill / No Fill” Mission setting now persists accordingly after being changed.
  • Players will no longer be stuck in first-person camera view after playing a match in Ballistic.
  • Fixed an issue where multiple Traps could be placed on a single player-built structure after each structure upgrade.
  • Husks will now keep attacking the player using R.O.S.I.E., no matter which direction the player faces.
  • Weapon projectiles now properly collide with the Mist Titan’s fist.
  • The invisible, unnatural collision around the Drone Crash side objective area has been fixed.
  • Sleeping Husks no longer appear on the (mini)map.
  • Fixed a visual issue where the Reload Time and Magazine Size stats weren’t accurate after applying Perks to a weapon.
  • Visual effects now trigger immediately when hitting Weak Points with the Microbot Mallet Pickaxe.
  • The tornado in Frozen Fjords now has proper textures and no longer appears fully smooth/opaque.
  • Fixed an issue where the Urban Assault Headhunter Outfit displayed excessive shine on the Mission Launch / Hero Loadout screens.
  • When using a consumable item, the timer size now matches the player’s HUD scale.
  • Deployed a full fix for an issue where the "Mission Activation Vote" UI would not open after pressing the designated button during an ongoing Mission.
  • Players can again open the Sidebar / Social menu during a Mission.
  • Player movement will now be halted upon opening the Text Chat Settings to prevent players from ending up in potentially dangerous situations.
  • The "View Profile" Text Chat function now opens the appropriate menu while in a Mission.
  • Objectives’ health bars will no longer be displayed over screens opened via Text Chat.
Undocumented Changes

Undocumented Changes[]

  • Snowstrike can now be recruited from the Collection Book with a Hero Recruitment Voucher.
  • Primal Flame Bow can now be researched from the Collection Book with a Weapon Research Voucher.
Known Issues

Known Issues[]

The following issues will be fixed in Update v33.30:

  • Tornadoes may destroy Traps in Wargames or Endurance runs.
  • Fall damage not reduced while riding the Hoverboard.
