Overview


Update v16.10 of Fortnite was released on March 30th 2021.

April 1st changes

April 1st changes[]

Spring Breakout[]

The Egg Launcher returns! and you can also be on the lookout for a new type of forageable egg on the Island.

Weapons + Items[]

  • Unvaulted the Egg Launcher.
  • Bouncy Egg Added.
    • Grants the Low-Gravity effect for a certain period of time upon consumption.
    • Found on the floor scattered across the Island.

World[]

  • Added Webster NPC at Gas N’ Grub.

Battle Royale

Battle Royale[]

General[]

  • Fixed a issue where duplicate XP notifications would appear when completing Legendary "Craft Items" Quests in a party.
  • Fixed a issue where Quests given by Characters in a match were not counting progression towards Quests that require the completion of other Quests of specific rarities.

Weapons + Items[]

  • Makeshift Shotgun changes:
    • Magazine size increased from 2 to 3.
    • Reload times decreased from 3.69/3.15/2.64s to 3.5/3.0/2.5s
    • Faster pullout times
  • Primal Shotguns fire-rate decreased from 3.73 to 3.33.
  • Grenade Launcher damage decreased from 110/105/100 to 77/74/70.
  • Crafting material costs now scale with Makeshift rarity. As such, adjustments have been made:
    • Common to Uncommon upgrades now cost 2 Animal Bones or Mechanical Parts.
    • Uncommon to Rare upgrades remains at 4 Animal Bones or Mechanical Parts.
    • Rare to Epic upgrades now cost 6 Animal Bones or Mechanical Parts.

Gameplay[]

  • Animal Bones and Mechanical Parts were added as Floor Loot.
  • Moving Storm Circles in non-competitive modes have returned for the last two zones for a week.

Bug fixes

  • Fixed a issue where players in Split Screen could've had difficulty playing another match together due to the "Ready Up!" option not working properly.
  • Fixed a issue where opening the inventory while performing a tap to search/interact/Revive action would cancel that action.
  • Fixed a issue where stackable and consumable items (Grenades, Bandages, etc.) were not split when using Shift+Drag to select or drop half of the stack using keyboard+mouse input.

World[]

  • Raptor Eggs across the map are now broken, and Raptors roam the Island.
    • Raptors roam in pairs, and will attack whoever and whatever crosses their path.

Bug fixes

  • Fixed a issue where the anomaly on Shark Island could not appear after completing the required steps.

UI[]

Bug fixes

  • Fixed a issue where legacies for Chapter 2: Season 5 would not show up in the Career tab.
Creative

Creative[]

What's new?[]

Heal or harm with the new Health Powerup, which allows you to manipulate player health and shield levels during games. Apply healing or damage instantly or over time, or even set health and shields to the exact numbers that you want.

Devices[]

  • Health Powerup.
    • This Powerup allows player health and shield values to be manipulated during the game. Like other Powerups it can be used as a pickup object or applied remotely via channels.
    • The Powerup model will change automatically based on which attributes are being affected and whether the impact is positive or negative.
    • This device has the following options:
      • Modify: Health Only, Shield Only, Both (Default: Health Only)
      • Effect: Flat Increase, % Increase, Flat Decrease, % Decrease, Set To (Default: Flat Increase)
      • Effect Magnitude: 0-15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50, 60, 70, 80, 90, 100, 125, 150, 175, 200, 250, 300, 350, 400, 450, 500, 600, 700, 800, 900, 1000, 1250, 1500, 1750, 2000, 2500 (Default: 20)
      • Effect Duration: Instant, 3 seconds, 5 seconds, 10 seconds, 20 seconds, 30 seconds, 1 minute, 2 minutes, 5 minutes, Infinite (Default: Instant)
        • For durations other than Instant, the Effect Magnitude is applied once every second.
      • Show Visual Effect on Player: Yes, No (Default: Yes)
      • Disables Effect on Pickup: Yes, No (Default: No)
      • Pickup Radius: On Touch, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1, 1.5, 2, 2.5, 3, 3.5, 4, 4.5, 5 (Default: On Touch)
      • Time to Respawn: Never, Instant, 5 seconds, 10 seconds, 15 seconds, 20 seconds, 25 seconds, 30 seconds, 45 seconds, 1 minute, 1.5 minutes, 2 minutes, 3 minutes, 4 minutes, 5 minutes (Default: 15 seconds)
      • Spawn on Minigame Start: Yes, No (Default: Yes)
      • Ambient Audio: On, Off (Default: On)
      • Pick Up Audio: On, Off (Default: On)
      • Selected Team: Any, 1-16 (Default: Any)
      • Selected Class: Any, 1-16 (Default: Any)
      • Apply To: Player, Player’s Team, Player’s Class, Same Class in Player’s Team, All Players (Default: Player)
      • Who Can See This Powerup: None, All, Only Players that can Pick Up (Default: Only Player that can Pick Up)
      • Spawn When Receiving From: No Channel, 1-150 (Default: No Channel)
      • Despawn When Receiving From: No Channel, 1-150 (Default: No Channel)
      • Pickup When Receiving From: No Channel, 1-150 (Default: No Channel)
      • When Item Picked Up Transmit On: No Channel, 1-150 (Default: No Channel)
  • Additonal changes to Powerups.
    • The Powerups Gallery has been removed and individual Powerups can now be found in the Device Gallery.
    • Existing Powerups have had new options added to them:
      • Selected Team: Any, 1-16 (Default: Any)
      • Selected Class: Any, 1-16 (Default: Any)
      • Apply To: Player, Player’s Team, Player’s Class, Same Class in Player’s Team, All Players (Default: Player)
      • Who Can See This Powerup: None, All, Only Players that can Pick Up (Default: Only Player that can Pick Up)
  • Mutator Zone device - new options
    • Allow Editing: Yes, No (Default: Yes) - Enables or Disables editing of player-built structures within the zone (normal Edit permissions apply).
    • Building Cost: 0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%, 125%, 150%, 200% (Default: 100%) - Adjusts the resource cost for building structures within the zone.
    • Building Speed: 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%, 125%, 150%, 200% (Default: 100%) - Adjusts the speed at which player structures build once placed.

Bug fixes

  • Fixed an issue where players were able to use Wolverine Claws with other skins after using the Phone Booth.
  • Fixed an issue where players were stuck in Build mode after exiting the Phone Booth.
  • Fixed an issue where the underside of the Barrier Device had z-fighting.
  • Fixed an issue where the Barrier Device and Barrier Plate Device had the same name in the inventory.
  • Fixed an issue where the Barrier Device had collision when Enabled During Phase was set to All.

Islands[]

Bug fixes

  • Fixed an issue where switching islands did not reset island settings.
  • Fixed an issue where the player rift would not appear when Creative Don’t Fill was selected on the front end tile.
  • Fixed an issue where players entered slow motion in the Hub after a game ended.
  • Fixed an issue where the My Island Player Name and Location setting overrode the Class Designer’s Player Names and Location setting.
  • Fixed an issue where the gold rift changed ownership after a new island was created by players in a large group.

Gameplay[]

Bug fixes

  • Fixed an issue where players could not set 999 for max ammo.
  • Fixed an issue where players could not drive vehicles.
  • Fixed an issue where Creatures would get stuck or glitch through obstacles.

Weapons + Items[]

Bug fixes Fixed an issue where players were able to spawn unreleased mythic items. Fixed an issue where Tires did not apply bounce when hidden under primitives or props.

Save the World[]

COMING APRIL 3 AT 8PM ET - GRAB YOUR GEAR, COMMANDERS. THE SCURVY SHOALS VENTURE SEASON BEGINS[]

Get ready for a new Ventures Season, Commanders. The next Save the World Ventures Season -- Scurvy Shoals -- begins April 3 at 8PM ET and is expected to conclude on June 19 at 8PM ET.


--WARNING-- The Husks are exceptionally Super during the Scurvy Shoals Venture Season. The Husks, now known as Super Husks, are packing some serious modifier power in the Scurvy Shoals Ventures missions. Here’s what we’re seeing in the field:


Super Melee Monsters - Takers and melee-based Husks move and attack faster, and their attacks slow Commanders by 30% for 5 seconds. Eliminating Super Melee Husks will stun smaller enemies in a small radius for a short amount of time. Chaining these stuns together may stun stronger Husks.


Super Ranged Monsters - Flingers, Pitchers, and Lobbers throw additional projectiles, while Zappers and Blasters fire twice as quickly. All their blasting efforts are causing them to have 30% less health.


Super Tough Husks - Husky Husks and Smashers have increased resistance to trap and ranged weapon damage, and deal increased damage to structures. Sure they’re super, but all that smashing power causes them to move 20% slower.


Now it’s our turn, Commanders. Grab your gear because it’s a shootin’ and lootin’ season. Eliminating Husks, especially those Smashers, causes them to periodically drop a chest full of valuable items. Greater monsters mean greater rewards and the more Husks we eliminate, the more chests we can loot.
