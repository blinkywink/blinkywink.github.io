The Getaway LTM and Outlaw Midas Come to Fortnite Battle Royale v34.10!

The gilded man with the golden touch is back and badder than ever! Outlaw Midas has arrived with v34.10 along with the updated Getaway LTM, a slew of new weapons in the Black Market, and the return of Siphon in Ranked. Let’s get down to brass gold tacks!

Make Out Like a Bandit in the Getaway LTM

Make Out Like a Bandit in the Getaway LTM[]

The Getaway limited-time mode is returning from Chapter 1! From now until April 1 Eastern Time, retrieve one of four Crystal Llamas (Jewels) on the map as a duo or 4-player squad, and successfully extract them in one of the Island’s three Getaway Vans. The first three teams to make their way to a van win! You can also win in traditional Battle Royale style, by eliminating all other teams.

Holding a Jewel will cause you to move slower and show on the map for other players. But on the flip side, holding a Jewel will restore your Health and Shield over time! The Getaway LTM is live now with Build and Zero Build options — play Duos or Squads in unranked or… Ranked!

This is our first Ranked LTM and there are a few differences from other Ranked experiences. Namely, each member of your team will receive a large bonus to their rank for extracting a Jewel, and rank progression from each match is increased. The progress you make in Ranked Getaway will not unlock Styles for existing Ranked rewards.

Earn Your Place in the Outlaw Faction

Earn Your Place in the Outlaw Faction[]

The baron Fletcher Kane is levying a hefty tax on all vending machines and paid porta-potties. It’s up to you all to put an end to his insatiable greed! v34.10 introduces a new Quest, where the community is tasked to collectively drain Fletcher Kane of his resources by stealing Bars from his banks and from stashes around the Island.

Once the Quest is complete, all players will earn a basic membership to the Outlaw faction, which comes with its own upgradable Outlaw Keycard. With your Outlaw Keycard, you’ll gain access to the backroom of Black Markets around the Island! Complete Outlaw Keycard Quests to upgrade your card and gain access to more services and Exotic new weaponry. Here’s what you get with each upgrade:

  • Uncommon Outlaw Keycard: Gain access to Black Market backrooms, where you can purchase Exotic items. Some Black Market backrooms also have muscle for hire.
  • Rare Outlaw Keycard: You’ll be able to open an Outlaw Chest in Black Market backrooms for free Bars.
  • Epic Outlaw Keycard: You’ll be able to buy premium loadouts from each Black Market shopkeeper for a significant amount of Bars.
    • Skillet’s Loadout - Sticky Grenade Launcher, Mammoth Pistol, Kneecapper, Chug Jug, Gold Ammo Boon, and Greed Boon.
    • Keisha’s Loadout - Falcon Eye Sniper, Outlaw Shotgun, Gold Splashes, Chug Jug, Gold Ammo Boon, and Greed Boon.
    • Joss’ Loadout - Holo Twister AR, Pump & Dump, Rocket Drill, Chug Jug, Gold Ammo Boon, and Greed Boon.
  • Legendary Outlaw Keycard: Outlaw Chests in Black Market backrooms will also drop a Legendary item and a Dill Bit.

Once you’ve got those Black Market backrooms open, you’ll find new Exotic Items for sale, hot off the back of a truck. The following weapons will be available in exchange for Dill Bits!

  • Lawless Slap Cannon: Fire healing Slap at your allies to restore their Health and Shield.
  • Lawless Blink Pump & Dump: Grants a few seconds of Zero Point Dash after reloading.
  • Lawless Stink Rifle: Unleashes a stink cloud on impact.
  • Lawless Shockwave Rocket Launcher: Fires a projectile that explodes to impulse anyone nearby.
  • Lawless Trinity Assault Rifle: Fires four shots in a triangle pattern, at the cost of a decreased fire rate.
  • Lawless Heavy Impact Tracking Rifle: Briefly reveals the location of players and Chests around the impact point of your shots.

Dropping from Chests around the Island outside of Black Markets are the Outlaw Shotgun and Rocket Drill. There’s also Big Dill’s fave — the regular Pump & Dump. Occupying a single weapon slot, you can now hold a Shotgun in one hand and an SMG in the other!

Siphon Returns in Ranked

Siphon Returns in Ranked[]

Siphon, which partially heals you when you eliminate an opponent, is returning to Ranked Battle Royale and Zero Build for the remainder of LAWLESS! Like current competitive tournaments — every time you eliminate an opponent in a Ranked Battle Royale or Zero Build match, you’ll now gain 75 Health over five seconds. Siphon is not enabled in Ranked OG, Reload, or The Getaway.

Unlock the Infamous Outlaw Midas

Unlock the Infamous Outlaw Midas[]

The glittering, SHADOWY gunslinger is here! Starting now, owners of the LAWLESS Battle Pass will be able to unlock the Outlaw Midas Outfit by earning 10 Account Levels. You can work towards this goal by earning XP from any experience!

By meeting other Account Level goals, you can unlock matching accessories like the Outlaws Knife Rack Back Bling as well as the Outlaw's Whipknives Pickaxe, Bullet Rider Glider, Trouble's Comin' Emote, and more. Keep meeting the goals to unlock Outlaw Midas’ Gunslinger, SHADOW, and GHOST Styles! These goals will be completable until Fortnite Battle Royale Chapter 6 Season 2: LAWLESS ends May 2, 2025 Eastern Time.

Get ready for the Getaway Van — v34.10 is dropping now!

LEGO® Fortnite Updates - What to Expect in March 2025!

The world of LEGO® Fortnite expands with new updates for both Brick Life and Odyssey this March! Hit the pitch as soccer comes to Brick Life on March 11, and keep up the fight against the Storm King as improvements come to Odyssey on March 25.

Without further ado, let’s dig in… but wait a minute, what did our shovel just hit? The Rebel Oro Outfit and more rewards await in the Goldrush Gallery LEGO Pass arriving March 11!

LEGO Fortnite Brick Life Updates - March 11

LEGO Fortnite Brick Life Updates - March 11[]

Get off the Sidelines in Soccer!

Go for the goal and slide into [[[LEGO Fortnite:Soccer|soccer]], or commonly called “football” around the world! At the soccer field across from Bobom Milk Tea, score more goals than other players in a 90 second time limit. The winning player gets 1,000 Creds and a trophy for their home!

Soccer is not a “daily activity” like Litter Boxing or Vampire Experiment, so you can win the full rewards no matter what time of day it is.

React to Homes!

See a kickin’ brick pad and wanna give kudos? Interact with a home’s mailbox and leave reactions! Whether it’s a house made entirely of toilets that makes you laugh or a maxed-out mansion that leaves you in awe, reactions let you have an influence on your favorite homes.

Reactions are visible from afar too, so you’ll be able to see Brick Bay’s most conversation-starting homes without needing to interact with their mailbox. Earn 50 Creds every time you react to a home for the first time! (Remember to be a good guest — don’t just react and dash!)

Can’t Carry It? Get Mailed It!

Got the Undisputed Gloves from Litter Boxing, but don’t have room in your inventory? You don’t have to go home feline sad! Items you earn or buy will be instantly sent to your mailbox if your inventory is full.

If you weren’t planning to stop back at your house, you can also use public mailboxes to get your stuff!

Keep checking your mail to see other things that may be waiting for you ✉

45-Degree Decoratin’

Not everyone likes to decorate by the book. When placing decorations in your home, you can now place them at 45-degree angles like in Odyssey. Add a new twist to your interior design!

LEGO Fortnite Odyssey Updates - March 25

LEGO Fortnite Odyssey Updates - March 25[]

Now let’s talk about the Odyssey improvements coming later in March!

Do Your World a Favor with the Recycler!

With the new Recycler Station, recycle crafted items to gain a portion of the used materials back. This means you can break down old Charms and weapons into resources you can reuse. Unlock the Recycler recipe by building or using a Stone Breaker starting March 25.

Wall Mount Changes

Starting March 25, you’ll be able to put non-holdable items on Wall Mounts, like resources and Charms. Use these as labels for the chests you put below them!

And if your Wall Mounts are hanging things that can turn on or off — like the Illuminator or a Lightsaber — you'll be able to turn them on or off on the Wall Mount.

For easier access, the Wall Mount will move to the Utility section of the Build menu.

Sprint Attack

Be a force with the new sprint attack! Sprint to close the gap on enemies, delivering a powerful strike when you catch up.

Jump Attack Changes

The jump attack will have clearer visuals so it's easier to see when you hit an enemy. Also, the issue that prevents the attack from doing damage when it looks like it should have done damage will be fixed.

Sprint Jump

Leap into the adventure like never before with the sprint jump. This new jump adds an extra spring to your step and makes exploring your Odyssey world even smoother!

Advanced Weapons Journal

The new Advanced Weapons Journal will help remind you which weapons you can craft! Activate the journal in two ways:

  • Try refining your raw materials to stone slabs, metal bars, cut crystals, etc.
  • Interact with Stations like a Gem Cutter or Metal Smelter and start crafting a weapon of the same rarity as the refined materials you previously crafted.

Next-Level Small Talk

Villagers might have some things to say to you, without you needing to talk to them first! If they do, you’ll see a little bubble above their head with a comment.

Safety from the Storm (and… Rocks)

When you run out of time in the Storm, you’ll be teleported into the sky at a safe distance. This is to help prevent an issue that makes players sometimes get stuck in rocks and other things!

Thunderclap Blade Updates

Landing hits with the Thunderclap Blade will charge up the projectile attack, making it simpler to get this attack ready. The spin animation before the projectile launches will be removed so it's easier to land attacks on distant enemies!

Also, the more you charge up, the more damage you'll do with both the normal and projectile attack.

A Raise in Shield Usefulness

In skirmishes with enemies, raising your shield will be faster and more responsive!

Goldrush Gallery LEGO Pass - Get it March 11!

Goldrush Gallery LEGO Pass - Get it March 11![]

Revenge is best served gilded in gold, which is why the famous skeleton Oro is rising as Rebel Oro! This Outfit is in the new Goldrush Gallery LEGO Pass going live March 11.

Unlock free and premium rewards in the Goldrush Gallery LEGO Pass by earning XP in any Fortnite experience. To claim the premium rewards as you earn XP, purchase the Pass for only 1,400 V-Bucks or get it through Fortnite Crew!

You’ll automatically get the Rebel Oro Outfit after purchasing the Pass or accessing it through Fortnite Crew. Here’s a surprise: this LEGO Pass has TWO Outfits! Progress through the Pass to unlock the Carmine Rosso Outfit as another premium reward.

There’s a lot more to unlock too! For example, the Oro’s Abode Decor Bundle is perfect for anyone looking to give their bedroom a glamorous golden makeover.

If you’re an expert in metals, like gold, maybe you also appreciate the art of welding! Become a tradesperson with the Welding Essentials Decor Bundle, or for a more traditional fusion of gold and art, turn your home into a museum with the Golden Peel Gallery Decor Bundle. (Be careful — art collecting is a slippery slope!)

Struck gold, so to speak? If you’re feeling smug, celebrate your riches with the Loot Stash Emote! Misfortune struck instead? Let it be known with the Tragedy! Emote.

Some of the free and premium rewards in the Goldrush Gallery LEGO Pass.

All the rewards mentioned above are just PART of what’s in the Pass. The Goldrush Gallery LEGO Pass will be available to progress in until May 2, 2025 Eastern Time. As a reminder, everything in a LEGO Pass may come to the Fortnite Shop at a later date.

Before we go — reminder that some fan-favorite LEGO Kits will return to the Shop on March 12 Eastern Time! Find them in the Durrr Burger Bundle, Beachy Boardwalk Bundle, and Mage's Favorites Bundle all making a comeback.

Other fan favorites will return on March 19 Eastern Time.

Fortnite Ballistic v34.10 - Early Access Development Update

Ballistic players,

Today’s v34.10 update includes several improvements and fixes, with more to come in v34.20 later in March. We’ve also got an update on our near-future plans for Ballstic, which includes new maps, weapons, and gadgets dropping in April.

Early Access Development Update

Early Access Development Update[]

As we shared recently, we want to get new updates for Ballistic into your hands faster. One way we’re planning to accomplish this is by adding new maps to Ballistic as shells — fully designed layouts but without all the environmental details that you might expect. This will allow us to bring two new maps to Ballistic in April. Expect new weapons and gadgets in April as well!

For the new maps, we’ll examine how players move, rotate, and use sightlines during matches to inform how we fine-tune each. We’re also continuing to iterate on the Skyline 10 map and are looking for more feedback there.

Speaking of feedback, send us your thoughts! You can share your input through our online survey, or by completing an in-game post-match survey. More surveys will be shared in future blog posts, in addition to the [Fortnite Battle Royale subreddit https://www.reddit.com/r/FortNiteBR/] (which we’re currently using for Ballistic) and the [English Fortnite Discord https://discord.gg/fortnite]. Right now, we’re specifically looking for feedback on:
  • Mode timings (Such as Round timer, Buy Phase timer, Detonation timer, Defuse timer)
  • Existing weapons and gadgets
  • Movement and shooting
  • The Skyline 10 map
  • Credit economy
  • UI/UX

The time it takes for us to act on feedback depends on what we’re trying to change. Value changes, such as adjusting fire rate, magazine size, or the time it takes for the Rift Point Device to be planted or detonated, are all simple updates we can turn around rather quickly. Larger updates, such as changing a map's layout or how a gadget works, will take longer.

In our next blog post later in March, we’ll discuss some of the feedback we received during this round and how we plan to address it. Until then, let us know how you’re feeling about the improvements and fixes below going live with v34.10 today. (You can use the survey to let us know!)

Let's start with a major pain point. Matches starting with uneven teams are not fun — we know! Beginning with v34.10, we've made an adjustment so that matches will no longer begin unless the lobby is full (5v5). If the lobby is unable to fill, all players will return to the main menu and restart matchmaking.

Other Improvements and Fixes - v34.10

Other Improvements and Fixes - v34.10[]

  • Made improvements to server/client data exchange to prevent elim-trading (players can no longer eliminate each other at the same time).
  • Fixed an issue where players could lose control of the camera.
  • Fixed an issue where sliding visual effects and sound effects would persist after sliding.
  • Fixed an issue where weapons with optics would appear as held higher up compared to those without.
  • Fixed an issue where the light reflection off of weapons would not appear correctly when going around corners.
  • Fixed a visual issue where eliminated players would appear to have full HP in the top Player Bar when spectating.
  • Fixed a visual issue where weapon attachments disappeared when the weapon holder stood too close to a wall.
  • Fixed a visual issue on mobile devices that would sometimes occur when throwing a flashbang.
  • Fixed a visual inconsistency related to weapon recoil.
  • Fixed a typo on the "Time's Up" message at the end of a round.
  • Polished some aspects of animation related to players moving left and right.
  • Implemented several technical performance improvements.

The v34.10 update has arrived! Here’s a summary of the latest changes and bug fixes, including but not limited to:

Bug Fixes

Bug Fixes[]

  • Players can now pin Quests without issues.
  • Players are again respawning at Banner Gadgets as intended.
  • BluGlo Pylons will now properly indicate whether BluGlo has been deposited.
  • The issue causing players to incorrectly appear as a default Battle Royale character when first loading into Save the World has been fixed.
  • The newly tamed animal’s Health Bar will no longer disappear upon mounting.
  • The Sharpened Shadow Pickaxe now has a correct swing animation.
  • Sliding while using the Default Hero Appearance as your Outfit no longer triggers the “Can't perform this action” audio.
