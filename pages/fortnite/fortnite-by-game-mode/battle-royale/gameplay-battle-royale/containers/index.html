Overview

Containers are a special type of item in Fortnite: Battle Royale. They are one of the primary methods of obtaining loot, aside from Floor Loot, Foraging and Eliminating opponents.

Functionality

Functionality[]

Containers can be found at various locations across the map, with varying spawn rates and loot drops. The primary method of obtaining loot from a container is to open it. This is normally done by interacting with the container. However, a majority of containers can simply be destroyed to drop their loot.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Supply Drops are the only containers that do not spawn at the start of a match. Instead, they spawn randomly near the mid-end game, and do not have specific tiles they can land on.
    • This makes them unique to the game, as all other containers have preset spawns and must spawn only where allocated.
    • They also cannot be destroyed, but rather the balloon holding them up can be.
      • Supply Drops can spawn in certain locations, such as the Pawntoon without needing to spawn from a Balloon.
  • In earlier patches of Battle Royale, destroying a chest deleted the loot that it dropped.
  • Despite Chests not currently having a 100% spawn rate, chest spawn locations are visible when at a distance. The chest then disappears on closer inspection.
  • An Elemental Chest was still present on the back of The Great Turtle despite all other Elemental Chests being vaulted.
    • It did not drop any Boons when searched and was removed with the launch of Galactic Battle.
  • Rita's Supply Comet features Message Feed popups for when a player gets Eliminated or Knocked.
