OG[]

The Block is an Unnamed Location in Fortnite OG that returned in Season 7 to the island Athena.

During Season 7, The Block missed Risky Reels by several meters to the north and instead landed on the northern edge of Athena before slipping into the ocean below, leaving behind a large square-shaped crater. During its steady fall The Block still attempted to load props on it, leading to them falling into the ocean and eventually sinking at the end of the season.

One season later, The Block once again missed and landed incorrectly, this time ending up on a mountainside west of Motel.

In Chapter 3: Season 3, The Block returned as The Block 2.0. The Block 2.0 was integrated with Tilted Towers, where three buildings were built using Fortnite: Creative. These building were The Restaurant, The Apartments, and The Shops.

At the start of Chapter 3: Season 3, the island and props for The Block 2.0 were released in Creative for creators who wanted to participate. Submissions lasted until June 15th 2022.

On July 20th, three finalists from each building were selected and voting began on the next day using Donation Stations to fund the buildings. Construction of the buildings were completed on August 16th.

The Block was a Named Location in Fortnite: Battle Royale, that was added in Season 7 to the island Athena. The Block was a giant, flat concrete block, used to display different creations with shared island codes built in the Creative gamemode.

Before Season 8, it was located on the location of Risky Reels as it completely flattened it in a video shown during The Game Awards 2018.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Most (if not all) of the Block islands are no longer playable.
  • On April 2nd 2019, "Mysterious Mansion" (9004-2917-3778) by FurryNenja was removed from The Block for including a suicide scene.
    • An outfit designed by FurryNenja (FuryLeaks, NotFury)[1][2], named Harmony Lee, would later end up getting added to the game for Rainbow Royale 2023. Just like "Mysterious Mansion" it also got removed.
  • The Block was the first non-alliterative named location, followed by Neo Tilted.
  • After the POI's removal, featured blocks that didn't get the chance to be featured were found in the creative hub until Chapter 2: Season 2.
  • The island template can still be found in Fortnite: Creative to this day.
