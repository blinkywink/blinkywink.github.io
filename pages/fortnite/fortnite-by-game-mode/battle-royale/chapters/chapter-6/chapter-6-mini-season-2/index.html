Chapter 6: Mini Season 2, also referred to as Chapter 6: Season MS2 or Season 38, was the thirty-eighth season of Fortnite: Battle Royale. The season started on November 1st 2025 and concluded on November 29th 2025 immediately following the Zero Hour Live Event.

The entire season was a crossover with The Simpsons, chronicling Homer Simpson's misadventures with a shard of the Zero Point. Tie-in animated shorts were released weekly in Fortnite and on Disney+ as part of the story. This season had no proper slogan — promotional material changed the game's name to "Fartnite" (or "Bartnite" in international promotion), and the season was simply named "Fortnite | The Simpsons" in official blog posts.

It was the sixth and final season of Chapter 6, and the final season of Phase 1.

Previously, in Fortnite...

Previously, in Fortnite...[]

A Swarm of mutated insectoids ransacked Oninoshima after a failed attempt by a possessed Daigo to summon the Dark Presence to the Island. A dormant extermination militia known as the Onimorph eXtermination Rangers (O.X.R.) were reactivated to help combat the threat alongside the Island's inhabitants, but they were quickly overwhelmed by the Swarm's rapid spawning, and numerous locations were overrun. Zordon sent the Power Rangers to reinforce O.X.R., who summoned the Megazord to eradicate the Queen bugs and repel the Swarm. Oninoshima began to rebuild in the aftermath, but the threat of the Dark Presence remained — prompting O.X.R., Hope, Jones, and their allies to mobilize and confront it in the Spirit Realm.

Leadup to The Simpsons

Leadup to The Simpsons[]

Main Article: Welcome, Our Alien Overlords

Such plans were shelved when a fissure appeared beside a treehouse that mysteriously sprouted on Oninoshima. A pair of aliens named Kang and Kodos arrived from outer space to investigate, while Hope and Jones rifted to a nearby control center to use an upgraded Rift Device to seal the breach and prevent the Dark Presence from invading. The fissure was momentarily closed before reopening into a larger portal that allowed the demonic creature to cross over.

Story

Story[]

Apocalypse D'oh[]

“
Local dullard wields unimaginable power
”
— Springfield Shopper

As battle erupts, Kang and Kodos became captivated by the Dark Presence, hoping to find a mate to reproduce with. They abduct Hope and Jones to gain the demon master's favor, but Hope overpowers them and takes control of their saucer to attack the Dark Presence. Her attack is parried and the saucer is thrown into an inter-reality wormhole, but Jones alerts Hope to the Zero Point Shard embedded in the Dark Presence's chest — she uses the tractor beam to steal a piece of it and carry it into the wormhole.

Inside the wormhole, Kodos and Hope fight over the saucer's destination: Springfield or Oninoshima. Their constant meddling with the calibration causes multiple realities to fuse with Springfield's, creating Springfield Island. Kodos plots to conquer it, but the Battle Bus — piloted by Otto Mann — crashes into the saucer, and Hope loses the Zero Point Shard in the air.

Back home, Homer Simpson tries to watch TV with an uncooperative remote when the Shard flies into his house, just as Marge tells him to call the police if he ever finds such an object. Hearing about its power and extraterrestrial origins, Homer outfits the Shard into his remote, turning it into a reality-bending conduit that he quickly learns to wield. Although Marge raises concerns, Homer wins her over by using the remote to change his hairstyle. He sets off on a quest to achieve "nirvana," using the Shard remote to improve the lives of Springfield's residents.

At Moe's, Homer learns from Moe that Hope and Jones are looking for him regarding the Shard. To keep them distracted, Moe serves them pretzels that Homer had modified with the remote to be sentient and force themselves into eaters' mouths.

Hope and the Looper had been cleaning up Homer's mess caused by his Shard usage, which led some Springfielders to accuse them of being from Shelbyville. The Looper confronts Homer, who refuses to relinquish the Shard — though he offers to reconsider if they bring him a Slap Juice-flavored Squishee. As part of the delivery, they air his "complaints" at the Town Hall by causing a ruckus and fleeing before authorities arrive. While Hope scours the Simpsons residence for clues among various remotes, Homer receives his Squishee from the Looper but decides to keep the Shard, wanting to improve Springfield and be idolized for it. Hope concludes that Homer is oblivious to the Shard's dangers and resolves to continue undoing his mess with the Looper.

Sugar High[]

“
Delicious Destruction: Dieters cautioned to remain indoors
”
— Springfield Shopper

Professor Frink examines Homer's Shard Remote and informs him about the power of the Zero Point, declaring it the most dangerous force in the Omniverse. Frink wanted to study the Shard to achieve notoriety and Nobel Prize consideration by publishing research, but relents when Homer insists on keeping it — in part because Homer had already turned his robo-caller into a "ladybot." Hearing this, Homer transforms Frink into a walking banana so that he can perform research on himself in hopes of Nobel attention.

Upon spotting a donut-shaped cloud, Homer instinctively uses the Remote to turn it into a giant pink frosted sprinkled donut, which falls from the sky. Aiming to provide his fellow Springfielders with an endless food supply, Homer creates a "food rain" — supersized pizzas, donuts, pies, and fried chicken drumsticks continuously raining down on the Island. Rather than delighting his people, Homer instead incites chaos as the food destroys homes and establishments, turning Springfield against him. With the Looper's help, Frink and Ned Flanders rallied numerous Springfielders — including Moe, Krusty, and Mayor Quimby — at the Town Hall, where they denounce Homer's actions and agree to stop him.

The Looper delivers an angry letter from the Springfield mob to Homer at the Nuclear Power Plant, which he struggles to comprehend due to his poor reading. He removes the Zero Point Shard from the Remote and takes it to the nuclear core, throwing it into a fuel pool to dispose of the evidence. As he steps back to sit down, Homer spots a bar of unfinished candy beside the pool — but accidentally falls into the vat while reaching for it. The reaction between Zero Point energy and radiation triggers an explosion in the core, splitting Homer's essence into an army of mindless pink clones set loose on Springfield.

Multiplidiocy[]

“
"Creepy Clone Chaos: Multiple morons make mayhem"
”
— Springfield Shopper

Back at the Simpsons house, Bart and Lisa find Homer clones at their doorstep. Chief Wiggum, on the scene, uses a pair of laser pistols to eliminate the clone army — dissolving them into pink gas that wafts into the air. Instead of dissipating, the gases coalesce into a giant pink storm cloud. Knowing Homer's essence is inside the cloud, the Simpson children attempt to provoke a response through over-annoyance. Their antics include repeatedly chanting "Are you there yet?", Lisa playing her saxophone from the rooftop of Moe's, and Bart enlisting the Looper to masquerade as El Barto and vandalize Springfield. They also visit Krusty Burger and Lard Lad in search of vegan options for Homer.

When Homer fails to respond to their antics and the children run out of ideas, they join Hope, Marge, and the others in cleansing Springfield of the Homer clones. As more and more are eliminated, the clouds containing Homer's essence condense and reconstitute into a kaiju version of Homer — left wearing nothing but his underwear. Hope discovers the Shard is hidden inside Homer's body, the source of his colossal transformation. Lisa theorizes that this version of her dad could be a "force for good," only for the mindless kaiju to immediately go on a destructive rampage at the Town Square before fleeing for the hills — taking the statue of Jebediah Springfield with him.

The Incredible Bulk[]

“
"Big idiot now bigger idiot"
”
— Springfield Shopper

Following the trail of footsteps, Hope, Jones, Marge, Bart, Lisa, and Peely locate the embiggened Homer off Springfield's shores. Since the Zero Point Shard is inside Homer's stomach, Hope suggests vaporizing his frontal lobe to immobilize him, then retrieving the Shard via a strike team operation through his bowels. An appalled Marge suggests a more empathetic approach to catch her husband's attention — which Hope and Jones are forced to adopt after Hope's plan proves ineffective.

The Looper visits Professor Frink, who declares that they must take his greatest inventions and improve upon them to solve the crisis. He provides a complex schematic for a phone with an eerily familiar ringtone, then sends them to retrieve the Sarcasm Machine and Hamburger Earmuffs. The Looper then helps Marge collect family photos to plaster on a billboard in front of Homer and restore his sense of humanity. Originally displaying an ordinance outlawing donuts, the photos obscure the text to read "DO IT FOR US" — catching Homer's attention. He burps the Zero Point Shard out of his body and onto Springfield Island, where the Looper collects it and hands it over to Hope and Jones. Hope gets to work using Frink's devices alongside the Rift Device, while Jones handles the Shard with a pair of oven mitts.

Even with the Shard removed, Homer remains colossal due to lingering Zero Point energy in his body. Frink arrives and attempts to use a "de-bigulator" blaster to shrink Homer, but the Zero Point energy resists its effects. Marge tells Homer he must clean up the mess he created on Springfield — he suggests using the Shard, but Marge insists he leave it in Hope's care. Hope gets the idea to bring Homer back to Oninoshima to help confront the Dark Presence, and an aloof Homer relents after Marge reminds him he still needs to clean the gutters at home.

The End of Chapter 6

The End of Chapter 6[]

Main Article: Zero Hour

Hope used parts from the Sarcasm Machine and Hamburger Earmuffs to upgrade the Rift Device, which would serve as a housing for the Shard. While Homer slept in the Town Square, Jones and Hope used the Shard to summon Rift Beacons across numerous realities within the Omniverse, rallying those who answered the call into battle against the Dark Presence. Hope and Jones were unwillingly sent on a detour through several realities due to an improperly calibrated Rift Device before arriving back at Oninoshima with Homer to lead innumerable bands of heroes — including Godzilla, Kong, Superman, HUNTR/X, the Power Rangers, and an alliance between the Resistance and the Galactic Empire, among many others.

The fight quickly turned for the worse as the heroes struggled to keep pace with the Dark Presence, and Homer was pinned to the ground by a tentacle. A desperate Jones rifted a red Corruption Cube to the battlefield, which the Mecha Team Leader used to vanquish the Dark Presence. With the demon master's defeat, Homer returned to his normal size and was sent back to his home reality along with the rest of the summoned heroes, as the liberated Zero Point Shard produced a reality wave that purified Oninoshima and sealed it off from the Spirit Realm. Hope and Jones departed Oninoshima for a new Island — but on their way, a mysterious spacecraft shot down the Battle Bus.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Mark Rein's teaser word for Chapter 6: Mini Season 2 was "Shard-a-ma-jig".[1]
  • Chapter 6: Mini Season 2...
    • ...was the first mini season to introduce an entirely new Battle Royale Island.
    • ...was the first end-of-chapter mini season to not revisit an Island from a previous chapter.
      • Chapter 4: Season OG was set in Athena, Chapter 1's island.
      • Chapter 2 Remix was set in Apollo, Chapter 2's island.
    • ...was the fourth season to have a Battle Pass centered entirely around a collaboration.
      • Coincidentally, all four were collaborations with a Disney property: Chapter 2: Season 4 and Chapter 5: Season 4 were Marvel collaborations, Chapter 6: Mini Season 1 was a Star Wars collaboration, and this season was The Simpsons.
      • It was also the first collaboration Battle Pass to not feature cosmetics of an exclusive rarity.
    • ...marked Chapter 6 as the first chapter since Chapter 2 to have more than 5 seasons, and the first chapter to have more than 1 mini season.
    • ...is the shortest season of Chapter 6, and is tied with Chapter 2 Remix for the shortest season in Battle Royale history, lasting 28 days.
    • ...was the first season to have a maximum player count below 100, set at 80.
      • The maximum player count was further reduced to 60 during the "No Sweat Weekend" on the weekend of November 21st.
    • ...was the first season set on a Reload-sized island.
    • ...had the first Battle Pass to not feature any Lobby Music or Jam Tracks in the standard rewards track since the former's introduction in Season 6.
    • ...had the first Battle Pass to exclusively feature traversal Emotes in the standard rewards track.
    • ...had the first Battle Pass to not grant an Outfit as the first reward immediately after purchase — instead granting the Peels sidekick.
    • ...was the last season of Battle Royale to use the gameplay UI introduced in Chapter 3: Season 2.
    • ...was the first season to not have a Ranked Back Bling since Ranked's introduction in Chapter 4: Season 2.
    • ...was the first season where all major Bosses spoke in the player's chosen language instead of the fictional language of Fortlish, as their respective voice actors reprised their roles from The Simpsons.
  • As part of the Fortnite crossover, two episodes from Season 37 of The Simpsons featured Fortnite-inspired couch gags:
    • "Bad Boys... For Life?" sees the Simpson family come home to find Jones, Hope, Meowscles, Fishstick, and Springfielder Peely sitting on their couch. Peely slips and falls as the rest flee, then is eviscerated alive and turned into a banana split sundae eaten by the family.
    • The second couch gag cuts away from the usual intro, instead showing the Simpson family disembarking from the Otto Mann-piloted Battle Bus and skydiving toward Springfield Island. Whereas everyone else lands smoothly, Homer crashes through the ceiling of the living room from above.
  • To promote the season ahead of an official announcement, a select number of content creators were given early access by Epic Games on October 31st, 2025 to showcase the season's gameplay, Battle Pass, and the new Sidekicks feature.
    • This marks the first time content creators were granted early access to a Battle Royale season.
  • To promote the "Multiplidiocy" short for Week 3, a large number of actors costumed as Homer Simpson and Peelfessor Frink were unleashed on the streets of Santa Monica, California and other parts of the Los Angeles Metropolitan Area. Accompanying them was Matt Groening, the creator of The Simpsons.
