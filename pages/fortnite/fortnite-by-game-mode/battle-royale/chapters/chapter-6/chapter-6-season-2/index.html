Chapter 6: Season 2, also known as Season 34, was the thirty-fourth season of Fortnite: Battle Royale, and the second season of Chapter 6. It began on February 21st 2025 and ended on May 2nd, 2025.

The season's main slogan is Lawless (stylized as LAWLESS) with a theme around crime (particularly robbery) and gold.

Previously, in Fortnite...

Previously, in Fortnite...[]

A shard of the Zero Point followed Hope, Jones, and the Looper out of a wormhole, crashing into Oninoshima's surface and creating a shockwave that rejuvenated the Island's magic. Demon Portals were reactivated as a result, leading Hope to ally with Island natives Kendo and Jade. Not long after, Jade ended up trapped in the Spirit Realm due to a demonic curse that was accidentally worsened by a mask made by her brother Daigo. Kendo and the Looper investigated the impact crater and found fragments of the shard, using them to craft magical blades. Meanwhile, Hope discovered the shard, in possession of a mysterious entity in the Spirit Realm, has energy signatures identical to Pandora's Box.

Leadup to Season 2

Leadup to Season 2[]

Main article: The Confrontation Event

Kendo and the Looper had given the magical blades to the Night Rose, that they temporarily freed from the Puppeteer. She later gave those blades to Daigo, who engaged the demon leader Shogun X in a duel to save Jade. Although seriously wounded, Daigo successfully eliminated Shogun X, causing his essence to flow back into the Demon Portal. Overwhelmed, the portal exploded and splattering liquid gold across the mountainside. At the same time, the Night Rose was finally freed from his influence for good, as it was revealed shortly before the confrontation that it was Shogun X who connected her to the Puppeteer. The latter's death then broke the curse. After all that, Daigo was nowhere to be found afterwards and Jade believed the presence of the gold would attract chaos to the Island.

Storyline

Storyline[]

After The Confrontation Event, the Spirit Realm portal began emitting golden energy, drastically altering the surrounding area. Around the same time, Baron Fletcher Kane, an old acquaintance of Midas, saw an opportunity and seized control of the newfound gold. To exploit this resource, Kane founded Alpha Mining Company, securing a monopoly over gold extraction in Shiny Shafts Mine. With his growing wealth, he established multiple banks across towns and major cities, including one in Crimson City, a city founded by him. His ambitions didn't stop there—he also constructed a luxurious resort and a grand mansion, cementing his status as the Island's crime kingpin. However, his vast reserves of gold soon attracted the attention of rival criminals, who flocked to Crimson City, turning it into a hotbed of lawlessness. As crime surged, the city's identity shifted, earning it the new name: Crime City. A brutal power struggle erupted between Kane and the criminal underworld, igniting an all-out war for gold.

To support outlaws efforts, Skillet, Joss, and Keisha Cross set up black markets throughout the Island. These hubs not only provided criminals with weapons and equipment but also became meeting points where outlaws could gather, relax, and forge new alliances. One of these new alliances was Midas, a legend among many like Cassidy,[1] going around helping people for free, which catches some questions from people such as Joss[2] and Skillet about what he's actually planning.[3]

Meanwhile, Skillet, dubbing the name King of the Boom, slightly returns out of retirement here and there and decides to himself that he should use his explosive plans to blow Lonewolf Lair into ruins after robbing him of all his wealth,[4] including making plans for this bombing at the black market of Crime City.[5] Joss on the other hand, intends on discovering how Oninoshima's magic is just science that is yet to be understood.[6]

Wanted: Skillet[]

The Looper finds themselves contacted by Skillet for a big gig. Skillet has them prove their worth by acquiring gold, granting them the nickname "Ratso". The Looper opens up a bank vault with Thermite and collects its treasure, serving as an explosive test for Skillet. Together, they sabotage Fletcher Kane's payphones to limit reinforcements, allowing Valentina, the so-called Robber Queen, to break into Kane's personal vault, while The Looper distracts the Alpha Guards on standby. They discover but ultimately decide to leave the collars and puppy chew toys, but split the gold between them.

Wanted: Joss[]

Joss, fascinated by chemistry and other sciences, refuses to believe what the people say about the magic surrounding her, thinking that there must be a scientific explanation for it all, including the seemingly miraculous birth of Big Dill and the gold spreading from the Spirit Realm portal. Noticing The Looper wasn't like other Outlaws, she contracted them to help her during her research. She first sends them to talk to Big Dill, in order to study him. Upon talking to Big Dill, The Looper help them to collect a few records and opening some bottles of brine, to get the party started over at Crime City and see him in his "natural habitat". Joss ultimately concludes Dillinger's just a regular party animal stuck in a briny body.

Later, Joss assumes that she might get evidences of gold's impact on people out of DNA samples, and sent The Looper to "recover" DNA samples from people who were absolutely not attacked with assault rifles. Finding no contamination samples within their DNA, she sent The Looper recover mineral samples with the Plasma Burst Laser to crumble stone structures. Noting them to be nearly perfect, she then asked The Looper to return at her black market at Magic Mosses in order to analyse the mineral samples. However, The Looper's analysis shows that it is simply a normal gold ore, leaving Joss dumbfounded. However, she still doesn't believe in magic and thinks there is another explanation.

Baron Escalation Tax[]

Baron alpha wolf Fletcher Kane, wanting to increase his wealth, took control over the Outlaws' Vending Machine mechanisms and applied a 100% tax increase onto previously sold items. However, the Black Market shops have remained exactly the same, with Keisha Cross considering it as a community good, especially after the outrageous Baron tax.[7]

Golden Gunslinger & rebellion against Kane[]

The legendary criminal, Midas, rises again in order to take down his old acquaintance, Fletcher Kane. He and his loyal right-hand man, Brutus, join forces with the Outlaws running the Black Market, in order to supply more weapons. The Golden King contacted The Looper, who had impressed him back in Chapter 5: Season 2, and gave them missions to test their abilities. Recognizing their worth, Midas decided to ally himself with The Looper, however, he did not trust them. In the meantime, The Outlaws all over the Island rebel against Fletcher Kane, following his taxes. Growing in fame and reverence, Midas gains his new title, The Golden Gunslinger.

Wanted: Midas[]

After a significant blow was dealt to Kane's multiple vaults, The Looper gained membership in the Outlaw Faction, and Midas contacted them again. He first tested whether he could trust them by having them do SHADOW Briefings, and then sent them to bribe the Outlaws with bars, before making them uniting the Outlaws under him against Fletcher Kane to ensure their loyalty. With The Looper's loyalty proven, Midas gave them a mission. He told them he had a hunch, and that The Looper had to confirm it by sending them analyze the residual energy traces near the broken Spirit Realm portal. Midas told them that if he was correct, both he and The Looper should recognize this mysterious energy as this is linked to their common past in Reality Zero. As The Looper used the sensor backback, they discovered energy traces of The Zero Point.

Midas later sent The Looper in Daigo's lair which had recently returned to activity in order to steal a copy of his mask making book, feeling the Mask Maker also wanted The Zero Shard. For unknown reason, Daigo associated himself with Fletcher Kane and supplied his army with masks of powers. Midas and The Looper later had a discussion, in which Midas revealed a lot of people wanted the shard, including Daigo and Fletcher Kane and admitted that he also wanted it for his own ambitions, but that for the moment, The Looper's and his interests converged: stopping Daigo and Fketcher Kane from getting the Shard. He even agreed to have to work with Jones and Hope, The Looper's allies, if necessary.

Legendary Outlaw: The Looper[]

The Looper continued to work on uniting the Outlaw, becoming a renowned Outlaw in the process and gaining access to the Legendary Outlaw Keycard of the Outlaw Faction, allowing them to benefit from all the Outlaws services. They again got a talk with Midas. In this discussion Midas recognized the extent to which uniting the outlaws with so much disparate perspective was a thankless work, but told them that nevertheless if the momentum could be maintained, Kane's days as Baron would be numbered, as surprisingly the "team" work very well together. Midas then thanked The Looper for their assistance.[8]

Wanted: Keisha Cross[]

As Cassidy Quinn is preparing a heist as Outlaws were ordered by Midas to keep Kane bothered and busy, her best friend Keisha Cross contacts The Looper. Testing their abilities first, she send them help Cassidy. Agreeing to help Cassidy steal Kane's personal Whiplash, both go to his personal garage, whose keys Cassidy had already stolen. The Looper and Cassidy enter the car, with the former taking the wheel, and run away with it, while talking.

Later, The Looper went to spy on Kane's communication. Spying on communication, they hear Kane react to their recent heist with Cassidy, then discover things such as Kane delivering gold to Daigo, Midas being on the move and a mysterious machine being installed at Daigo's workshop.

Espionage continues[]

Continuing to spy on Kane's communications, The Looper learns about the latter wishing to put an end to Midas. Then, they learn move about his involvement with Daigo, the latter being given enough authority to command Alpha guards, and his workshop being protected by them. They also learn of the arrangement between the two: Fletcher provides Daigo with gold, so that he can give him the power he promised him.

Wanted: Baron[]

Midas sent The Looper to investigate Kane's plan, first sending them to Lonewolf Lair, where they found files concerning research on the Zero Point Shard, which worried them. They then searched Kane's vault, noticing the significant absence of bars. They then found a mysterious mask hanging on a wall, which The Looper quickly made the connection.

Midas then asked The Looper to gear up with suitable equipment to take Kane down, as part of his plan to take him down because he needed The Looper alive in order to win. After that, he sent them to take down Kane's lieutenants, knowing they were carrying orders, and hoping that one of them had received secret orders. Reading the orders, they realized that the gold was being given to a secret collaborator, whose identity Midas easily guessed.

Seeking to verify if his intuition was correct, Midas sent The Looper to attack the convoys to find out where they were headed. Learning it was indeed the Mask Workshop, The Looper was sent investigating it. Arriving at the scene, the dark fog made their skin tingle, and they guessed that the Island itself wasn't happy with what Daigo had done there. Descending into the secret lair, they found the dark mask-making machine, which seemed to have been used for something sinister. Then, continuing their search, The Looper discovered what Daigo had done with Kane's bars and went to report to Midas.

Reporting their discoveries to Midas, the latter understood Kane and Daigo's plan: Daigo had been supplying Alpha with masks and promised Kane ultimate power. With Kane's gold, Daigo made a mask powerful enough to breach the spirit realm and collect the Zero Shard. While noting Daigo will likely betray Kane if he succeeds, Midas notes that they absolutely must be stopped, even if it meant working with The Looper's "hero" friends.

Daigo's Fate[]

Daigo and Kane soon relocated to a remote riverbank in Magic Mosses, where they restored a lone Demon Portal that had been dormant since Shogun X's defeat. With Kane acting as his bodyguard, Daigo began to work his magic on the portal in order to break into the Spirit Realm, fully convinced that only he can save the Island. As he fondled with the Spirit Realm, a beam of energy rose into the sky, slowly intensifying over time. There, the Looper fought and defeated Kane, but they could not stop Daigo from entering the Spirit Realm. The dark presence inside the Spirit Realm began to corrupt Daigo, turning him into a demon.

One night, hip hop duo Tasty Bois broke into Daigo's shop in Masked Meadows to steal treasures when they came across one of Daigo's masks. Big Dill picked it up and began to wear it, which caused him to experience visions of Daigo's fate in the Spirit Realm. Before the duo could flee, the now-demonic Daigo appeared behind them.

Daigo's actions had also attracted the attention of a mysterious spacestation, which entered atmospheric orbit and began to draw itself closer to the Island.

Map Updates

Map Updates[]

Update v34.00

February 21st 2025

  • Crime has populated the western side of the Island as a new biome overflowing with gold appeared on the western side of the Island, bringing new Named Locations:
    • Crime City (replacing Warrior's Watch)
    • Lonewolf Lair (replacing Lost Lake)
    • Outlaw Oasis (replacing Nightshift Forest)
    • Shiny Shafts (replacing Twinkle Terrace)
  • Shattered Span finished re-construction, restoring it as Shining Span.
  • BURD was removed.
  • Added new Landmarks:
    • Predator Peak, a mountain housing the Frozen Portal, now covered in gold stains and with the top being carved into a giant wolf sculpture. The portal itself has broken into pieces.
    • Predator Peak Overlook, a small souvenir shop located not far from Predator Peak.
    • Three banks owned by Alpha: Crime City Bank, Seaport City Bank and Masked Motorway Bank.
    • Three Black Markets: Boom Hole Black Market, Rouge Repairs Black Market (replacing BURD To Go near Rolling Blossoms Farm), and Backwoods Black Market.
  • Smash N' Grab Jewelry Stores owned by Alpha got added: one between Pumped Power and BURD To Go, one in Hopeful Heights, one in Yōkina Boardwalk, one in Whiffy Wharf and one in Canyon Crossing.
  • Everything in Daigo's hideout in the sewers under Masked Meadows got packed up.
  • 3 mines around the map are under construction.

Update v34.10

March 11th 2025

  • A server was constructed inside a cave north of Shogun's Solitude.
  • Construction of other 2 mines continued.
  • The locked rooms in all three Black Markets storing Exotic and Mythic weapons were updated to be accessible with the Outlaw Keycard.
  • Daigo's workshop has relit and the machine has been reactivated.
  • The Thousand Faces' Broken Mask reappeared in Daigo's workshop.
  • Alpha Bars and suitcase were added to Daigo's workshop.

Update v34.20

March 25th 2025

  • A second server was constructed south of Outlaw Oasis.
  • Construction of Mine Entrance 3 continued.
  • More Alpha Bars were added to Daigo's workshop.
  • The Broken Mask is no longer in display case.
  • Lonewolf Lair garage was updated to fit a custom Whiplash in there.

Update v34.21

April 1st 2025

  • Scorpion arrives to the Island as a Boss, plunging the Island into Kombat at one of three new Landmarks:
    • The Pit - east of Shining Span
    • The Dead Pool - under Masked Meadows
    • The Living Forest - replacing Sleepwalker's Peril.
  • Smash N' Grab Jewelry Stores and Dill Bits Mining Servers are now marked on the map. The machinery in the servers now has a chance of dropping Dill Bits.
  • The latter also recived the Landmark statuses, becoming Mine Entrance 1 and Mine Entrance 2 respectfully.

Update v34.30

April 8th 2025

  • A third server was constructed west of Foxy Floodgate.
  • Dark magic fog re-appeared around The Thousand Faces.
  • The Broken Mask has been repaired with kintsugi gold. The mask is glowing with dark magic and it will occasionally make sinister voices and whispers.

April 15th 2025

  • Daigo has activated the mask and the Spirit Realm Portal. He is accompanied and protected by Fletcher Kane. Daigo attempts to open the gate at the beginning of every match, when the Battle Bus arrives.

April 16th 2025

  • The dark magic void beam has intensified, sending out some small energy sparks.

April 17th 2025

  • The dark magic void beam has intensified, sending out some big energy sparks.

April 18th 2025

  • The dark magic void beam has intensified, sending out huge shockwaves, dark thunders and earthquakes.

April 19th 2025

  • Death Star appeared in the sky.
    • Over the course of the next few weeks it got closer and closer to the Island.

Update v34.40

May 1st 2025

  • The dark magic void beam has intensified, creating a spiral of energy around the beam as it blasts into the sky, along with the thunder crackling more violently.
  • Daigo has completely powered up the Spirit Realm Portal and now runs inside it wielding the Splinter Blades upon Fletcher Kane's defeat.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Mark Rein's teaser word for Chapter 6: Season 2 was 'Pickled'.[9]
  • Marketing for Chapter 6: Season 2 revolves around a fictional rap beef between Big Dill and veteran rapper T-Pain.
    • This could be in reference to the Drake-Kendrick Lamar feud, which notably erupted in 2024.
  • The season's tagline, "LAWLESS", ties into the Mortal Kombat collaboration as it riffs off the iconic phrase "Flawless Victory".
  • Chapter 6: Season 2 is the first season in Chapter 6 to feature weapons in Reload.
  • Chapter 6: Season 2 is the only season in Chapter 6 without a Transformation Event.
