Shotguns

Overview

Shotguns are a type of weapon in Fortnite: Battle Royale. The purpose of these weapons is for short range combat. All Shotguns use Shells.

Functionality

Functionality[]

Shotguns fill the role of a short range weapon and are often used for up close engagements, due to their high damage per shot. Shotguns uniquely fire a spread of pellets rather than a single shot. Swapping between two shotguns after firing results in a small cooldown to prevent the player dealing too much damage per second. Shotguns are very weak at longer ranges and oftentimes cannot even hit a player at medium range.

Strategy Guide

Strategy Guide[]

This section outlines a strategy.
Feel free to add tips, tricks and general advice about the topic.
  • Many Shotguns load one shell at a time. This means that if you are careful, you can interrupt your reload in order to shoot at an opponent if you are unable to complete a full reload. Some shotguns load multiple shells at once, such as the Combat Shotgun, meaning the same is applicable but with two shots.
  • The Combat Shotgun is more of a medium ranged weapon than a close ranged one, meaning it should be handled differently to the other shotguns.
  • Though cross-hairs show where the spread will hit, players should still rely on the center of the reticle when aiming, otherwise they risk only getting a partial shot in, thus dealing out sometimes much lower damage.
  • Some shotguns are inaccurate in general, so (this works with all guns) crouch, stay still, and aim down sights to get your maximum accuracy and minimum spread.
  • The Dub and Striker Pump Shotgun will shoot faster when you swap between several of them in your inventory, rather than waiting for the delay between shots to pass.
  • For slow firing shotguns like the Pump Shotgun, it is best to follow up with an Assault Weapon or SMG to eliminate an opponent if you deal enough damage with the shotgun. This is not needed for shotguns with a fast fire rate like the Auto Shotgun.
Trivia

Trivia[]

  • In season prior to Season 5, shotguns, especially the Pump Shotgun, could have been swapped quickly without needing to 'pump' the weapon. This allowed for much higher than intended damage per second outputs, and so a delay was added between swapping guns and firing.
  • In Update v20.30, every single shotgun was reworked. Every pellet now does higher damage and headshot damage. To combat these adjustments, every shotgun was given a maximum damage cap, which affects the highest possible damage able to be delivered in a single shot in the case that all pellets hit. Due to these changes, the Legendary Dragon's Breath Shotgun, the Epic/Legendary Charge Shotgun, and the Mythic Havoc Pump Shotgun are (currently) the only shotguns able to deal 200 or more damage.
    • Using the Legendary Pump Shotgun as an example, the weapon does 128 base damage with a headshot multiplier of x1.85. Theoretically the shotgun would dish out 236.8 damage to the head. Assuming that the receiving player has full health, shield, and overshield, a single shot WOULD be enough to eliminate a player in build mode, while heavily weakening a player in zero build with only 13.2 health remaining. Because of the damage cap, however, the maximum headshot is instead 185 damage.
    • This is the first time that an entire weapon class was changed in a single update.
    • The only shotguns currently unaffected by these changes are the OG and Remastered Heavy Shotgun, the OG version of the Pump Shotgun, and the OG version of the Tactical Shotgun.
