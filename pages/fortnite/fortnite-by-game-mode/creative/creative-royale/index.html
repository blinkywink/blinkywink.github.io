Creative Royale was a feature in Fortnite: Creative. It allowed creators to create their own rules on the Battle Royale island.

Overview

Overview[]

Creative Royale was the successor to Battle Lab. Introduced in Update v22.30, it allows you to customize the Battle Royale Island with a select group of pre-existing and unique devices, such as the Melee Designer (which allows for the creation of custom melee weapon) and the Team Settings & Inventory (which allows you to assign unique settings to different team members).

Creative Royale was accessed in the same way as a normal Creative island. To make a new Creative Royale island, select the Battle Royale Island template island.

Late Game Arena and Big Battle are two official modes created in Creative Royale.

Restrictions[]

The Creative Royale island updates alongside Battle Royale Island. Because of this, players cannot place any props or builds on the island, and only certain devices can be placed (usually ones that can remotely control gameplay).

Additionally, many features are completely unavailable in Creative Royale, such as Mud, Rift Encounters, Characters, and more.

Some items do not spawn or drop properly in Creative Royale, such as Keycards, rendering features like Vaults inaccessible. Additionally, some items that are not in the creative menu normally such as Ocean’s Bottomless Chug Jug or Midas’ Drum Gun will completely disappear if dropped from the player inventory.

Bars are replaced with Gold in Creative Royale. However, some features, such as Rerolling Augments, were not updated to be compatible with gold and are thus unusable.

Island Options

Island Options[]

Creative Royale allows you to Customize Island Settings. As well as having the Default Options, it also has a few bonus options, including:

  • Basic Options
  • Storm Options
  • Deployment Options

Basic Island Options[]

Option Value Description
Target Bots Yes, No Determines whether Target Bots spawn at the start of the game. Target Bots participate in matches to fill out teams. Target Bots ignore some My Island settings, such as Respawn and Glider Deployment Height.
Bosses Spawn Yes, No Determines whether Bosses, such as The Ageless, spawn. This does not affect Guards.
Vehicles Enabled Yes, No Determines whether vehicles spawn on the island at the start of the game.
Wildlife Enabled Yes, No Determines whether wildlife spawns on the island during the game.
Victory Screen Battle Royale, Creative, Custom This Option allows you to Customize the Victory Royale screen. "Custom" gives some advanced options to allow for detailed customization of the screen.

Storm Options[]

For a full list of options, see Storm Caller

Option Value Description
Number of Storm Phases 10, Pick a number Determines how many phases your BR Island's storm will have.
Initial Storm Radius 2500 meters, Pick a radius Determines the radius size of your storm, in meters. Use the arrows to choose a distance, or click in the field to type in a number.
Edit Storm Phase All, Phase 1, Phase 2, Phase 3, Phase 4, Phase 4, Phase 5, Phase 6, Phase 7, Phase 8, Phase 9, Phase 10 By default, options for all 10 phases are shown. If you choose a specific phase to edit (such as Phase 1), only the options for that phase are displayed.
Phase Resize Time 60 seconds, Pick a number This is the amount of time it takes the storm to resize. Use the arrows to choose a number of seconds, or click in the field to type in a number.
Phase Damage Percent 0, 1%, Pick a percent This is the amount of damage the selected phase inflicts on players, as a percentage of their maximum health.

Deployment Options[]

For a full list of options, see Deployment Console

Option Value Description
Spawn Type Battle Bus, Rift Determines whether players deploy from a Battle Bus or spawn from a Rift.
Spawn Battle Bus for Each Team Off, On Only displays if you choose Battle Bus for the Spawn by Battle Bus or Rift option. Determines if each team deploys from their own Battle Bus, or if everyone deploys from the same Battle Bus.
Battle Bus Speed 50 m/s, Pick a speed Only displays if you choose Battle Bus for the Spawn by Battle Bus or Rift option. Determines the speed of the Battle Bus. Click the arrows to choose an angle, or click in the field to type in an amount.

Group Teams On, Off Only displays if you choose Rift for the Spawn by Battle Bus or Rift option. By default, team members will spawn near each other. If you choose Off, players will spawn randomly regardless of what team they are on.
Force Glider Deployment Height 50 meters, Pick a distance The height at which the player's glider is forcibly deployed.

Strategy

Strategy[]

This section outlines a strategy.
Feel free to add tips, tricks and general advice about the topic.

Here are some tips to consider when creating an island in Creative Royale:

  • You can create a custom loot pool by setting the loot pool to "none" and then use supply drops or some other mechanic (such as destroying props) to drop your loot. Setting loot to "none" and using item granters does not work as chests will become unsearchable.
History

History[]

Chapter 3: Season 4[]

  • Update v22.30: Added Creative Royale (Currently in Beta)
  • Update v22.40: Added the Health Powerup and Melee Designer to Creative Royale.

Chapter 4: Season 1[]

  • Update v23.00: Changed the Creative Royale island from Artemis to Asteria. Added the Custom Victory Royale screen options. Removed time of day and camera filter options temporarily due to compatibility issues.
  • Update v23.10: You can now set which wildlife and vehicles spawn individually, as well as the "vehicles spawns" and "wildlife spawns" options.
  • Update v23.20: Added the Accolade and Switch Devices to Creative Royale.

Chapter 4: Season 2[]

  • You can now fly across the island in Creative Royale.
  • Added more devices, such as the Guard Spawner, Floor Loot Proxy, Vehicle Proxy, and more.
  • Added options to allow Loot Island and Loot Caches to spawn.

Chapter 4: Season OG[]

  • Changed the map from Asteria to Athena.

Chapter 5: Season 1[]

  • V28.00 Creative Royale has been disabled. Reason is unknown.

Chapter 5: Season 2[]

  • V29.40: Creative Royale has been removed from the game permanently due to resources shifting support to UEFN and Creative.
Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Quests could not be completed in Creative Royale.
    • However, players could have used the Tracker and Accolades devices to make their own quests.
  • Support-a-Creator monetization was disabled on Creative Royale Islands, due to the Battle Royale Island being Epic Games' IP.
