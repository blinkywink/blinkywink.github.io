Overview


Ventures is a seasonal feature in Fortnite: Save the World in which Players can re-experience level and resource progression as they gain Venture XP and complete Venture Quests and climb to the top of each new Ventures season. Each season takes place in a seasonal zone with new and unique modifiers to tackle. There are currently six different Venture zones.

Quests

Quests[]

Venture Quests are a set of Quests in Fortnite: Save the World that allow the player to earn Venture XP, and Superchargers and Core RE-PERK! rewards in the later zones, upon completion.

Types

Types[]

Venture Quests are categorized into 5 types:

Directive

Directive Quests are issued by Director Riggs, and require the player to complete certain missions such as Fight the Storm. Resupply or even Mission Alerts in a Venture Zone.

Destroy

Destroy Quests are issued by Dennis, and require the player to destroy specific objects. They are marked on the minimap with yellow exclamation marks.

Weapon

Weapon Quests are issued by Cassie "Clip" Lipman, and require the player to get eliminations with certain weapon classes in a Venture Zone.

Eliminate

Eliminate Quests are issued by Major Oswald, and require the player to eliminate certain types of husks and a certain number of them.

Locating

Locating Quests are issued by Lars, and require the player to locate certain landmarks such as Suburban Locations and Rural Areas.

Weapons

Weapons[]

Weapon Crafting is disabled in Ventures, and Commanders start with a few basic starter weapons. Vending Machines with season specific loot pools can be found to acquire Weapons in exchange for harvested Materials. The level of weapons in Vending Machines depends on the recommended Power Level of your mission. Repair Benches can be found to repair your weapons.

Leveling

Leveling[]

Ventures has its own progression similar to the Battle Pass in Battle Royale where your Venture Level determines your F.O.R.T. stats and Power Level during the season. Venture XP is used to increase your Venture Level. Superchargers can be obtained in this path of progression along with a and a Hero Voucher. Additional rewards such as Legendary Troll Stash Llama Tokens can also be obtained.

Mission Alert are missions with a timer icon next to them. They grant one-time bonus rewards that include bonus Venture XP.

Mission Badges can be earned by increasing Combat, Building and Utility Scores. They increase the base Venture XP that can be rewarded from a mission.

Mission Badges
  • Exploration Badge
    • Explore the entire map while in a mission.
  • Building Badge
    • Building Score - Build or upgrade structures. Lead Husks to activate your Traps.
  • Combat Badge
    • Combat Score - Eliminate or deal damage to Husks.
  • Utility Badge
    • Utility Score - Craft ammo, destroy objects, search containers, and activate and destroy Llama Fragments.
  • Survivor Badge
    • Rescue a Survivor while in a mission.

Intro[]

To efficiently and legitimately reach level 50 within 5-10 days (or within 25-45 hours):

  1. Complete all Mission Alert, starting with Red or Orange difficulty missions.
  2. Collect 12 BluGlo to increase mission difficulty to Insanity++.
  3. Try to get at least Bronze Badge in Combat, Building, Utility and Exploration.
  4. Aim to complete your Venture Challenge Quests.
  5. If no viable Mission Alerts are available, then repeatedly complete the highest level 4-Player Recommended missions.

Why do players love/hate ventures?

  • Seasonal level reset; starting over is fresh!
  • Separate inventory; must farm to progress
  • Can't craft weapons; forced to try non-meta weapons.
  • Build Loadouts based on hero abilities and weapons found; some loadouts are weapon dependent.
  • Perfect rewards for new and endgame players; rewards not worth "the grind" for mid-game players.

All Seasons[]

Loadouts

Most Essential And Viable Core Heroes Commanders for All Seasons:

Commando Spitfire B.A.S.E. Kyle Dragon Scorch Trailblazer Quinn
Sergeant Jonesy Power B.A.S.E. Knox Stonefoot Crash Enforcer Grizzly
Resources
  1. Play with a team to maximize build efficiency, resource management and reduce playtime.
  2. Ability-based Heroes for Goin' Commando, T.E.D.D.Y., Dragon Slash, etc are the most time/cost efficient; Do not rely on weapons or weapon-based Heroes. In Ventures, players are restricted from accessing their supercharged 144 schematics, must scavenge whatever they find in vending machines or storm chests.
  3. Venture Levels 1-15 are the easiest, with plenty of random lobby players, and are best spent utilizing a dedicated Farming Loadout to build a sizable "warchest" that will last to Levels 50+. Complete Target Practice, Storm Chests, Anomalies, rescue Survivors, and build Radar Towers.
    • Afterwards, farming Storm Chests, once they are made available, is sufficient to provide all material needs.
Missions
  1. Complete Resupply first! Every new season is a blank slate with zero resources.
  2. Do the highest level Fight the Storm second.
  3. Ride The Lightning and Retrieve the Data third.
Quests
  1. Weapon/pickaxe eliminations: Pro tip, just smack or shoot the husk once with whatever weapon the challenge wants. Then use an ability finish them off; you still get full credit!
  2. Xenon Bow's projectile travels through the entire map, damaging everything in its path, essentially destroying smaller objects. This is especially helpful for Dennis' Destroy Venture Quests.
  3. Flingers are the hardest mist monsters to find, the best way to find them is to hop on missions where you are defending a base, they don't spawn in non-objective missions like Resupply or Eliminate and Collect, this quest takes time and luck.
  4. Survivors that spawn on RVs or cars have to survive for 45 seconds before you can interact with them. Normally, Husk can't reach the survivor, so they just attack the vehicle until it's destroyed. But here's the trick. If you give the Husk a path to the survivor without destroying the vehicle, the survivor becomes completely immune to damage. There's actually another way to make these survivors immortal, using the Xenon Bow. The trick is to aim right at their feet and tap fired, meaning don't charge the shot at all. That tiny nudge will knock them safely off the vehicle. And once they're on the ground, the husks now have a path to them. But since the vehicle isn't destroyed, the survivor becomes completely immune to the husk's damage.

Flannel Falls[]

KILL IT WITH FIRE!

  • Chrome Huskies are vulnerable to fire and water damage.
Quests
  • Staircase: On city biome maps; Look for New York style fire escapes on brick apartment buildings.
  • Chandeliers: On city biome maps; Find the hotel building that looks like a staircase; this will complete this quest in a single match. If you can't find this building, the black church building has 2.

Hexsylvania[]

Loadouts

Most Essential And Viable Fortnitemares Heroes Commanders and Support for Hexsylvania:

Swamp Knight Arrlene Izza Sarah Hotep Cassie "Clip" Lipman
Chaos Agent Brainstorm Dire Beetlejess
Raven Brainiac Jonesy Sentry Gunner Airheart Skull Ranger Ramirez


Resources
  • Melee Weapons and Abilities are powerful this season; enemies receive double damage from all sources whenever they are within 2-tiles of a Defender or Player character. Conversely, enemies receive half damage farther than 2 tiles.
  • Fibrous Herbs: In Swamp biomes, breaking the strawmen located at the marketplace stall will drop up to 32 Fibrous Herbs which can be used to craft Traps.
Missions
  • Deliver the Bomb does not work in Hexsylvania; there is a very rare chance it can appear in Hexsylvania, if it does, just ignore it.
  • Trap the Storm has a mission ending bug if you place the trap down too fast. When you run that mission, wait like 10 seconds after locating the Trap before actually placing it down
    • Also if you for some reason can't get the Trap to activate, try doing the Mansion (gold roof building) first, then the Castle
Quests
  • Complete Clip's Nightmare ASAP; the questline remains for a short amount of time.
  • Catacombs are underground Bunkers, and thus, extremely well hidden; they are frequently located in the basements of Castles, Mansions, Towers, Keeps, Mausoleums, Cemeteries, Crypts, etc. Every Hexsylvania biome has 2-5 dungeons, with Dark Forest and The Swamps having the highest consistency.

Resources
