Overview

This article is about the Missions in Save the World. For the Challenge bundles in Battle Royale of the same name, see Missions (Battle Royale).

Missions are modes within Fortnite: Save the World that require players to complete a certain goal in order to be successfully completed. They typically include time limits, either for defending specific objectives, or for completing an objective or a set of objectives within a certain time period.

Side Objectives

Side Objectives[]

Side Objectives are small goals within a Mission. They don't end the mission and offer various rewards.

Build the Radar Tower - Follow the holographic plans to build and activate a radar tower.
Retrieve the Anomaly Shards - Destroy the anomaly and retrieve 3-4 shards around it. Rewards 4x BluGlo.
Eliminate all Encampment enemies - Clear the Encampment by eliminating all enemies. Varied rewards.
Storm Chest - A Group Activity requiring you to eliminate Husks and Mist Monsters within an allotted time. Rewards Weapons, Traps, and Materials if at least 25 enemies were eliminated. Bonus rewards if a total of 50 enemies were eliminated.
Target Practice - Practice your aiming skills by shooting discs from a launcher with a ranged weapon. Upon shooting 10, you will receive a reward containing Weapons, Traps, and Materials. Shoot a bonus of 10 more discs (20 total) to earn more rewards.
Whack-a-Troll - A whack-a-troll style mini-game where you hit a Troll with your weapon or pickaxe multiple times. Rewards 2 BluGlo and some Building Materials.
Salvage the Drone - A Group Activity that occurs randomly during any mission. Head to the Crash Site objective and protect the crashed drone as it digs for crafting materials and resources. Can only occur if 2 or more players are present.
Mission Alerts

Mission Alerts[]

Mission Alerts appear on the Map as Missions with little clocks on them. Missions in these zones grant additional one-time rewards including Evolution Materials, Experience, Schematics, Heroes, Crafting Ingredients, and sometimes V-Bucks. Most Mission Alerts will also have Modifiers on them that affect how the mission is played.
