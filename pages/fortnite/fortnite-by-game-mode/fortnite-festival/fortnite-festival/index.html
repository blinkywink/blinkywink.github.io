Fortnite Festival is a rhythm game developed by Harmonix that was released within Fortnite on December 9th 2023. It consists of two playlists: the Main Stage[2] and the Jam Stage[3].

Main Stage

Main Stage[]

On the Main Stage, play Jam Tracks from either the rotating selection or your own collection. Compete against friends for the best performance or team up to climb the leaderboards.

Before joining the Main Stage, you will come across three different pre-made setlists: Daily Vibes, Spotlight Setlist, and Festival Selects. You can also choose to build your own setlist.

Backstage

Upon joining the Main Stage, you will find yourself Backstage, where you can choose any featured, new, default, or owned Jam Tracks. Each person in the lobby can pick 1-4 Jam Tracks, depending on the amount of players. You may also select your difficulty and instrument backstage. The terminal at the center of Backstage has a screen that will show the selected setlist and the amount of notes those tracks will have with your selected instrument. Once you are ready to start, you can ready up, and you will be rifted onstage.

On PlayStation and Xbox platforms, up to 4 players can join on the same console for local multiplayer, with each player receiving their own note highway on the screen. Each player must be signed in with their own Epic Games account (which must allow ESRB T-rated experiences) and platform account.

Going Pro

Each instrument has a "Pro" variant designed to make gameplay feel like playing a real instrument.

  • Pro Lead and Pro Bass: Players can use a compatible guitar controller from Guitar Hero and Rock Band, which features gameplay elements such as strumming and "hop-on/hop-off" notes.
  • Pro Drums: Compatible drum kits or MIDI sets such as the Novation Launchpad can be used, featuring dedicated inputs for snares, toms, and bass kicks; dedicated inputs for Cymbals can be enabled in Settings, turning it into Pro Cymbals.
  • Karaoke: The microphone is utilized to enable players to sing along, with performance graded on pitch and intonation accuracy. Instrumentals will not have charts for Karaoke.

To be able to select Pro instruments, the "Always Display Pro Parts" must be enabled in Settings. Currently, Pro Drums and Karaoke is only available for PC and Console.

Scoring

Each note you play will contribute to your personal score and shared band score. The closer you are to the timing of a note, the more score you will get from that note. Doing better on a song will grant up to 5 stars, with golden stars being given out to those on expert difficulty. There are two types of stars during each track: personal stars and band stars. Personal Stars are given based on your performance. Band Stars are given out based on the performance of everybody onstage.

Auras will glow around you when you are on a note streak on the Main Stage.

You will fill your overdrive meter by playing strings of glowing notes without missing any. Each string you complete will fill your overdrive meter 1/4 of the way. Using overdrive will double your personal score multiplier, and add a 2x multiplier to band score for each person using overdrive at once. Overdrive will last a short duration after being used. Overdrive can be lengthened by playing strings of glowing notes while being active. On Pro Drums/Cymbals, Overdrive will automatically be activated by hitting a designated note at certain points during the song; not playing it will not count as a miss.

On Karaoke, performance is graded on pitch accuracy. Singing correctly will build a meter, which will add to the player's score after a certain amount of beats. Failing to adequately build a meter will still contribute to the score but the score multiplier will also be reset, akin to a miss.

Missing or misplaying notes will not deduct score, but you will not receive any score for it. A misplayed note is known as a strike. Missing or striking will reset your personal score multiplier and/or cancel any current overdrive string.

Special Notes
  • Tap Vocals / Lead / Bass / Drums
    • Sustain notes: Needs to be held to receive the full point value. Releasing the note early will not count as a miss, but pressing the button again will enact a strike.
    • Lift notes: Appears as an arrow after a note intended to be played by letting go of the button. Lifts can also be played like normal notes.
  • Pro Lead / Pro Bass
    • Strum notes: Appears as a regular-sized note on regular instruments, and these have to be played with the strum bar.
    • Hammer-on/Pull-off notes, or HOPOs: Only requires the fret lane to be tapped as long as there's a note streak active. If there is none, HOPOs can be strummed to start a note streak.
    • Sustain notes: Needs to be held to receive the full point value. In addition, the whammy bar can be used to alter the note's pitch, although it has no effect on scoring.
  • Pro Drums / Pro Cymbals
    • Bass Kicks: Shown as an orange bar that spans the width of the entire note highway, which can sometimes be grouped with standard notes and cymbal notes, requiring them to be hit simultaneously.
    • Overdrive Activator: A green note or cymbal (if Cymbals are enabled) that activates Overdrive. Not playing it will not count as a miss, and the player will be visually informed if one is coming up.
    • Lanes: A highlighted rapid succession of notes on a single fret lane. These notes have a more lenient hit detection window. An example of a Lane can be found in "Stellar Stellar" by Hoshimachi Suisei.
  • Karaoke
    • Pitch notes: Represented as blue lines on the highway and are used as a reference for the player to adjust the pitch of their voice to the song.
    • Talk notes: Represented as blank spaces, meaning pitch will not matter as long as you are singing.
Post-Stage

After playing a song, you can see your and your band's scoring. If there is another song, you will be able to change your difficulty and instrument. If there is not another song, you will be shown your challenges and your current amount of XP.

Jam Stage

Jam Stage[]

On the Jam Stage, up to 4 people can band together and create a mix of songs. In your emote wheel, you will find a "Jam Tracks" category. In there, you will find your equipped Jam Tracks. You can choose which instrument to play in the emote wheel. Once in a "Jam," you can adjust the key, tempo, and if the jam is in major or minor. You can Emote while jamming; Emotes that are Beat Synced will automatically adjust their speed to the tempo of the jam. The Jam Stage is intended to mash multiple songs together to make a new song.

Players can also start a Jam by themselves. In a solo Jam Circle, three additional plots will appear in front of the player, and they can assign a Jam Loop to one of these plots.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • The codename for this game mode is "Sparks".
  • Fortnite Festival was first teased during The Big Bang live event.
  • Fortnite Festival was the last of the modes to release after the launch of Chapter 5, following LEGO Fortnite and Rocket Racing.
  • As of October 5th 2025, Fortnite Festival is Harmonix's only available title, after Rock Band 4 (2015) and its DLC were delisted from digital storefronts on the game's tenth anniversary due to expiring music licenses.
  • Similar to prior Guitar Hero and Rock Band titles, specialized guitar controllers were produced for Fortnite Festival, which were the PDP Riffmaster and the CRKD x Gibson Les Paul controller.
  • Prior to their release in Update v40.20, data for Pro Drums and Karaoke (then known as "Pro Vocals") could be found within the files for Jam Tracks.
    • Pro Drums charts were first uncovered in mid-2024, while Karaoke charts were first uncovered in April 2025.
    • Prior to an official release, all new Jam Track additions had Pro Drums and Karaoke charts (the latter being excluded for instrumental Jam Tracks).
    • Existing Jam Tracks were periodically updated with data for Karaoke, with the final Jam Track (Ride Wit Me) receiving Karaoke support on March 9th 2026.
    • After most of Fortnite Festival's quality assurance team were laid off in March 2026 as part of the mass layoffs affecting Epic Games, Harmonix developers filled in for the lost roles to ensure Pro Drums and Karaoke were able to be released on time alongside Festival's fourteenth season.
  • Some UI concepts that were released in 2024 include songs and albums that are either currently unreleased, scrapped, or had slight alterations for Fortnite Festival.[4] These include:
    • WANNABE by ITZY.
    • The Ballad Of Mona Lisa by Panic! At The Disco.
    • DUU-DU DDUI-DU by BLACKPINK.
    • Shut Down by BLACKPINK.
    • THE PLANET by BTS.
    • Leona by Millencolin.
    • The Number Of The Beast by Iron Maiden.
    • Pink Venom by BLACKPINK.
    • Smells Like Teen Spirit by Nirvana (using the original album art from Nevermind).
    • Industry Baby by Lil Nas X (albeit without album art or Jack Harlow's credit).
    • The album art for Off The Edge of The Earth and Into Forever, Forever by Art vs Science.
    • The album art for DAMN. by Kendrick Lamar.
    • The album art for Flower Boy by Tyler, The Creator.
    • The album art for Pretty. Odd. by Panic! At The Disco.
    • The album art for London Calling by The Clash.
    • The single cover for ENTWURF by NMIXX.
    • The album art for The Black Parade by My Chemical Romance.
    • The single cover for Song 2 by Blur (compared to the in-game version using the art for Blur: The Best Of).
    • The album art for The Now Now by Gorillaz.
    • The single cover for Cracker Island by Gorillaz ft. Thundercat.
    • The album art for Leisure by Blur.
