Overview

“
Welcome to Party Royale, a new experimental and envolving space. Leave your weapons and mats behind and hang out with friends, play games and enjoy live entertainment. Catch live shows. Race around obstacle courses by land or sea. Go fishing with friends, perfect your skydive, and get up close and connect with some of your favorite artists. Hop on the party bus and enjoy the ride.
”
— In-Game Description

Party Royale is a Core Game Mode in Fortnite that was added in Update v12.50. It is the first casual game mode to not contain PvP or building as a whole, instead featuring various mini-games for players to play with their team.

Map

Map[]

Party Royale's map (codenamed Papaya) contains 3 named locations:

  • The Main Stage, The Plaza, and The Big Screen. The Main Stage originally played an assortment of Epic-Original music whilst the screen displayed art from Loading Screens, however it was disabled in Update v22.40.
  • The Plaza contains Vending Machines and Changing Booths that players can interact with inside of stores and buildings.
  • The Big Screen was used for a few concerts, but otherwise remained mostly functionless.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • The naming of the Party Royale island as "Papaya" was the first to break the trend of islands being named after Greek gods.
  • Party Royale is the first Social Game Mode, with no objective to eliminate other players.
  • Party Royale used the pre-Chapter 5 movement animations until Chapter 6: Season 1. It still uses the pre-Chapter 4 graphics.
  • Originally in the Party Royale trailer, Kirby was visible on The Big Screen. This was later blurred out. The original trailer with Kirby is still available on IGN's YouTube channel.[1]
