LTM[]

This article is about a Mode available for a limited time only.

“

Horde Rush is a Limited Time Mode in Fortnite: Battle Royale that was first introduced in Season 9.

Since Chapter 2: Season 8, Horde Rush was brought back annually as part of Fortnitemares, with the loot pool being adjusted to be identical to the loot pool in Battle Royale. Horde Rush was rebranded as Demon Rush during Chapter 6: Season 4 and was themed around KPop Demon Hunters.

LTM Rules and Gameplay

LTM Rules and Gameplay[]

  • Demon Rush
  • Horde Rush (C2S8 - C5S4)

Demon Rush is a cooperative player vs. environment gamemode where players must fight through hordes of Demons and Ogres in the confines of the Storm. Players must complete five Encounters, with the Caretaker and The Final Peel awaiting them. Should they complete the ordeal, they can engage in an endless score attack mode.

General

  • Monsters drop loot upon elimination, most commonly Ammunition or Sideways Monster Parts.
  • Rarer and tougher Monsters also have the chance of dropping Weapons and Consumables.
  • Players gain points from opening Chests and Ammo Boxes, eliminating Monsters, destroying Spawn Obelisks, and collecting Score Multiplier Crystals for the team.
  • Players also have an individual score multiplier for points gained by eliminating Monsters in a certain period of time, stacking up to a 4x multiplier (KO Streak).
  • Eliminating Monsters at this point simply refreshes the timer.
  • These points do not grant any benefit to the player, other than showing on a leaderboard below the minimap, and at the end of the game.
  • If eliminated at any point, players will enter Spectator Mode until the next round begins, when they respawn with their inventory intact.
  • If all players are eliminated during a round, the game ends, leading to a Defeat.

Gameplay

Prepare for Battle (2m 30s)

  • The Battle Bus goes over a small area in the middle of the Storm.
  • They must land and gather loot by searching Chests and Ammo Boxes.
  • Players have 150 seconds to loot the area, and prepare for an attack as the Storm closes in.

Survive! (3m)

  • Sideways Monster Fiends, Ranged Fiends and Brutes will spawn from Obelisks.
  • Players complete the round by surviving for three minutes.
  • Score Multipliers occasionally spawn within the zone, interacting with which causes the team's score multiplier to increase.
    • Upon claiming one, all nearby Monsters will despawn as well.
  • When the round is complete, all Monsters, Spawners and unclaimed Score Multipliers will despawn. Players who have died during any wave will also respawn above once it ends.
  • During Round 2, a Boss has a chance to appear. Being either Cube Assassin, Revenant Kado Thorne, Cerberus, or Ultima Carver.

Follow the Storm (3m)

  • When the Storm finishes shrinking, players must follow it to a new location, fighting randomly spawned monsters along the way.
  • Loot quality is increased, as is Sideways Monster diversity - Poison Fiends, Ice Fiends, and Exploding Brutes are now added to the mix, as well as all Monsters and Obelisks spawning more often moving forward.
  • At the end of the round, either Rumi, Mira, or Zoey have a chance to appear as free hireable Characters that will fight with their respective Pickaxes.

Round Modifiers

  • During rounds in Demon Rush, a round modifier may be imposed that alters the conditions of the gameplay:
    • Ranged Attackers! - Only Demons that attack with projectiles will spawn.
    • Small Problem! - Only Small Fiends will spawn during the round.
    • Speed Walk! - Sprinting is disabled, but everyone's walk speed is increased.
    • Nothing Can Stop Us Now! - Players gain a pseudo-Nitro effect: they will damage and knock back enemies they collide into with the addition of a Slap effect but do not damage any structures.
    • For the Fans! - Players and enemies gain a surge of speed periodically.
    • Building Not Permitted! - Building is disabled for the duration of the round.
    • Light Headed! - Players have low gravity, increased sprint stamina use and reduced movement speed.
    • Gold Rush! - Eliminating enemies grants the Gold Rush effect.
    • Up Close and Personal! - Players do increased melee damage, but decreased ranged damage.
    • Impactful Headshots! Enemies have more health, but headshots deal more damage!
    • Dance Party! - Players and enemies randomly start dancing!
    • Feeling Ice! - Ranged Fiends will only be ice. (No normal Ranged Fiends will spawn this round.)
    • Hyper Mode! - Enemies move faster, hit harder, but are defeated easier.

Boss Battles

Caretaker (Round 3)

  • Monsters spawn again from Obelisks, along with the Caretaker Boss.
  • Players complete the round by eliminating the Boss.
  • Storm damage is increased to 5 HP per tick, and closes in fully in 3 minutes.

The Final Peel (Round 5)

  • Monsters spawn again from Obelisks, along with The Final Peel Final Boss.
  • Players complete the round (and clear the gamemode) by eliminating the Boss.
  • The Storm will not close, but its damage per tick is massively increased.

Score Attack

  • In Demon Rush, defeating The Final Peel in Round 5 will initiate an endless score-attack mode where players fight increasingly powerful hordes of enemies until every player is eliminated.
  • The Storm will close slowly during Endless, and the damage per tick can rise as high as 65 DPS by Round 15.
  • Bosses will appear as the rounds progress, with the same selection as Round 2 along with the chance of The Final Peel.

Horde Rush is a cooperative gamemode, similar to the Destroy the Encampments Mission in Fortnite: Save the World. Players must work together to eliminate Sideways Monsters, and complete three Encounters. Players win the mode by eliminating the Boss, The Caretaker.

General

  • Monsters drop loot upon elimination, most commonly Ammunition or Sideways Monster Parts.
  • Rarer and tougher Monsters also have the chance of dropping Weapons and Consumables.
  • Players gain points from opening Chests and Ammo Boxes, eliminating Monsters, destroying Spawn Obelisks, and collecting Score Multiplier Crystals for the team.
  • Players also have an individual score multiplier for points gained by eliminating Monsters in a certain period of time, stacking up to a 4x multiplier (KO Streak).
  • Eliminating Monsters at this point simply refreshes the timer.
  • These points do not grant any benefit to the player, other than showing on a leaderboard below the minimap, and at the end of the game.
  • If eliminated at any point, players will enter Spectator Mode until the next round begins, when they respawn with their inventory intact.
  • If all players are eliminated during a round, the game ends, leading to a Defeat.

Players fight in three major encounters:

Round 1

Prepare for Battle (2m 30s)

  • The Battle Bus goes over a small area in the middle of the Storm.
  • They must land and gather loot by searching Chests and Ammo Boxes.
  • Players have 150 seconds to loot the area, and prepare for an attack as the Storm closes in.

Survive! (3m)

  • Sideways Monster Fiends, Ranged Fiends and Brutes will spawn from Obelisks.
  • Players complete the round by surviving for three minutes.
  • Score Multipliers occassionally spawn within the zone, interacting with which causes the team's score multiplier to increase.
    • Upon claiming one, all nearby Monsters will despawn as well.
  • When the round is complete, all Monsters, Spawners and unclaimed Score Multipliers will despawn. Players who have died during any wave will also respawn above once it ends.

Heal and Gear Up (1m)

  • After the first round has passed, players are given a minute to regroup as The Storm shrinks down to a smaller area before it begins to travel.
  • The Storm currently deals 1 HP of damage per tick.

Follow the Storm (3m)

  • When the Storm finishes shrinking, players must follow it to a new location, fighting randomly spawned monsters along the way.
  • Loot quality is increased, as is Sideways Monster diversity - Poison Fiends, Ice Fiends, and Exploding Brutes are now added to the mix, as well as all Monsters and Obelisks spawning more often moving foward.

Round 2

Prepare for Battle (1m)

  • When the Storm stops moving, the players will be at another named location on the map. They are given a minute to loot the area as the Storm grows back to its previous "play area" size to allow players to explore.

Survive! (3m)

  • Sideways Monsters, Obelisks, and Score Multipliers will once again spawn, along with Cube Assassin, who wields a Sideways Minigun.
  • She has high accuracy, and eliminating her is optional.
  • She drops an Epic, Legendary or Mythic weapon upon elimination along with Items and Bars.

Heal and Gear Up (1m)

  • Players are given a minute to regroup as The Storm shrinks down to a smaller area once again.
  • The Storm currently deals 2HP of damage per tick.

Follow the Storm (3m)

  • Players must then follow the Storm again to a new location, fighting monsters all the way.
  • Loot quality is increased once more, as is the Monster spawn rate, as well as the new chance of Mega Brutes spawning, much stronger versions of Brutes with better loot.

Round 3

Prepare for Battle (1m)

  • Players have another 60 seconds to loot the area and get into position before the final battle.

Boss Fight

  • Monsters spawn again from Obelisks, along with the Caretaker Boss.
  • Players complete the round by eliminating the Boss.
  • Storm damage is increased to 5 HP per tick, and closes in fully in 3 minutes.

Hunter Rank

Hunter Rank[]

In Demon Rush, the Hunter Rank is a progression system that rewards players with benefits for concurrent and future playthroughs of the mode. Players improve their Hunter Rank by defeating Demons.

Season 9[]

Update v9.21[]

  • Introduced Horde Rush.

Chapter 2: Season 8[]

Update v18.20[]

  • Horde Rush returns.
    • Several changes to the loot pool were made.
    • All Cube Monsters and Bosses were replaced by Sideways Monsters and Bosses.
    • The last encounter now features a Final Boss, The Caretaker.

Update v18.20 Hotfix[]

  • Removed Combat SMG form the loot pool.

Chapter 3: Season 3[]

Update v21.20[]

  • Horde Rush returns, despite not currently being available on Discover.
    • Players who favourited Horde Rush while it was previously available can access the mode through the Favorited tab on their Library despite it not being officially available.
    • Rebalanced the loot pool, removing all Cube Monster Parts, Sideways Weaponry and Chapter 2 base weaponry.

Chapter 3: Season 4[]

Update v22.20[]

  • Horde Rush returns now in Zero Build.
    • All changes made during Chapter 3: Season 3 were reverted.
  • October 20th 2022: Increased the Shield regenerated from each Cube Monster elimination.
    • Increased the time before the Final Boss's Storm circle closes.
  • October 22nd 2022: The Sideways Scythe now restores Health with eliminations.
    • Storm damage during the second phase has been reduced.
    • Several weapons have had their drop rate adjusted.

Chapter 4: Season 4[]

Update v26.00[]

  • Horde Rush returns, despite not currently being available on Discover.
    • Players who favourited Horde Rush Zero Build while it was previously available can access the mode through the Favorited tab on their Library despite it not being officially available.
    • Multiple changes to the loot pool were made, including the addition of the currently unreleased Sticky Grenade Launcher.
    • Upgrade Benches have been added to each safe zone, with upgrades costing Cube Monster Parts instead of Bars.
    • Golden Brutes and Cube Assassin now drops Mythic weapons upon elimination.
    • Augments are enabled with a separate loot pool from Battle Royale.
    • Beating the Caretaker at the end will give players a Legacy for a Squads win.
  • October 10th 2023: Horde Rush officially returns with the start of Fortnitemares 2023.

Chapter 5: Season 4[]

Update v31.40[]

  • Horde Rush returns.
    • Several changes to the loot pool were made.
    • Golden Brutes and Cube Assassin still drop Mythic weapons, with the addition of Powers.
    • Reduced the Cube Assassin's spawn time from 15 seconds after the round starts to 6.
    • XP gain has been enabled, and Accolades can now be earned.
    • New sounds have been added to the break countdown timer, and monster spawner destructions.
    • The Upgrade Benches in stationary rounds have been replaced by Monster Part Mod Benches, meaning that weapons can no longer be upgraded but can be modded in exchange for Cube Monster Parts .

Chapter 6: Season 4[]

Update v37.40[]

  • Horde Rush returns as Demon Rush, themed around KPop Demon Hunters.
    • The mode features music from KPop Demon Hunters as well as themed weaponry based on the film's characters. Music includes:
      • Golden - HUNTR/X
      • Takedown - HUNTR/X
      • How It's Done - HUNTR/X
      • Your Idol - Saja Boys
    • A dance stage players can emote on to restore health and shield quickly is in the center of the safe zone during stationary rounds
    • Upgrade Benches that cost monster parts to upgrade weapons are back, replacing the Mod Benches that were present in the mode in Chapter 5.
    • Monster Part Vending Machines, Rare Monster Part Vending Machines (which contain higher rarity weapons) and KPop Demon Hunters Vending Machines (which contain the 4 pieces of KPop Demon Hunters gear) which all cost monster parts also appear in the storm safe zone.
    • A level-up system was introduced. Leveling up will unlock permanent perks for current and future playthroughs of Demon Rush.
    • Augments are enabled, this time as Perks (Demon Rush). Players can drop these perks from their inventory to share with others unlike boons or augments.
    • The health of the Caretaker and The Final Peel bosses scale with the number of players in the match at the start of it. This means the bosses will have less health in matches with only 1 player in them compared to matches with 4 players.
    • The final boss is The Final Peel, a reskin of Shogun X. He is fought in Round 5.
      • Defeating The Final Peel will lead into an endless score attack mode with increasingly powerful enemies, including appearances from past bosses from Battle Royale.
    • Score multipliers are automatically collected after the end of every survival round, which is different from all previous years.
