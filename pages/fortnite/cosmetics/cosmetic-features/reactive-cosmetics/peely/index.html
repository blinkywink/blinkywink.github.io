Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Peely was created by Donald Mustard to troll his brother Chad (Mustard Plays), who hates bananas since childhood.[1]
    • He was originally going to be a man in a banana suit. Given how old some survey Outfits actually are, it's possible that Jonesy in a banana suit from the May 14th, 2021 lineup is Peely's original design.
  • Peely has a duplicate TBD Outfit added in Update v12.00. His purpose is unknown.

Peely is a young Banana from the Peely tribe, one of Jones' best friends and a "legendary" figure of the island, serving as one of the key members of The Resistance and later becoming the superhero who goes by the name "Peelverine" of The Underground.

Personality

Personality[]

Peely is a quiet banana, initially believed to have minimal intelligence, he was revealed to be more intelligent and thoughtful than he usually poses, when the situation actually really requires it. Most of the time he acts like an idiot and and loves putting himself in all kinds of dangerous situations from jumping into an active Volcano, goofing around in The Loop, driving truck through a literal warzone or just getting kidnap into a hostage situation. Peely loves being the center of attention.

Peely also seems to model his personality on others; when becoming Peelverine, he completely copied Wolverine's personality, even taking on his speech quirks and violence.

Appearance

Appearance[]

Peely is literally a banana with human arms and human legs. It would seem that inside his banana peel is just a normal banana, meaning that he might be edible and has been turned into a smoothie. However, the existence of the Peely Bone Outfit calls his physiology into question. Upon inspection, it seems that Peely possesses an entire skeletal structure and intestines. Other organs are presumably omitted due to concerns with gore. While it seems that Peely does possess banana matter within his body, it is more or less contained within his ribcage and pelvis (presumably it makes up the majority of his limbs) rather than being what Peely's body is solely comprised of. It is unknown, however, if the Peely Bone Outfit actually says anything about Peely or whether it is its own kind of species.

Timeline

Timeline[]

Pre-Season 8[]

A young banana is born into a species of humanoid bananas.

Season 8[]

After Agent Jones, sent for a mission in The Loop, first encountered Peely at Lucky Landing's Bao Bros, the two became friend and started hanging out together. After some time, they found themselve trapped in the bunker to escape the Volcano eruption. However, they found themselves without food, and Jones had no choice but to devour Peely to survive.

Season 9[]

After being trapped for decades, if not centuries, Jones, who turned into Bunker Jonesy and Peely, who became a smoothie were freed by Sentinel, Vega and Rox and discovered Neo Tilted. However, during their imprisonment in the Bunker, the act of devouring Peely allowed Bunker Jonesy to see visions of both the past and the future. In one of these visions, he saw a fight between a Kaiju and a Mecha that would happen in the near future and tried to warn the inhabitants of The Island. At some point, Jones would escape The Loop, leaving behind the Bunker Jonesy snapshot.

Season X[]

The Zero Point's reality waves revert his smoothie form back to full banana.

Chapter 2: Season 2[]

He became Agent Peely and joined GHOST.

Chapter 2: Season 4[]

Peely pulls out claws made out of bananas in an attempt to fight Wolverine.

Chapter 2: Season 5[]

The Mandalorian carbon-froze him and kept him in Hunter's Haven.

Chapter 2: Season 6[]

Agent Jones came across Peely in the middle of all chaos in The Loop and they were happy to see each other again. However, Ryu obliterates Peely with a Hadouken in front of Jones.

Chapter 2: Season 7[]

He was interviewed by a news reporter on the Abduction events, masking his identity as John Doe.

Chapter 3: Season 1[]

The Scientist had suspicions of "Banana Loopers" vandalizing The Foundation's statue, who are possibly related to Peely or other members of his species.

Chapter 3: Season 2[]

Peely joins The Seven resistance against the IO. He asks The Origin to get Jones to teach him how to drive so he can help the resistance. After the reclaiming of The Daily Bugle, he drove an ice cream truck and failed to steer, crashing the truck near the shore in Sleepy Sound. The Origin mentioned that he is not fit to be a covert operative, leading Peely to be reassigned to his previous mission, learning to drive again. In the Collision live event. Peely helps Jones and The Seven by delivering a Slurp Truck to heal the critically damaged mech.

Chapter 3: Season 3[]

Peely goes to the beach as Unpeely to join the No Sweat Summer.

Chapter 4: Season 2[]

Peely is listed as a potential recruit in the Peace Syndicate's members info files.

Chapter 4: Season 4[]

Agent Peely goes for a confidential mission in Whispering Waters and refuses to join Doctor Slone's heist crew. He later turns into Peely Bone during Fortnitemares 2023.

Chapter 4: Season OG[]

During The Big Bang event, a missing poster featuring Peely can be seen floating in space.

Chapter 5: Season 1[]

Peely is kidnapped by The Society, in order to get Jones to deliver information about what Valeria is looking for, however not only did Jones not know anything about Pandora's Box, but he also goes on a personal war against The Society. Throughout the season, Jones has The Looper hang up Missing posters and place radars in an attempt to locate Peely. Over the course of the Takedown Quests, Jones, Hope, and The Looper work together to Eliminate Legion Guards and Elites, Steal Society Medallions, and defeat the Bosses of the Society, which culminate in Jones finally Rescuing Peely from Fencing Fields.

Chapter 5: Season 2[]

Peely received one of the edicts of Zeus which was intended for another God. They gave it to The Looper. Later, The Oracle reveals that Peely is the lynchpin holding together all of reality.

Chapter 5: Season 4[]

To fight Doom's invasion on the island, Peely takes on the role of Peelverine, accompanied by his best friend Captain Jones and Hope, also collaborating with other heroes from Reality 616. Transformed into Peelverine, with the Nanamantium in his body. Peely also receives a change in mood, and can now he can speak.

During Absolute Doom Event, Peely was seen fighting Doom alongside his Underground friends.

Chapter 6[]

Peely followed Jones, Hope and The Looper, being likely inside the Battle Bus that followed them to Reality: 783. He is seen on several occasions hanging out with Jones, and sometimes involved in conflicts such as those against the Galactic Empire, the establishment of the Supernova Academy, the battle against the Onimorphs, and the emergence of the Dark Presence. He was also one of the Loopers who rifted to Springfield Island, collaborating with Jones, Hope, and the Looper to retrieve the Zero Shard, before returning to fight the Dark Presence in Oninoshima.

Chapter 7: Season 1[]

Following again Jones and co in a new Reality, Peely was chosen by the film director Kingston to star in his movie: Peelian. A movie based on banana alien invaders. Although some of the staff such as Kingston or Carter Wu were worried about him, they hoped he could become a star. Once filming was complete, however, the offers dried up and Peely became homeless, relocating under a bridge on Battlewood Boulevard. He later found work in Latte Landing's Big Shot.

Chapter 7: Season 2[]

When the Ice King arrived, Latte Landing was taken into the Ice Kingdom's territory, and Peely was captured by the Ice King in one of the Ice Pillars of the throne room. When Foundation escaped and fought the Ice King, however, one of the Ice King's shots destroyed his pillar, shattering Peely's body and leaving only his ankles and feet.

Trivia[]

  • In Chapter 2: Season 5, Peely could have been found stuck in Carbonite in the Mandalorian's apartment in Hunter's Haven.
    • Additionally, in the The Hunt Loading Screen, the Jungle Hunter is also seen hunting Peely.
  • Peely has been seen speaking once with his voice pitched down under the alias of "John Doe" to not reveal his identity in the Chapter 2: Season 7 Story Trailer, saying "Then... the rest of them showed up." John Doe is a name given to someone who is generic and unidentifiable, and so was used to hide Peely's identity. It's unknown why Peely is able to talk.
    • According to dialogue said by Rick Sanchez, Peely also knows how to create slurp.
  • Bushranger warns not to eat a banana in front of him.
  • It is possible that Peely knows The Imagined's plans, considering his special Outfit reaction when talking to a player wearing The Imagined.
  • Peely has a recorded 1,274 driving infractions and currently owes 503,245 Bars in fines.[3]
  • Peely is apparently one of the lynchpin holding together The Omniverse, according to The Oracle.
    • This concept seem similar to MCU's Anchor Being concept, in which each Reality has a being of such great importance that once this being die or is removed from that reality, their reality begins to degrade until complete annihilation.
      • However, in Peely's case, this seems to apply to the entire Omniverse.
