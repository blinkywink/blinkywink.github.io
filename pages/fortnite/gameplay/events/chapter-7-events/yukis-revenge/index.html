The Lost Chapter: Yuki's Revenge - Fortnite After Dark was a Live Event in Fortnite: Battle Royale that took place on November 30th 2025 at 2:00 PM EST, with an encore showing at 8:00 PM EST.[1]

Plot

Plot[]

Bill meets with Yuki Yubari after her twin sister Gogo was killed in a duel with the Bride. Knowing that the Bride would target Vernita Green in Pasadena, he sends Yuki to Los Angeles, California to hunt the Bride down and avenge Gogo's death. Touching down in the States, Yuki purchases a bright pink Chevrolet Corvette Stingray and uses it to tour the city, visiting a number of attractions and taking pictures with the locals there. She encounters the Bride at the airport and begins to stalk her, following her to a motel by dusk before confronting her.

The Bride immediately recognizes Yuki's voice and tries to ward her away from vengeance, to no avail as Yuki starts approaching her with a concealed firearm. The Bride reaches for her own pistol but is blasted by a hail of bullets from Yuki's machine gun, forcing her to run for cover. Yuki uses grenades to flush her out into the open and chase her down in her pink car. Peely runs out of his home with a shotgun in hand, but the Bride orders him back inside.

The Bride barges into an occupied home for cover. She kicks one of Yuki's grenades back at her to total her car, before luring her upstairs where she shoots her in the chest. Hearing police sirens, the Bride taunts Yuki to kill her quickly before authorities arrive to the scene, prompting Yuki to take an overdose of Bill's blue concoction and pop out. Yuki is quickly shot down, but she pulls out a knife and lunges at the Bride in a last-ditch attempt to kill her. The Bride runs out of bullets and is nearly overpowered by Yuki, but Yuki suffers cardiac arrest and succumbs to her injuries. Injured and exasperated, the Bride lies down on the stairs to catch her breath.

Trivia

Trivia[]

  • Yuki's Revenge was originally intended to be part of Kill Bill: Volume 1 (2003), but was left out of the final script due to budget and runtime concerns.[2][3] It would've been the second part of Chapter 5 in the film.
  • Yuki's Revenge was included as a post-credits scene for Kill Bill: The Whole Bloody Affair (2025), the theatrical re-release of Kill Bill: Volume 1 (2003) and Kill Bill: Volume 2 (2004) as a combined picture.
  • In order to maintain the violent nature of the Kill Bill films within the constraints of Fortnite's teen rating, blood and gore are substituted with Rezz Energy, while death is represented by a Spawn Drone.
    • The baby blue powder drug used by Yuki in the original Yuki's Revenge script is represented by Small Shield Potions.
    • There are several instances of uncensored swearing. While the words that were censored, they are uncesored in the theatrical release of the short.
  • Writer-director Quentin Tarantino plays Bill, replacing original actor David Carradine, who passed away in 2009.
  • Yuki's Revenge is implied to be a movie filmed on the Golden Coast, as not only is it being shown at the Golden Reel Theater, but the filming location for the scene where Yuki crashes her car into the house can be found on Battlewood Boulevard.
    • The interior of the house does not match the one depicted in Yuki's Revenge, so the house could possibly have been used for only the external shots.
  • Following the premiere, the red carpet event could be revisited from the Discover menu or through a ticket stand inside the Golden Reel Theatre at Battlewood Boulevard.
